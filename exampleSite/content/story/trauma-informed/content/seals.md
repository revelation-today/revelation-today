Before we go further: what follows is hard, but brief, and it is not the end of the story - so let it pass through without holding onto it too tightly.

The seals break, one by one, and four riders come. The first looks like victory but is not; it is conquest wearing a false crown. Behind it comes conflict, then scarcity, then loss - one following the other, the way hard seasons sometimes do.

Then something different happens: a seal breaks open, not on armies, but on an altar. Underneath it are the people who stayed loyal to God even when it cost them everything. They are not forgotten or lost in the noise - they are gathered close, seen, and heard, crying out honestly: how long, Lord, until you set this right? They are answered gently, not dismissed: soon, just a little longer.

The sky itself trembles at what has been let loose in the world, as if creation is grieving too - and in the middle of the wreckage, God's people cry out alongside it, and are not alone in doing so.
