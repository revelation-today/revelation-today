Now the same story is told again, pulled back to reveal the frame around it - a different kind of Christmas story, played out across the sky. A woman appears, clothed with the sun, a crown of stars circling her head. She is about to give birth.

Something enormous and threatening waits in front of her, wanting to harm the child the instant he arrives. We do not need to dwell on it - only to know that it is real, and that it does not get what it wants.

The child is born safely, and before any harm can reach him, he is carried up to God's own throne, safe for good. Far above, in a place we cannot fully see, a battle is fought and settled - the threat loses its place there entirely and is thrown down, defeated already by the Lamb.

Knowing its time is short, it turns to trouble the world a little longer. But take heart: defeating it was never your job. Jesus already did that, before it ever reached you. Yours is simply to keep pointing people toward him, however loudly the noise gets for a while.
