Jesus already won this at the cross, and everyone who has stayed loyal to him shares fully in that win. Picture, instead of any battlefield, a crowd standing together on a sea of glass and fire, singing - not because the road here was easy, but because their only qualification for standing there at all was simply staying faithful through it.

Not everyone gets to sing in that crowd. Some played it safe along the way, going along with the counterfeit power in the story to avoid its cost, and now face something heavier than what they were trying to avoid.

Two harvests happen, side by side, gathered by the same hand: one brought in gladly, home at last, and one gathered for judgment. We will not linger on that second harvest - only say plainly that it happens, and that it is fair. This is simply the moment where what was quietly true all along - who was faithful, and who was not - finally stops being hidden, and everyone can see clearly at last.
