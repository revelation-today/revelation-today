Before anything gets worse, something remarkable happens, and it is worth pausing on: God's people are sealed.

Marked, one by one, by name. Not counted as a crowd and left at that, but claimed individually - each person stopped, noticed, and given a mark that says, plainly, unmistakably, this one is mine. It happens in the middle of the storm, not after it, as if God could not wait even one more moment to make sure his people knew whose they were before anything else was asked of them.

Think of what that means: nothing that comes next - no threat, no danger, nothing the world can throw - gets to decide who you belong to. That was already decided, and sealed, first. Not earned. Not conditional on how well you hold up under what is coming. Simply given, the way a parent marks a child as theirs before sending them out the door.

This is the army of God, and it looks nothing like any army you have pictured. No swords. No borrowed violence, no claiming ground by force. Just people, sealed and steady, worshiping right through whatever comes, with a loyalty that nothing seems able to wear all the way down.

So whatever the chapters ahead hold, hold onto this first: you have already been found, already been marked, already been kept safe at the center of everything. That is not the ending - it is the starting point for everything that follows.
