One last deception waits to be unmasked before the story can end - and unmasking it reveals what this was always about: not simply defeating harm, but God binding himself to his people forever, like a marriage.

Here is the deception: something dazzling, draped in jewels that should have belonged to the bride, promising good things to anyone who trusts it. Underneath, though, it is not what it seems - and plenty of people, even inside the Church, were taken in by the act.

Its ending is not gentle - it comes undone completely, torn apart by the very forces that once served it. We do not need the details; what matters is that it falls, and does not get back up.

The road is finally clear for what comes next.
