Picture an old man, alone on a rocky island in the sea, exiled there for his faith. An ordinary Sunday morning - until a voice behind him, sounding like a trumpet, changes everything.

He turns, and the vision comes all at once: seven golden lampstands burning in the air, and standing among them, someone almost impossible to describe. Eyes like fire. A voice like a waterfall. A face bright as the sun at noon. John falls down as if he had died.

But a hand touches his shoulder before the fear can even finish forming, and gentle words meet him there: "Don't be afraid. I am the First and the Last, the Living One. I died - and look, I am alive forever. I hold the keys of Death and of the Grave itself."

That is the first thing worth knowing about this whole story: the one telling it already holds every key that could ever frighten you.

Word spreads like sunrise - the kingdom you have been waiting for has already arrived, and you belong in it, a king and a priest. One thing is asked in return: that the Church shine, like those seven lampstands, not on her own fuel, but held alight by the very one standing among her.

So: is she ready?
