So how do we get ready for what is coming? Jesus says: look up.

The answer is not a strategy, a plan, or a set of defenses to build. It is a throne room. Heaven opens, and there it is - the one steady place in all creation, where everyone and everything finally finds its true and settled position before God. Whatever else is shifting in the world, this room does not shift. The door into it stands open, always, to anyone who comes.

Worship here costs something real: laying your own crown down, giving God every part of your life without holding pieces back. And in that room, one figure stands out from all the rest - not a warrior, not a judge waiting to strike, but a Lamb, looking as if it had once been slain, and yet fully, gloriously alive. The most powerful being in the room is the one who was hurt and did not stay hurt.

He is the hero of this whole story - worthy, when no one else was found worthy, to open what needs opening and to hold safely whatever comes next. That is worth sitting with for a moment: whatever comes next in this story is already held by hands gentle enough to have once been wounded themselves. Once you follow him through that door, you are held by him for good, and there is no going back to standing outside it alone.
