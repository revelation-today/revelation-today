The first strike has failed. Hard times can crack open almost anything - except a heart that has already set itself on the wrong thing. Suffering, on its own, does not fix people. Something else is needed.

So something gentler arrives, in the middle of all that noise: a small scroll, and John is told to eat it, not just read it. It is meant to become part of him, the way something true has to be taken all the way in before it can actually change anything. It tastes sweet at first, and then sits heavier - because truth about God is like that: welcome, and also weighty, asking something real of whoever receives it.

This is not another disaster. It is the opposite of one - a revelation, something true and good about who God actually is, arriving quietly in the middle of everything loud. It takes time to settle. That is allowed. Some things are too important to understand all at once, and this is one of them.
