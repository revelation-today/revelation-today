Step past the curtain, and you see both worlds as they truly are. Loyalty to God is not something you earn a place by - it is simply what shows itself, one way or another, when it is tested.

Two witnesses tell the truth as plainly as they know how, and the world turns against them for it. For a moment - only a moment, three and a half days - it looks like the end. It looks, to everyone watching, like the story is over and the wrong side has won. We tell you this straight away so you are not left sitting in that moment longer than you need to: it does not last, and it is not how this ends.

Because then they stand up. Alive, on their feet, in plain view of the very people who thought they had won and had already stopped watching. Whatever seemed finished was not finished. Whatever looked defeated gets up again, in front of everyone.

This is the pattern woven through the whole of this book, and through every story like it since: not a victory that skips past hard things as though they never happened, but one that walks all the way through them - through the days that felt like the end - and comes out the other side, standing, breathing, alive. If you are living through your own version of those three and a half days right now, this chapter is written for you specifically: whatever has knocked you down is not the last word on your story. Getting back up is.
