Here is the part worth slowing all the way down for - the part this whole long story has been walking toward from its very first page.

A wedding. A city comes down out of heaven, dressed like a bride on her wedding day, and a voice speaks from the throne, saying the very thing every tired, grieving, frightened heart has been waiting the whole story to hear:

"Look - God's home is now among his people. He will live with them."

Sit with that for a moment. Not visit. Not watch from a distance. Live with them - close, permanently, no more separation left to endure.

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes."

Not ignore the tears. Not explain them away or ask you to pretend they were never there. Wipe them - the way someone who loves you would, gently, with his own hand, tear by tear, for as long as it takes.

"There will be no more death, no more mourning, no more crying, no more pain, because all of that has already passed away."

Read that list again, slowly, and let each part land: no more death - the losses that took people from you will not happen again. No more mourning - the grief that has followed you will finally set down its weight. No more crying - not because feeling is forbidden, but because nothing will be left that deserves tears. No more pain - not the kind inflicted on you, and not the kind that lingers in memory long after the danger has passed. All of it, everything you have carried, will have already passed away. Already - meaning by the time you notice, it will be behind you, not still being endured.

The city itself is almost too much to describe. Walls like jasper. Streets like gold so pure it looks like glass. Foundations set with every color of jewel, as if even the ground had been chosen with care. Twelve gates, each one carved from a single pearl - and here is something worth noticing closely: those gates never close. Not because no one is watching, but because there is nothing left outside to guard against. No more night. No more danger. No more need to lock a door against anyone or anything, ever again.

There is no temple anywhere in this city, because God himself, and the Lamb, are the temple now - meaning there is no longer any distance at all between his people and his presence. There is no sun and no moon, because they are simply not needed: the glory of God is the light, warm and constant, with no dusk and no dark stretch of night to get through alone.

And through the middle of the city runs a river, clear as crystal, flowing straight from the throne of God. On either bank stands a tree, bearing fruit in every season, and its leaves are for healing - healing every nation that has ever bled, every people ever driven from their home, every person who has ever needed something to mend what could not otherwise be mended. Whatever wound you are still carrying has a leaf on that tree with its name on it.

The curse that cracked the world open, all the way back in a different garden, is undone here - completely, and for good. God's people will see his face at last. They will carry his name. They will rest, finally, in a home that can never again be taken from them.

The bride and the groom, together at last, for good. God himself moves in with his people - and there is no more distance left to close, not now, not ever again.

You have carried enough for one story. Let this be the part you keep.
