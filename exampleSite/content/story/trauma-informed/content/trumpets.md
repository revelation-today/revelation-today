Now it is the Church's turn to shake the world - not with weapons, but the way Jesus always worked: through what looks like weakness. Prayer rises like incense, the trumpets sound, and creation itself shudders.

Food fails. Water turns bitter. Trade and safety both grow thin. It is hard, and it does not last forever - a season of shaking, not a permanent state.

Even so, it does not turn many hearts around. Hardship alone rarely does; something gentler and truer is still needed, and it is already on its way.
