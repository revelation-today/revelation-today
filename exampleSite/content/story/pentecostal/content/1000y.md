It is done — even though you never saw it happening while you were in the middle of it. Watch an angel come down out of heaven with the key to the abyss and a great chain in his hand, and seize the dragon, that ancient serpent, and bind him for a thousand years, and throw him into the abyss and shut it and seal it over him.

All along, while it looked like you were losing, you were ruling — right now you can see it, thrones in front of you, and seated on them those who had been beheaded for their testimony to Jesus and for the word of God, those who had refused to worship the beast or take its mark. They come to life, and they reign with Christ, right in front of you, for a thousand years — not with the kind of power anyone was watching for. That reign has shaped the outcome of everything.

When the thousand years end, watch Satan released one last time, gathering the nations for battle, surrounding the camp of God's people — and fire comes down from heaven and consumes them, and the devil is thrown into the lake of fire to join the beast and the false prophet, tormented forever.

Now comes the last reckoning, right in front of you: a great white throne, and the one seated on it, and earth and sky fleeing from his presence. The dead stand before the throne, and books are opened, and another book too — the book of life — and the dead are judged by what is written in those books. It is decided, once and for all, right in front of your eyes, who spends eternity with God, and who has chosen, all along, not to.
