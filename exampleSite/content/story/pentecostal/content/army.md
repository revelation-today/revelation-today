Before it gets any worse — feel this happen to you, right now, in the middle of the storm — something stops. Four angels hold back the winds of the earth, waiting. And then: God's people are sealed. Marked. Claimed, right there in the wreckage, as unmistakably his. You feel the mark settle on you like a hand pressed to your forehead.

This is the army of God, and you are standing in its ranks — though it fights like no army you have ever seen or imagined. No swords in these hands. No borrowed violence, no counterfeit crowns. Just relentless worship rising up out of you and a loyalty that outlasts everything the enemy can throw at it.

Look around you: a multitude no one can count, from every nation, every tribe, every people, every language, standing in front of the throne and in front of the Lamb, robed in white, palm branches in their hands, crying out together in a voice loud enough to shake you: "Salvation belongs to our God who sits on the throne, and to the Lamb!"

Whatever comes next, one thing is already settled in you, bone-deep: you know whose side you are on.
