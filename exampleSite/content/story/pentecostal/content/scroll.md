The first strike has failed, right in front of you — you watched it fail. Hard times can crack open just about anything in a human heart. Anything except a heart set on the wrong thing.

A mighty angel comes down, wrapped in a cloud, a rainbow over his head, feet like pillars of fire, and in his hand a little scroll, lying open. He plants one foot on the sea and one foot on the land, and he shouts, and seven thunders answer him — and you reach for a pen to write it down, and a voice stops you: seal it up, do not write this one down.

Then the scroll is in your hands, and you are told: take it, eat it.

You do. It is sweet in your mouth, sweet as honey. Then it turns bitter in your stomach, and something in it will change everything about you, though it will take time to digest, time working its way down into the rest of your life. This is not another disaster landing on you. It is a revelation — something true about God himself, sweet going down and hard to swallow once it settles, about to break through where judgment alone could not reach.
