You are on a rocky island in the middle of the sea. Alone. Exiled here for refusing to bow to anyone but Jesus. It is a Sunday morning like any other — the wind off the water, the same worn stones under your feet — until a voice behind you cracks like a trumpet, and everything changes in an instant.

You turn.

The vision breaks over you all at once, too much and too fast: seven lampstands burning gold in the air in front of you, and standing among them, someone impossible to describe and impossible to look away from. Eyes like fire, burning straight into you. Feet like bronze pulled glowing from a furnace. A voice like the roar of a waterfall crashing over your whole body, and a face too bright to look at directly, like staring straight into the sun at noon. Seven stars rest in his right hand. A sword — sharp on both edges — comes from his mouth.

Your legs go out from under you. You fall like a dead man, face down on the ground, the breath knocked clean out of you.

Then: a hand on your shoulder. Weight and warmth, right there. And words landing before the fear even finishes forming inside you: "Don't be afraid. I am the First and the Last, the Living One. I died — and look, I am alive forever. And I hold the keys of Death and of the Grave itself."

It breaks over you like sunrise breaking over a dark room: it is true. The kingdom you have been waiting for has already arrived. And you — yes, you, right now, on your face on this rocky ground — are a king and a priest in it.

Before you can even catch your breath to celebrate, one urgent thing has to be said, and it is said straight to you. This vision cuts right to the heart of what the Church is actually for: to shine, like those seven lampstands — burning not on your own fuel, but held alight by the very one standing among you right now, right here. Nothing more is asked of you. Nothing less will do.

So: are you ready?
