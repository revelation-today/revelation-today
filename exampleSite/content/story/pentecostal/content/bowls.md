Now God's zeal pours out over you like something long held back and finally released. Seven angels stand right in front of you, each holding a golden bowl full of it, and one by one they tip them out over the earth — bowl after bowl, wave after wave, until the kingdom built on lies has nowhere left to stand.

Feel each one land exactly where it is aimed. Sores break out on those who wear the mark. The sea turns to blood in front of you, and everything in it dies. The rivers and springs turn to blood too, and you hear the angel of the waters say it plainly: they poured out the blood of your saints and your prophets, so you have given them blood to drink — they deserve it. The sun is given power to scorch people with fire, and they curse God's name instead of turning to him, even now, even with the proof burning right in front of their faces.

The fifth bowl plunges the beast's kingdom into darkness. The sixth dries up the Euphrates to clear a road for kings from the east — and you watch three unclean spirits, like frogs, leap out of the mouths of the dragon and the beast and the false prophet, gathering the kings of the whole world for battle.

They gather at a place whose name has become a byword for disaster: Armageddon. You watch them come, certain of victory, banners raised, utterly convinced.

They are already finished. They just do not know it yet.
