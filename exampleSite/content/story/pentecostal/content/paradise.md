And then: a wedding, right in front of you. Watch a city come down out of heaven from God, dressed like a bride adorned for her husband, and hear a loud voice from the throne say what you have been waiting the whole story to hear, right into your own ears: "Look — God's home is now among his people. He will live with them. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death, no more mourning, no more crying, no more pain, because all of that has already passed away."

The city itself is almost too much for you to take in, right in front of you: walls of jasper, streets of gold so pure they look like glass under your own feet, foundations set with every color of jewel, twelve gates and each one carved from a single pearl. There is no temple anywhere in it, because God himself, and the Lamb, are the temple now, standing right there. There is no sun and no moon, because they are simply not needed anymore: the glory of God is the light pouring over you, the Lamb is the lamp, and the gates never close because there is no more night left to shut them against.

Through the middle of it runs a river, clear as crystal, flowing straight from the throne of God past your own feet, with a tree on either bank bearing fruit every month, and leaves for healing every nation that ever bled. The curse — the one that started all the way back in a different garden, the one you felt crack the world open at the very beginning of this story — is gone for good. You will see his face at last, wear his name on your own forehead, and reign with him forever.

The bride and the groom, together at last, for good, right in front of you. God himself moves in with his people — with you — no more distance left to close.

And now that you know the story — get up and go live like you believe it.
