Step past the curtain with me right now, and see both worlds as they truly are, laid open in front of you. You are handed a measuring rod and told to measure the temple — but the outer court is left out, trampled underfoot. The temple in front of you is not a place you earn your way into. It is where your loyalty either shows itself, or does not.

Two witnesses stand in front of you now, clothed in sackcloth, and they testify with everything they have for twelve hundred and sixty days. Fire comes out of their mouths to devour anyone who tries to harm them. And then, when their testimony is finished, watch the beast rise up out of the pit and overpower them right in front of you. The world kills them for it, and leaves their bodies lying in the street, and throws a party over the corpses — gifts exchanged, celebrations in the streets, because these two had tormented everyone's conscience and now, finally, they are silent.

For three and a half days, it looks like total defeat. You feel the weight of it, the sense that evil has actually won this time.

Then — watch closely, right now — breath enters them. They stand up on their own feet. Terror falls on everyone watching. A loud voice from heaven calls, "Come up here," and they rise into the cloud in front of everyone's eyes, enemies included.

That is exactly the pattern you are asked to live: not a victory that skips over suffering, but one that walks straight through it and comes out the other side standing.
