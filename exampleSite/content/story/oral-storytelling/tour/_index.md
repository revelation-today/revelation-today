*A script for one narrator and a gathered group. The group's answer never changes - teach it once, and they will call it back before you finish asking. Practice it now: you ask "Who sits on the throne?" - they answer "The Lamb who was slain!" Good. Begin.*

# How everything started

**Narrator:** In the beginning, there was nothing. Then - breath. Light broke over the water. God knelt in the dust, shaped a man with his own hands, and breathed into him. *(pause here, let the silence sit)* His name was Adam. God gave him a garden and said: this is yours - tend it, rule it.

One thing was not good: Adam was alone. So God built Eve from Adam's own side. For one shining moment, everything was as it should be.

It did not last. The man and the woman wanted what God had given more than they wanted God himself. One small reach. One small bite. The world cracked like a dropped jar.

Driven from the garden, their children built their own gardens instead - cities, towers, kingdoms - anything to fill the hole where God used to be. But God did not let go. He called Abraham: leave everything, follow me. Abraham said yes - even when it cost him the son he waited a lifetime for. Out of Abraham, God grew a whole nation: Israel.

# The promise

**Narrator:** Israel carried the promise like a lamp. Would she love the lamp more than the light? She did. Generation after generation, the people drifted, until the covenant lay in ruins and they were driven from their own land - just like Adam, driven from his garden.

But God was not finished. He promised a Redeemer - one who would build a kingdom that never falls, its door open to anyone willing to walk through.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

# New beginnings

**Narrator:** Picture an old man, alone on a rocky island in the sea. His name is John, exiled there for refusing to bow to anyone but Jesus. A Sunday morning like any other - until a voice behind him cracks like a trumpet.

He turns. *(turn your head slowly)* And the vision breaks over him: seven lamps burning gold in the air, and among them, someone impossible to look away from. Eyes like fire. Feet like glowing bronze. A voice like a roaring waterfall. A face too bright to look at - like staring into the sun. Seven stars rest in his hand. A sword comes from his mouth.

John falls down like a dead man. Then - a hand on his shoulder, and words that melt the fear before it forms: "Do not be afraid. I am the First and the Last. I died - and look! - I am alive forever. I hold the keys of Death and the Grave."

*(lift your voice - this is good news)* The kingdom you have waited for has come. And you - yes, you - are a king and a priest in it.

But one thing must be said first: those seven lamps are the Church. She burns not on her own fuel, but because he stands among her, holding her alight. Nothing more is asked of her. Nothing less will do.

So I ask you: is she ready?

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

# A grand disaster

**Narrator:** Seven churches. Seven letters. Seven mirrors held up to seven rooms full of people just like us.

Ephesus works herself to the bone - correct in doctrine, faithful in duty - and has let love slip through her fingers. Without love, all that labor is worth nothing.

Smyrna is poor, hated, squeezed on every side - one of only two churches Jesus praises without blame. Pergamum stands her ground in the shadow of Satan's own throne - some die for it - yet false teaching has crept in the back door. In Thyatira, the rot starts at the top.

Sardis looks alive - *(shake your head slowly)* - but underneath she is already dead, so sure she can manage without God. Philadelphia is the other church Jesus praises without holding back - small, worn thin, and still faithful.

And Laodicea: lukewarm, comfortable, blind to how poor she is - the only church that gets no praise at all.

*(soften your voice)* But even here, Jesus does not walk away. He knocks. He still wants in.

# Guidance is ahead

**Narrator:** So it falls to us to get ready for what is coming. But how do you prepare for something this big?

Jesus says: look up. Heaven opens, and there it is - a throne room, where everyone finds their true place before God. The door stands open. Always.

But worship is not cheap. It costs everything - laying your own crown in the dust. And in that room, one figure stands out from all the rest: a Lamb, looking as if it had been slain. *(drop your voice on "slain")*

He is the hero of this whole story - worthy, when no one else was found worthy, to open what needed opening and rule what needed ruling.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

# Evil awakes - you need to join the army

**Narrator:** The seals break, one by one, and each one lets something loose. *(count on your fingers as you go)*

First, a white horse - a rider with a bow and a crown, hungry for conquest. For one heartbeat it looks like victory. It is not: this rider has stolen the true King's color to sell a lie. Behind him, a blood-red horse, and peace is torn off the earth. Behind that, a black horse, weighing a day's bread against a day's wage. Behind that, a horse the color of ash - its rider's name is Death, the grave riding at his heels.

Conquest becomes war. War becomes famine. Famine becomes death.

Then a seal breaks that shows not armies but an altar - and under it, souls crying: how long, Lord, until you judge the earth? They are told: wait, a little longer. The sky answers: the sun goes black, the moon turns to blood, the stars fall like fruit shaken from a tree.

*(now the turn - lift your voice with hope)* But before it gets worse - God's people are sealed. Marked. Claimed as unmistakably his.

This is the army of God! No swords, no borrowed violence - only worship that will not quit. Whatever comes next, one thing is settled: you know whose side you are on.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

# The first strike

**Narrator:** Now it is the Church's turn to shake the world - not with weapons, but the way Jesus always worked: through what looks like weakness. Prayer rises like smoke. The trumpets sound. The earth trembles.

Food fails. Water turns bitter. Trade collapses. Surely now people will look up and ask what is real?

*(pause, shake your head)* They do not. Even as their idols turn on them, people cling tighter, not looser. Hard times alone have never changed a human heart.

# Reality check

**Narrator:** The first strike has failed. Hard times can crack open almost anything - except a heart already set on the wrong thing.

So a scroll appears. John is told: eat it. This is not another disaster - it is a revelation, something true about God himself. Sweet in the mouth. Hard to swallow.

# The big clash

**Narrator:** Step past the curtain now, and see both worlds as they truly are.

Two witnesses testify with everything they have. The world kills them for it and throws a party over the bodies. For three and a half days, it looks like total defeat.

*(let the silence hang - three, two, one)* Then - they stand up. That is the pattern the Church is asked to live: not a victory that skips the suffering, but one that walks through it and comes out standing.

Now the same story again, pulled back further. A woman appears in the sky, clothed with the sun, twelve stars circling her head. She is pregnant, crying out in labor. Then the sky tears open, and something worse appears: an enormous red dragon, seven crowned heads, ten horns, a tail sweeping a third of the stars down to earth. He waits before her - the instant the child is born, he means to swallow him.

*(lean forward, drop your voice)* The child is born anyway - a son who will rule every nation. Before the dragon's jaws close, the boy is snatched safe to God's own throne.

War breaks out in heaven! Michael and his angels against the dragon and his -

**All respond:** And the dragon loses!

**Narrator:** He is hurled down to earth, beaten by the blood of the Lamb and the testimony of people who would not save their own lives to escape him. Furious, running out of time, he turns his rage on the woman - and on everyone who belongs to her.

*(warm your voice back up)* But take heart: defeating the dragon was never your job. Jesus already did that, before the fight ever reached you. He tried to kill Jesus - failed. He tried to keep his place in heaven - thrown out. He tried to wipe out Israel - failed again.

The dragon roared. The dragon lost. The dragon always loses.

So he reaches for new weapons - two beasts, one from the sea, one from the earth. One rules by force, one deceives by wonders - together, a cheap copy of God, Christ, and the Spirit. Look closer, though: it conquers only by violence and builds nothing that lasts, because the One it copies has already won.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

# Victory

**Narrator:** Jesus already won it at the cross, and now the enemy's forces scatter like smoke. On a sea of glass and fire, the faithful sing. Their only qualification was staying loyal.

Not everyone sings. The ones who played it safe and sided with the beast now face a far heavier cost. Two sickles swing across the earth - one harvest gathers in, one is for judgment. Truth stops being a story you can argue about.

Then God's zeal pours out like a river long held back - bowl after bowl - until the kingdom built on lies has nowhere left to stand. The armies of the earth gather for one last stand, at a place whose name is now a byword for disaster: Armageddon. They come certain of victory.

*(almost a whisper)* They are already finished. They just do not know it yet.

# The ultimate battle

**Narrator:** One last deception waits to be unmasked. A woman, dazzling, dressed in jewels that should have belonged to the bride, promises riches to anyone who drinks from her cup. Pull back the dress, and there is the beast underneath, teeth and all. She is no bride. She is a harlot - and plenty inside the Church itself have fallen for the act.

Her final performance looks flawless. It is not. The very powers the devil sent to destroy the Church turn instead and tear the harlot apart - his own weapon, turned against him.

And that is what this whole story was always about - not simply beating evil, but God binding himself to his people forever, like a marriage. The road is finally clear to finish the dragon for good.

# A new beginning

**Narrator:** It is done - even though you never saw it happening. All along, while it looked like the Church was losing, she was ruling. Just not with the kind of power anyone was watching for.

Now comes the last reckoning: a final judgment, deciding once and for all who spends forever with God, and who has chosen, all along, not to.

And then - a wedding! *(let your voice open wide - this is the loud part, let the people cheer)*

A city comes down out of heaven, dressed like a bride. And a voice from the throne says what everyone has waited the whole story to hear: "Look! God's home is now among his people. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. No more death. No more mourning. No more crying. No more pain. All of that has passed away."

Walls of jasper. Streets of gold like glass. Twelve gates, each one a single pearl. No temple in the city, for God himself and the Lamb are the temple now. No sun, no moon - not needed, for the glory of God is the light, and the gates never close, because there is no more night to shut them against.

A river runs through the middle of it, clear as crystal, from the throne of God, with a tree on either bank bearing fruit every month, and leaves for healing every nation that ever bled. The curse - the one that started in a different garden - is gone for good.

God's people will see his face at last. Wear his name. Reign with him forever. The bride and the groom, together for good. God himself moves in with his people - no more distance left to close.

*(final pause - let it be still)* And now that you know the story - go and live like you believe it.

One last time, so you never forget it -

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!
