**Narrator:** The first strike has failed. Trumpet after trumpet shook the earth, and still no one turned back to God. Hard times can crack open just about anything you can name - except a heart that has already set itself on the wrong thing.

*(hold out both hands, as if holding a small scroll)* So a scroll appears. A small one, already open, in a mighty angel's hand - one foot on the sea, one foot on the land, as if to say: this word covers everything, all of it, land and sea alike. And a voice tells John: take it. Eat it.

He eats it. And it is sweet in his mouth, like honey.

*(pause)* Then it turns bitter in his stomach.

That is exactly how this works. Something in this scroll will change everything - though it will take time to digest, time to work its way all the way through him before it comes back out as words for others to hear.

This is not another disaster raining down from the sky. This is a revelation. Something true about God himself, breaking through where judgment alone could never reach - because judgment can break a life open, but only truth, held close and swallowed whole, can heal it.

Sweet going in. Hard to carry once it is inside you.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** That is the truth this whole scroll is made of - sweet to hear, and hard to live out. Chew on it. It is about to change everything that happens next.
