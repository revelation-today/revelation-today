**Narrator:** Now the same story is told again - pulled back further, so you can see the frame around it. A very different Christmas story, played out across the sky itself.

*(gesture upward, wide)* A woman appears, clothed with the sun, the moon under her feet, twelve stars circling her head like a crown. She is pregnant, crying out in the pain of labor.

Then the sky tears open again, and something far worse appears: an enormous red dragon. Seven heads, crowned. Ten horns. A tail vast enough to sweep a third of the stars out of heaven and hurl them down to the earth.

He plants himself right in front of the woman. Waiting. *(lean in, drop your voice)* The instant the child is born, he means to swallow him whole.

The child is born anyway - a son who will rule every nation. And before the dragon's jaws can close around him, the boy is snatched up, safe, all the way to God's own throne.

War breaks out in heaven! Michael and his angels against the dragon and his -

**All respond:** And the dragon loses!

**Narrator:** There is no place left for him in heaven. He is hurled down to the earth - beaten by the blood of the Lamb, and by the plain testimony of people who would not save their own lives to escape him.

Furious, and running out of time, he turns his rage on the woman - and when she slips out of his reach, he turns it on everyone who belongs to her instead.

*(reassure them - warm your voice)* But take heart anyway. Defeating the dragon was never your job. Jesus already did that, in heaven, before it ever reached you.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Your job is simpler: keep drawing people toward him, even while the dragon rages harder than ever, because he knows exactly how little time he has left.
