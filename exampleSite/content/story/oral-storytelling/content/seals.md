**Narrator:** The seals break. One. By one. And each one lets something loose into the world. *(count them off on your fingers as you go)*

First - a white horse gallops out. A rider with a bow. A crown handed to him. Already hungry for conquest. For one heartbeat, it looks like victory.

It is not. Jesus is not the only one asking for your loyalty, and this rider is a fraud - he has stolen the color of the true King's horse to sell you a lie.

Strip the costume off, and violence is all that is left. Behind the white horse comes a blood-red one, and peace is torn off the earth like a scab. Behind the red horse, a black one, its rider holding a pair of scales: a day's wages for a day's bread, while oil and wine stay safe for those who can still afford them. And behind the black horse - a horse the color of ash. Its rider's name is Death. The grave rides right behind him.

Conquest becomes war. War becomes famine. Famine becomes death.

*(let this land heavy)* Then a seal breaks, and it shows not armies, but an altar - and underneath it, the souls of those killed for staying loyal, crying out with one voice: how long, Lord, holy and true, until you judge the earth and pay back our blood?

They are told: wait. Just a little longer.

The sky itself answers next. The sun goes black. The moon turns to blood. The stars fall like fruit shaken loose from a tree. The sky peels back like an old scroll rolling shut.

The world is drowning in what it has done to itself. And the Church stands right there in the wreckage, crying out too.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Hold that answer close. It is about to be tested.
