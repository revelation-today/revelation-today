**Narrator:** And then - a wedding! *(let your voice open up wide here - this is the loud part, let the people cheer)*

A city comes down out of heaven, dressed like a bride on her wedding day. And a voice from the throne says what everyone has been waiting the whole story to hear:

"Look - God's home is now among his people. He will live with them. He will wipe every tear from their eyes."

*(slow down, count these on your fingers)* No more death. No more mourning. No more crying. No more pain. All of that has already passed away.

The city itself is almost too much to describe. Walls of jasper. Streets of gold so pure it looks like glass. Foundations set with every color of jewel. Twelve gates, and each one carved from a single pearl.

There is no temple anywhere in it - because God himself, and the Lamb, are the temple now. There is no sun and no moon, because they are simply not needed anymore: the glory of God is the light, the Lamb is the lamp, and the gates never close, because there is no more night left to shut them against.

*(gesture flowing water)* Through the middle of it runs a river, clear as crystal, flowing straight from the throne of God, with a tree on either bank bearing fruit every month, and leaves for healing every nation that ever bled.

The curse - the one that started all the way back in a different garden, the one this whole story began with - is gone. Gone for good.

God's people will see his face at last. Wear his name. Reign with him forever.

The bride and the groom, together at last, for good. God himself moves in with his people - no more distance left to close.

*(final pause - let it be still)* And now that you know the story - go and live like you believe it.

Ask them one more time -

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** And he always will be.
