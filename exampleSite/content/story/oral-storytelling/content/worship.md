**Narrator:** So it falls to us - to all of us - to get up and get ready for what is coming. But how do you prepare for something this big?

Jesus says: look up.

*(gesture upward, slowly)* The answer is not a plan, not a strategy. The answer is a throne room. Heaven opens, and there it is - the one place where everyone and everything finds its true position before God. The door stands open. It has always stood open.

But do not think worship is an easy fix. It costs something. It means laying your own crown down in the dust, and giving God every part of your life, without exception.

And in that room, one figure stands out from all the rest. Not a lion, though a lion was announced. Look again. *(pause)* A Lamb. Standing as if it had been slain.

*(drop your voice on "slain," then let it rise)*

He is the hero of this whole story. Worthy - when no one else could be found worthy - to open what needed opening, and to rule what needed ruling.

Once you follow him through that door, there is no going back to who you were before.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Say it once more, and mean it -

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Hold onto that. Everything that comes next in this story - all of it - is seen from right here, from this room, from this throne. Nothing that follows can shake what you have just seen.
