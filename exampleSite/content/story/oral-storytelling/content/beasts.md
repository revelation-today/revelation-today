**Narrator:** Look at the dragon's scoreboard so far. *(hold up one finger for each item)*

He tried to kill Jesus - and failed.

He tried to keep his place in heaven - and was thrown out.

He tried to wipe out Israel - and failed again.

The dragon roared. The dragon lost. The dragon always loses.

*(shift your tone - now he is scrambling, out of good moves)* So he reaches for new weapons. Two beasts - one clawing up out of the sea, one crawling up out of the earth. One rules by brute force, demanding worship at the point of a sword. One deceives with wonders and signs, so dazzling that people forget to ask who is really behind them. And together, they are a grotesque copy - of God, of Christ, of the Spirit - close enough to fool almost anyone who is not paying attention.

But look closer. *(narrow your eyes, lean in)* The imitation is already cracking. The beast conquers only by violence. It dazzles only by trickery. It builds nothing that will last.

Because the One it is copying has already won. Permanently. There is no copy of a Lamb who was slain and is alive forever - there is only the real thing, and everything else is a forgery wearing his face.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Not the beast. Never the beast. Say it again, so the forgery cannot fool you -

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!
