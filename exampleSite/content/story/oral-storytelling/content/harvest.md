**Narrator:** Jesus already won it at the cross. And the Church has kept faith with that victory ever since. Now the enemy's forces scatter like smoke before wind.

*(gesture as if standing on still water)* On a sea of glass and fire, the faithful stand and sing a new song, a song no one else can learn, because no one else has walked the road they walked to get there. Listen for what qualified them - it was not cleverness, not wealth, not power, not even how much they suffered. Their only qualification was staying loyal, all the way through, to the very end.

Not everyone gets to sing. *(let your voice sober)* The ones who played it safe, who sided with the beast to avoid the cost of standing with the Lamb - they now face a far heavier one than the one they were trying so hard to avoid.

Two sickles swing across the earth. *(mime a sweeping motion, twice)*

One harvest gathers in.

One harvest is for judgment.

This is the moment truth stops being theoretical. This is the moment it becomes real - for everyone, no exceptions.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** He is the one gathering the harvest, with his own hand on the sickle. Which one you are gathered into was never a mystery, and it was never decided at the last minute - it was decided by who you stayed loyal to, all along, every ordinary day before this one.
