**Narrator:** One deception still waits to be unmasked before the story can end. And unmasking it shows what this whole story was always about - not simply defeating evil, but God binding himself to his people forever. Like a marriage.

Here is the deception. *(gesture as if admiring fine clothing)* A woman, dazzling, draped in jewels that should have belonged to the bride. She promises prosperity to anyone who will drink from her cup.

Pull back the dress, though. *(rip the gesture away suddenly)* There is the beast underneath. Teeth and all.

She is no bride. She is a harlot, riding on the very beast she claims to have tamed. And plenty of people inside the Church itself have fallen for the act, drinking from that cup without asking what was in it, or who was pouring it.

Her final performance looks flawless. It is not.

*(let this land as a surprise)* The very powers the devil sends to destroy the Church turn instead, and tear the harlot apart with their own hands - his own weapon, used against him, the deceiver deceived by his own creation.

The road is finally clear to finish him for good.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Not the harlot. Not her jewels, not her cup, not her promises of easy prosperity. Only the Lamb - and he is coming for his bride, the real one, dressed not in stolen jewels but in righteousness given as a gift, at last.
