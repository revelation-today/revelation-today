**Narrator:** Now it is the Church's turn to shake the world. Not with weapons - the way Jesus always worked, through what looks like weakness.

*(mime holding something rising in your open palms)* Prayer rises like incense smoke, up before the throne of God. And when it reaches him - the trumpets sound, one after another, seven of them, and the earth itself begins to tremble.

Food fails. Water turns bitter. Trade collapses. Security disappears like morning mist. Everything people leaned on instead of God begins to buckle under them, one trumpet at a time. Surely now, surely now, people will finally look up and ask what is real?

*(pause. shake your head slowly.)*

They do not.

Even as their idols turn on them - even as those same idols strip away every last shred of hope, until death itself starts to look like relief - people cling tighter. Not looser. Tighter.

*(let this sit - do not rush past it)* Hard times alone have never been enough to change a human heart. Not then. Not now. Not ever. A person can lose everything - food, water, safety, comfort - and still cling to the very thing that is destroying them, rather than let it go and turn to God.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Remember that answer well - because judgment alone was never going to be what turns the world around. Something else is coming. Something sweeter, and harder to swallow.
