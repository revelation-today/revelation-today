**Narrator:** It is done - even if you never saw it happening while it was going on.

*(pause, let that surprise sit)* All along, while it looked like the Church was losing - losing her people, losing her standing, losing every visible fight - she was ruling. Not with swords. Not with armies. Not with any kind of power anyone was watching for. She ruled by staying loyal when it cost her everything, and that hidden reign has shaped the outcome of the whole story.

Think of the ones who came before you - the ones who stayed faithful and never saw the victory with their own eyes. They were not wasting their lives. They were ruling, the whole time, with him.

Now comes the last reckoning. A final judgment. *(slow down, let the weight of this show)* Every name is opened. Every deed is weighed. It is decided, once and for all, who spends eternity with God - and who has chosen, all along, not to.

No one is surprised by the verdict who was paying attention to their own life, and no one is left out who ever truly wanted in.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** He was ruling the whole time - even when it did not look like it. Even when you could not see it. Even now.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!
