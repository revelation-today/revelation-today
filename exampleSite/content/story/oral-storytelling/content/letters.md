**Narrator:** Seven churches. Seven letters. Seven mirrors, held up to seven rooms full of people just like us. *(hold up seven fingers, and fold one down as you name each church)*

Ephesus works herself to the bone. Correct in every teaching. Faithful in every duty. And somewhere along the way, love slipped through her fingers like water. Without love, all that labor is worth nothing at all.

Smyrna is poor. Hated. Squeezed on every side. And she is one of only two churches Jesus has nothing but praise for.

Pergamum stands her ground in the shadow of Satan's own throne - some die for it - yet false teaching has crept in the back door, quiet as a thief.

In Thyatira, the rot starts at the top: the leaders themselves have wandered off the path.

Sardis looks alive. *(shake your head slowly)* But underneath, she is already dead - so sure she can manage fine without God, and failing at almost everything because of it.

Philadelphia is the other church Jesus praises without holding anything back - small, worn thin, nothing left in the purse, and still faithful to the end.

And then - Laodicea. Lukewarm. Comfortable. Blind to how poor she really is. The only church that gets no praise at all.

*(soften your voice - this is not a door slamming shut)* But even here, Jesus does not walk away. He knocks. He warns. He still wants in.

Seven letters. Seven chances to wake up. And every single one of them is still being read out loud, even now, in rooms just like this one.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** He is the one writing every one of these letters - and he is not finished writing yours.
