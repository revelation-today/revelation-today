**Narrator:** Step past the curtain now, and see both worlds as they truly are. The temple is measured, marked out - not because you can earn your way into it with good behavior, but because it is where your loyalty either shows itself plainly, or it does not.

Two witnesses stand in the middle of a hostile city and testify. Dressed in sackcloth. Holding nothing back. Giving everything they have got, for as long as they are given to give it.

And the world kills them for it. *(let your voice drop low)* Kills them - and then throws a party over their bodies, laughing, celebrating, sending each other gifts, glad to be rid of them at last.

For three and a half days, it looks like total defeat. It looks finished.

*(let the silence hang here - count it out: three... two... one...)*

Then - they stand up.

*(lift your voice sharply - let this land like a shock)*

On their feet again, breath in their lungs, and the whole watching world goes cold with fear.

That is exactly the pattern the Church herself is asked to live out, in every generation, in every place she is hated for her testimony. Not a victory that skips over the suffering. Not a shortcut around the cost. A victory that walks straight through it, all the way through, and comes out standing on the other side, alive, unashamed, and still speaking.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** He died too. And he stood up too. Whatever they do to you for staying loyal - that is not the end of your story either.
