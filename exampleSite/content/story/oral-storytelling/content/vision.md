*Teach the refrain before you start, if the group has not heard it yet: you ask "Who sits on the throne?" - they answer "The Lamb who was slain!"*

**Narrator:** Picture an old man. Alone. On a rocky island out in the sea. His name is John, and he is there for one reason only: he would not bow to anyone but Jesus.

It is a Sunday, a morning like any other - *(pause)* - until a voice behind him cracks like a trumpet blast, and nothing is ever the same again.

He turns. *(turn your own head, slowly, and let them turn with you)* And there it is - seven lamps of gold burning in the air. And standing among them: someone impossible to describe, impossible to look away from.

Eyes like fire. Feet like bronze pulled glowing from the furnace. A voice like a waterfall roaring. A face too bright to look at - like staring straight into the sun. Seven stars rest in his hand. A sharp sword comes from his mouth.

John does not stay on his feet. He falls down like a dead man.

*(soften your voice now)* Then - a hand on his shoulder. Words that melt the fear before it even finishes forming: "Do not be afraid. I am the First and the Last. I died - and look! - I am alive forever. I hold the keys of Death and the Grave itself."

The kingdom you have been waiting for has already come. And you - yes, you, sitting right there - you are a king and a priest in it.

But one thing must be said before we celebrate. Those seven lamps are the Church - and she does not burn on her own fuel. She burns because he stands among her, holding her alight. Nothing more is asked of her. Nothing less will do.

So I ask you plainly: is she ready?

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!
