**Narrator:** Now God's zeal pours out like something long held back and finally released. *(mime tipping a bowl, again and again)* Bowl after bowl. Seven of them, one after another, wave after wave, until the kingdom built on lies has nowhere left to stand.

Every bowl lands exactly where it is aimed - at false worship, at cruelty toward God's people, at hearts too hardened to break even now, even after everything that came before it: the seals, the trumpets, the warnings, every chance already given and already refused. Nothing is wasted, and nothing is random.

*(gather your voice, building)* The armies of the earth gather for one last stand, drawn together as if by some invisible hand, at a place whose very name has become a byword for disaster.

Armageddon.

They come certain of victory. Banners flying. Weapons sharpened. Certain.

*(pause - almost a whisper)*

They are already finished. They just do not know it yet.

Who sits on the throne?

**All respond:** The Lamb who was slain!

**Narrator:** Not the armies. Not the beast. Not the lie dressed up as a kingdom, with its banners and its promises and its borrowed crown. Only the Lamb. He has already decided how this ends, and no army on earth, however large, however certain, can change the verdict.
