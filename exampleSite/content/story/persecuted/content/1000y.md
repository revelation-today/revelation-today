It is done, even if you never saw it happening. All along, while it looked like the Church was losing, she was ruling - just not with the kind of power anyone was watching for. The martyrs under the altar, the witnesses who stood back up, every quiet act of staying loyal when it would have been easier not to - none of it was wasted, and none of it went unseen.

Now comes the last reckoning: a final judgment, where it is decided, once and for all, who spends eternity with God, and who has chosen, all along, not to.

Nothing you have endured for his sake was wasted, even the parts of it no one else ever saw or counted. It all counted. It still does.
