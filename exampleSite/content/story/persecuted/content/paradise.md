And then: a wedding. A city comes down out of heaven, and a voice from the throne says what you have waited the whole story to hear: "God's home is now among his people. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. No more death, no more mourning, no more crying, no more pain, because all of that has already passed away."

No temple, because God himself and the Lamb are the temple now. No sun, because his glory is the light. No night left to shut the gates against. A river runs through the middle of it, and a tree with leaves for the healing of every nation that ever bled. The curse - the one that started in a different garden - is gone for good.

You will see his face. You will wear his name. You will reign with him forever. Whatever it costs you to hold onto this, it is already yours.
