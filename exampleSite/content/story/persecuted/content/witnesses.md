Two witnesses testify with everything they have. The world kills them for it, then celebrates over their bodies. For three and a half days, it looks like total defeat - proof, to everyone watching, that God had lost.

Then they stand up.

This is exactly the pattern the Church is asked to live: not a victory that skips over suffering, but one that walks straight through it and comes out the other side standing. Notice this too - they are killed only once their testimony is finished. Their mission was complete before the world declared it over.

If your story right now looks, from the outside, like a slow ending, hold onto this: the world's read on when a testimony is "finished," and God's read on it, are not the same thing. What looks like the end is not always the end.
