The seals break, one by one, and each lets something loose: conquest, then war, then famine, then death. Real disaster, in real time - the world drowning in what it has brought on itself.

Then a seal breaks that shows not an army but an altar. Under it are the souls of those killed for staying loyal to God, crying out with one voice: "How long, Lord, holy and true, until you judge the earth and avenge our blood?"

They are not corrected for asking. They are answered - white robes, and the honest truth: wait a little longer. Others will still join you.

If you have ever prayed "how long," you are praying exactly what they prayed. It is not a failure of faith. It is one of the most faithful prayers in this whole book.
