Jesus writes to seven real churches - a mirror for every church since. Some have grown cold. Some have let lies in. Some look alive and are already dead inside.

Two of the seven get nothing but praise from him. Smyrna: poor, hated, squeezed on every side. To her he says plainly - some of you will suffer, some will be imprisoned. Be faithful even to death, and I will give you life as your crown. Philadelphia: worn down, with nothing left in the bank, and still faithful.

Even to the coldest church, lukewarm and blind to its own poverty, Jesus does not walk away. He knocks. He warns. He still wants in.

If your church looks small and worn out to the world, take note of which churches he praised without reserve.
