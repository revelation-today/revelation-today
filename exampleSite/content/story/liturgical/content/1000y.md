It is accomplished, even where it went unseen. All along, while the Church appeared to be losing, she was reigning - not with the kind of power anyone was watching for, but with the quiet authority of those who worship rightly while the world rages. John sees thrones, and those seated on them are given authority to judge; and the souls of those beheaded for the testimony of Jesus and the word of God come to life and reign with Christ. This is the first resurrection, and blessed and holy is anyone who shares in it: over such the second death has no power, but they will be priests of God and of Christ, and will reign with him.

That reign, hidden as it was, has shaped the outcome of everything. It is the reign of those who kept the liturgy of faithfulness even under the beast's own altar-mockery, refusing his mark, refusing his counterfeit worship, and so refusing, quietly, all along, to bow anywhere but before the one true throne.

When the appointed time is finished, the deceiver is loosed once more for a little while, gathers the nations for battle, surrounds the camp of the saints and the beloved city - and fire comes down from heaven and consumes them. The deceiver is thrown into the lake of fire and sulfur, joining the beast and the false prophet, to be tormented day and night forever and ever. His long war against the woman and her offspring, against every altar and every lamp raised in testimony, ends exactly as it always would: cast out for good, exactly as he once was cast out of heaven.

Now comes the last reckoning: a great white throne, from whose presence earth and sky flee away, and no place is found for them. The dead, great and small, stand before that throne, and books are opened, and another book too, the book of life, and the dead are judged by what is written in the books, by what they have done. Whoever is not found written in the book of life is thrown into the lake of fire - the second death.

It is decided, once for all, who spends eternity before that throne in endless worship, and who has chosen, all along, not to.
