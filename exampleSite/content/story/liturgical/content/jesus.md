Now the same story is told again, drawn back to reveal the frame surrounding it - a very different nativity, enacted across the sky itself. A woman appears, clothed with the sun, the moon beneath her feet, a crown of twelve stars circling her head. She is with child, crying out in the pangs of labor - the Church, and Israel before her, bearing forth the Christ into a world already lying in wait to devour him.

Then the sky tears open again, and something far worse appears: an enormous red dragon, seven crowned heads, ten horns, a tail vast enough to sweep a third of the stars from heaven and cast them to the earth. He stations himself before the woman, waiting - the instant the child is born, he intends to devour him whole.

The child is born regardless - a son who will rule every nation with an iron scepter - and before the dragon's jaws can close, the child is caught up to God's own throne, safe beyond the reach of every enemy. War breaks out in heaven: Michael and his angels against the dragon and his, and the dragon is defeated. No place remains for him there any longer. He is cast down to the earth, and a voice rings out, declaring that salvation and power and the kingdom of God have come, and the authority of his Christ, for the accuser of the brothers has been thrown down - the one who accused them day and night before our God. He is overcome by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of the testimony of those who did not cling to their own lives in the face of him.

Enraged, and knowing his time is short, the dragon turns on the woman - and when she is carried to a place prepared for her, he turns his fury upon the rest of her offspring, all who keep the commandments of God and hold fast the testimony of Jesus.

Take courage nonetheless: overcoming the dragon was never the Church's task. Christ has already accomplished it in heaven, before the battle ever reached the earth. The task now given is simply to keep bearing witness, to keep drawing others toward the altar where that victory is proclaimed - even while the dragon, knowing exactly how little time remains to him, rages harder than ever before.
