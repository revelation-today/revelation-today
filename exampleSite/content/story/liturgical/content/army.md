Before matters worsen, something else occurs: God's own are sealed - marked upon the forehead, claimed in the midst of the storm as unmistakably his, exactly as every believer is marked at the font and at confirmation, set apart before the altar as belonging to the Lord.

Then John sees the multitude that seal has gathered: a great throng no one can number, from every nation and tribe and people and tongue, standing before the throne and before the Lamb, robed in white, palm branches in their hands - the same white robes worn by every soul beneath the altar, the same procession echoed whenever a congregation processes with palms, whenever the newly baptized are clothed in white at the font. They cry out with a great voice: "Salvation belongs to our God, who sits on the throne, and to the Lamb." The angels and elders and living creatures fall on their faces before the throne and worship, saying: "Amen! Blessing and glory and wisdom and thanksgiving and honor and power and might be to our God forever and ever. Amen." This is the same sevenfold doxology still echoed in every solemn "Glory be" offered at the end of a psalm.

One elder asks who these are, robed in white, and answers his own question: these are the ones who have come out of the great tribulation, who have washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb. Therefore they stand before the throne of God and serve him day and night in his temple - the eternal liturgy, unbroken, of which every earthly Divine Liturgy is already a participation. He who sits on the throne will shelter them; they will hunger no more, thirst no more, and God himself will wipe away every tear from their eyes.

This is the army of God - though it fights like no army ever assembled. No swords, no borrowed violence, only ceaseless worship and a loyalty nothing can exhaust. Whatever follows, this much is already settled: whose side has been chosen, and whose liturgy is being kept.
