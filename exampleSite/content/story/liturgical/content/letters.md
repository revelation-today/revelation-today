Seven churches, seven letters, seven mirrors held before seven very different congregations - each measured, still, by whether her lamp burns true before the One who walks among them.

Ephesus labors to exhaustion, sound in every doctrine, faithful in every duty - and has quietly let love slip through her fingers. Without it, all the labor comes to nothing, however correctly the liturgy is kept. Better, says the Lord, that this lampstand be removed than to persist so.

Smyrna could not be more different: poor, despised, pressed on every side by a hostile city - and one of only two churches to receive nothing but commendation.

Pergamum holds her ground in the very shadow of Satan's throne. Some die for it. Yet within her walls, false teaching has crept in unopposed, quietly leading the flock astray from sound worship.

In Thyatira, the corruption begins at the top: the shepherds themselves have wandered from the fold they were set to guard.

Sardis appears alive from without - her forms perhaps still observed. Within, she is already dead, persuaded she can manage without God, and failing at nearly everything as a result.

Philadelphia is the other church commended without reservation: small, depleted, with nothing left in her treasury, and faithful still - and to her the Lord promises a place as a pillar in the temple of his God, never again to go out from it.

Then there is Laodicea: lukewarm, self-satisfied, blind to her own poverty - the only church to receive no praise at all. And yet even at her door, the Lord does not depart. He knocks. He warns. He still desires to enter and to dine with her, to keep supper with her as with a friend - the first small foretaste, here, of the wedding feast the whole book is moving toward. To the one who conquers, he promises a seat beside him on his own throne, the way the redeemed will one day stand nearest the altar of the Lamb.

Seven lampstands, seven calls to watch and to worship rightly - each letter closing with the same refrain: let anyone with an ear hear what the Spirit says to the churches.
