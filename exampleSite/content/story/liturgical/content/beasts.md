Consider the dragon's record so far:
- He sought to kill Christ, and failed.
- He sought to keep his place in heaven, and was cast out.
- He sought to destroy Israel, and failed again.

So he reaches for new instruments - two beasts, one clawing up out of the sea, one crawling up out of the earth. The first is given the dragon's own power and throne and great authority, and the whole earth marvels and worships the dragon and the beast, asking, "Who is like the beast, and who can fight against it?" - a mockery of the very acclamation that belongs, rightly, only before the throne of the Lamb. It blasphemes God's name and his dwelling, those who dwell in heaven, and makes war on the saints.

The second beast rises from the earth, deceiving by wonders and signs, causing the earth to worship the first beast, even making an image of it and demanding that all - small and great, rich and poor, free and slave - be marked, and that none may buy or sell without that mark. Here is the counterfeit sacrament, the mark set against the seal already placed on the foreheads of the redeemed: a false christening offered by a false priesthood, for whoever the beast is, he is a grotesque copy of God, Christ, and the Spirit, close enough to fool almost everyone, demanding the very worship that belongs to the Lamb alone.

But look closer, and the imitation is already cracking. The beast conquers only by violence, dazzles only by trickery, and builds nothing that will last, because the One it is copying has already won, permanently, at the cross. Every altar it raises is hollow; every liturgy it stages is theater without substance, sound without a throne behind it. Here is a call for the endurance and faith of the saints: to know a false sanctuary when one meets it, and to keep worship for the One to whom it is due.
