If you picked this up out of curiosity — maybe a friend mentioned it, maybe you've just always wondered what's actually in that strange last book of the Bible with the dragons and the number 666 — welcome. You don't need any background in church or the Bible to start here. You don't need to already believe anything either. All that's asked of you is a willingness to read the actual story with fresh eyes, instead of the version pop culture has been selling for decades.

This is Revelation told as one continuous story, in plain, poetic language — from the Garden of Eden to a city coming down out of heaven — with no footnotes and no jargon. It's the fastest way in: about 10 minutes, start to finish, told straight through as a story rather than a puzzle to solve. Whenever a moment deserves a closer look, it links onward to a deeper walkthrough, so you never lose your way back to the main thread.

You can read it in one sitting, skim it, or come back chapter by chapter. Nobody's grading you on it.

**Best for:** a first honest look at a book that's usually misrepresented before anyone actually reads it — no assumed belief, no assumed background required.

**Production notes:** This version is written for skeptical or curious readers with no church background who are encountering Revelation for the first time, often already carrying the "doomsday scorecard" impression pop culture has attached to it. It keeps the same plain, poetic voice as the general version but opens with a short, honest acknowledgment of that reputation before the story begins, and occasionally pauses to connect a moment in the story to a modern skeptical question. It could pair well with an audio-narrated reading for listeners who'd rather have the story read to them than read it themselves.
