Now the same story is told again, pulled back to reveal the frame around it - a very different birth story, played out across the sky itself. A great sign appears in heaven: a woman clothed with the sun, the moon under her feet, a crown of twelve stars circling her head, the way a dreamer once saw the sun and moon and eleven stars bow down to him and understood, even then, that it meant a whole family. She is pregnant, crying out in the pain of labor and the agony of giving birth.

Then the sky tears open on a second sign, far worse: an enormous red dragon, seven heads crowned, ten horns - the horns and heads of every beast that ever rose dripping out of a churning sea to devour a kingdom, gathered now into one final shape - and its tail sweeps a third of the stars out of heaven and hurls them to the earth. It plants itself in front of the woman about to give birth, waiting to devour the child the instant he is born.

The child is born anyway - a son who will rule every nation with a rod of iron - and before the dragon's jaws can close, the boy is snatched up, safe, to God's own throne. The woman flees into the wilderness to a place prepared for her, to be nourished there twelve hundred and sixty days, the way a whole people once fled into a wilderness and were carried on eagles' wings and fed with bread from the sky for exactly as long as they needed it.

War breaks out in heaven: Michael and his angels against the dragon and his, the same prince who once stood watch over a people while an empire's own angelic patron warred against him unseen - and the dragon loses. There is no place for him there anymore. He is hurled down to the earth, and a loud voice cries out: the accuser of the brothers is thrown down, the one who accused them day and night before our God, beaten by the blood of the Lamb and the plain testimony of people who refused to love their own lives even to the point of death.

Furious now, and knowing his time is short, he turns his rage on the woman, and when she is carried on wings out of his reach, he turns it instead on the rest of her offspring - everyone who keeps the commandments of God and holds to the testimony of Jesus.

Take heart anyway: bringing the dragon down was never given to you to do. It was finished in heaven before it ever reached you.
