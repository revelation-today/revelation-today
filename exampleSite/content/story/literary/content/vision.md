An old man on a rocky island ringed by sea, exiled there for a testimony he would not recant. The Lord's own day, ordinary as any other Sabbath in a life grown used to exile - until a voice cracks behind him like a trumpet, and the island stops being merely a prison.

He turns, and the vision breaks over him whole, image piling on image the way they once piled on a prophet by the Kebar canal and on a seer by the Tigris: seven lampstands of gold, and standing among them one whose head and hair are white as wool, white as snow, eyes like flaming torches, feet like bronze glowing where it is refined in a furnace, a voice like the roar of many waters. A sword goes out of his mouth, sharp on both edges, and his face is the sun shining in its strength - not to be looked at, only endured, the way glory once could only be endured from the cleft of a rock. Seven stars rest in his right hand.

John does not remain standing. Something in him gives way, exactly as it always gives way before this, and he falls as a dead man falls, as a man once fell into a deep sleep at the edge of a vision by a great river.

Then a hand, and a voice that undoes the fear before it finishes forming: "Do not be afraid. I am the first and the last, the living one. I died, and see - I am alive forever and ever, and I hold the keys of Death and of Sheol."

Sunrise breaks through the words: it is true. The kingdom promised long ago, the one whose government would rest on a single shoulder and whose peace would have no end, has already arrived - and you, yes you, have been made into it: a kingdom, priests to his God, the way a whole nation was once told at the foot of a mountain that it would be a kingdom of priests, a holy nation, if only it would listen.

One thing must be said before the singing starts. This vision goes straight to the marrow of what the Church exists for: to burn gold as those lampstands burn, fed not on her own oil but kept alight by the one who stands, even now, among the lamps, as he once walked among lamps of another kind in a tent in the wilderness. Nothing more is asked of her. Nothing less will do.

So: is she ready?
