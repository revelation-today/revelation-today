And then: a wedding. A new heaven and a new earth, the first heaven and the first earth having passed away, and the sea - the old restless deep, the churning source of every beast that ever rose to devour a people - is no more. A holy city, new Jerusalem, comes down out of heaven from God, dressed like a bride adorned for her husband, and a loud voice from the throne says what everyone has been waiting the whole story to hear: Look - the dwelling place of God is with humankind, and he will dwell with them, and they will be his peoples, and God himself will be with them and be their God. He will wipe every tear from their eyes; there will be no more death, no mourning, no crying, no pain, because the former things have passed away.

The city itself is almost too much to describe: a wall of jasper, the city itself pure gold, clear as glass, its foundations adorned with every kind of jewel there is, twelve gates and each one carved from a single pearl. There is no temple anywhere in it, because the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb are its temple - the whole architecture of tent and courts and veil, finished and folded away, because the thing it always pointed toward has finally arrived. There is no sun and no moon, because they are simply not needed anymore: the glory of God is its light, the Lamb is its lamp - the sun will no longer be your light by day, nor the moon your brightness by night, but the Lord will be your everlasting light - and the gates never close, because there is no more night left to shut them against, and the nations will walk by its light.

Through the middle of it runs a river of the water of life, clear as crystal, flowing from the throne of God and of the Lamb, and on either bank a tree of life bearing twelve kinds of fruit, yielding its fruit every month, its leaves for the healing of the nations - the same tree once guarded by cherubim and a sword turning every way, no longer guarded from anyone, and the same river once measured ankle-deep, then knee-deep, then a river no one could cross, flowing out from under a threshold to make the salt sea fresh and everything live wherever it goes.

The curse - the one first spoken over cursed ground and thorns, all the way back in a different garden - is gone for good. There will be no more curse. God's people will see his face at last, wear his name on their foreheads, and reign with him forever and ever.

The bride and the groom, together at last, for good. And now that you know the story - go and live like you believe it.
