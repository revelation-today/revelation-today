Look at the dragon's scoreboard so far. He struck at the child and missed. He fought to keep his place among the stars and was thrown down to the earth instead. Long before either of those, he set himself against a whole nation carried once out of a furnace of iron, and failed at that too - four kingdoms rose against her in their season, lion and bear and leopard and a fourth beast, terrifying and dreadful, iron teeth, ten horns on its head, and still she was not finished.

So now he stands on the shore of the sea and calls up something new: a beast rising out of the water, ten horns, seven heads, each horn crowned, blasphemous names written on its heads - leopard-bodied, bear-footed, lion-mouthed, every beast that ever climbed dripping out of that churning water folded together into one final shape, and the dragon gives it his own power and his own throne and great authority. One of its heads seems slain and yet lives, a mockery of the wound that actually healed something, and the whole earth follows it in wonder, asking, Who is like the beast, who can make war against it? - the very question a whole nation once sang about someone else entirely, at the edge of a sea, after real water had actually closed over real horses and real riders.

Then a second beast climbs up out of the earth, gentle-horned as a lamb, and speaks like the dragon. It works great signs, even calling fire down from heaven in front of everyone, the way fire was once called down from heaven to prove which God was real, except now the sign proves nothing but is believed anyway. It makes an image to be worshiped and breathes into it, and marks everyone, small and great, rich and poor, free and slave, with a name or a number on the right hand or the forehead - a grotesque copy of the very seal already resting on the foreheads of the ones who serve the true King.

Together the two beasts are a blasphemous liturgy: a false trinity, dragon for father, sea-beast for son, earth-beast for spirit, close enough to fool almost everyone into bowing. But look closer, and the imitation is already cracking at every seam. It conquers only by the sword, dazzles only by counterfeit fire, and builds nothing that will outlast even a single generation - because the One it is copying has already won, and did not need a sword, a mark, or a lie to do it.
