Those who had conquered the beast stand on a sea of glass mixed with fire, harps in hand, and sing the song of Moses, the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb - the same song once sung on the far shore of a sea that had just closed over an army, horse and rider thrown into it, great and marvelous are your deeds, Lord God the Almighty, just and true are your ways.

Then seven angels come out of the temple of the tent of witness in heaven, carrying seven golden bowls full of the wrath of God, who lives forever and ever - and the temple fills with smoke from the glory of God, the way a temple once filled with smoke so thick the priests had to leave, and no one can enter until the seven plagues are finished.

Now God's zeal pours out like something long held back and finally released - bowl after bowl, wave after wave, an old pattern come round a final time. Foul, painful sores break out, the way sores once broke out on a stubborn nation's cattle and its own skin. The sea turns to blood, like the blood of a dead man, and every living thing in it dies, as a river once turned to blood, undrinkable, its fish dead and floating. The rivers and springs turn to blood too, and an angel of the waters says: you are just, for they poured out the blood of saints and prophets, and you have given them blood to drink; it is what they deserve. The sun is given power to scorch people with fire, and they curse God rather than repent - the same refusal to turn that once outlasted every plague sent against the same stubbornness. The beast's throne is plunged into darkness, and people gnaw their tongues in pain, a darkness that can be felt exactly as it once could.

The great river is dried up, to prepare the way for the kings from the sunrise - a highway made through the water the way one was once made through a sea and, centuries later, through another river's flood-flow. And out of the mouths of the dragon and the beast and the false prophet come three unclean spirits like frogs, gathering the kings of the whole world to the great day of battle, to the place called Armageddon - the field below the mountain where kings had gathered for slaughter before, more than once, and always for the same result.

They come certain of victory. They are already finished. They just do not know it yet.
