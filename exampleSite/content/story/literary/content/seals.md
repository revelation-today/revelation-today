The seals break, one by one, and each breaking lets something off its chain.

First a white horse gallops out, a rider with a bow, handed a crown, already bent on conquest. For one heartbeat it could pass for victory. It is not: the true King is not the only one who wants a throne, and this rider has only borrowed the color of his horse to sell a lie in its shadow.

Strip the costume away, and violence is what remains. Behind the white horse comes a blood-red one, and peace is torn off the earth like a scab - people turning on people with drawn swords. Behind the red horse comes a black one, its rider holding a pair of scales - the same scales a besieged city once used to weigh out bread by the ounce and water by the cup, until the very measuring became a kind of famine: a day's wages for a day's bread, oil and wine kept back for whoever can still afford them. Behind the black horse comes a horse the color of ash, and its rider's name is Death, with the grave riding at his heels the way it always has, close as breath.

Conquest gives way to war, war to famine, and famine to death.

Then the seal breaks that reveals not armies but an altar, and under it the souls of those killed for staying loyal, crying out with one voice that carries back to the first blood ever spilled on open ground, crying out for vengeance before it had words: how long, O Lord, holy and true, until you judge the earth and avenge our blood? They are not silenced for asking. They are given white robes, and told to wait, just a little longer, until the number is complete.

The sky itself answers next: the sun goes black as sackcloth of hair, the moon turns to blood, the stars fall like fruit shaken loose when a fig tree is caught in a gale, and the sky itself splits and rolls back like a scroll being wound shut. Every rank of the earth, kings and slaves alike, calls out to the mountains and the rocks to fall on them and hide them from the face of the one on the throne - the same face that once could not be looked at and lived, now impossible, for entirely different reasons, to keep looking away from.

The world is already drowning in what it has brought on itself, and the Church stands in the middle of the wreckage, crying out too, with exactly the same question, and exactly the same right to ask it.
