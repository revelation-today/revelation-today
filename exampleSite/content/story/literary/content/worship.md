So it falls to you, to all of us, to rise and make ready for what is coming. But how does anyone make ready for something of this size?

Look up, he says. Not a strategy - a door standing open in heaven, the way a door once opened over a plain of dry bones and over a prophet's head by a foreign river. Through it, a throne, and around the throne everything and everyone finding, at last, its true place: four living creatures, faced like a lion, an ox, a man, an eagle, full of eyes in front and behind, the same four that once rode on wheels within wheels beside a chariot of fire and never turned as they went. They do not rest, day or night, and what they cry has been cried before: Holy, holy, holy, until the very sound of it once filled a temple with smoke and shook its foundations.

Do not mistake this room for an easy comfort. Twenty-four elders lay their own crowns down before the throne. Lightning and thunder come from it, and seven torches of fire burn before it, and a sea of glass like crystal stretches out in front, still as the one Israel once walked through on dry ground and watched close again over an army. Someone sits enthroned, ancient, robed in light, and books lie open before him, ledgers a king once saw opened at the great assize, when the court sat and the books were opened and one like a son of man was brought near.

A scroll appears too, sealed with seven seals, sealed the way another scroll was once told to be sealed until the time of the end - and no one is found worthy to open it, not in heaven, not on earth, not under the earth, and John weeps hard, because it seems the story might simply end unread.

Then: do not weep. The Lion of Judah's tribe, the Root of David, has conquered, and is worthy to open the scroll. John looks for a lion and finds a Lamb instead, standing as though slaughtered, standing though slain, seven horns and seven eyes, and every living thing in heaven and on earth and under it falls down before him with harps and golden bowls of incense, singing a new song: worthy is the Lamb.

He is the hero of this whole story - worthy, when no one else was found worthy, to open what needed opening and rule what needed ruling. Follow him once through that open door, and there is no going back to who you were before.
