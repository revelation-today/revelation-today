The first strike has failed. Hard times can crack open just about anything in a man - except a heart already set on the wrong thing.

A mighty angel comes down wrapped in a cloud, a rainbow over his head, his face like the sun, his legs like pillars of fire, one foot planted on the sea and one on the land, and he calls out in a voice like a lion roaring, and seven thunders answer in their own voices. John is about to write down what they said, and is told instead: seal it up, do not write it - the opposite instruction given once to a prophet told to seal his scroll and close it until the time of the end. Some things are still not for the telling.

Then the angel lifts his hand to heaven and swears by the one who lives forever and ever, who created heaven and what is in it, the earth and what is in it, the sea and what is in it: there will be no more delay. In the days when the seventh trumpet is about to sound, the mystery of God will be finished, just as he announced to his servants the prophets - the whole long argument of everyone who ever spoke for God, coming due at last.

A small scroll lies open in the angel's hand, and John is told to take it and eat it. It will be sweet as honey in his mouth, and bitter in his stomach once it is down - the same taste, the same order, given once to a man told to open his mouth and eat what was handed him, a scroll written on the front and on the back with words of lament and mourning and woe, sweet in the mouth as honey, bitter for everything it would then have to be said aloud.

This is not another disaster laid on top of the others already piling up. It is a revelation, plain and unavoidable: something true about God himself, something that will not stay comfortable once it is fully digested, and it must be prophesied again over many peoples and nations and tongues and kings. Judgment alone could not break through where this small, sweet, hard scroll now can. What could not be forced from the outside is about to be spoken from the inside instead.
