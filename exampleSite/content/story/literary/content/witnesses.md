Step past the curtain, and both worlds show themselves as they truly are. A reed like a measuring rod is handed to John, and a voice says: rise and measure the temple of God and the altar and those who worship there - the same instruction once given a man in a vision, led round a future temple by someone with a measuring line in his hand, cubit by cubit. But leave out the outer court; it is given over to the nations, to be trampled forty-two months. The temple is not a place you earn your way into. It is where loyalty either shows itself, or does not.

Two witnesses are given power to prophesy for those twelve hundred and sixty days, dressed in sackcloth, and they are called two olive trees and two lampstands standing before the Lord of the whole earth, the way a priest and a governor were once shown as two anointed ones flanking a lamp fed by an endless supply of oil. They carry old powers with them: fire from their mouths that devours their enemies, the sky shut so no rain falls in the days of their prophesying, and water turned to blood, and the earth struck with every plague as often as they wish - the two great signs, drought and blood, that once rode with a prophet who called down fire and a prophet who once turned a river to something undrinkable.

When their testimony is finished - not cut short, finished - the beast that comes up from the abyss makes war on them and kills them, and their bodies lie unburied in the street of the great city, mystically called Sodom and Egypt, where also their Lord was crucified, while the peoples of the earth gaze at them and celebrate and send each other gifts, because these two had tormented everyone who lives on the earth.

Three and a half days it looks like total defeat, bodies left to rot the way bodies were once left unburied as the worst curse a covenant could name. Then the breath of life from God enters them, and they stand on their feet, and great fear falls on everyone watching, and a loud voice from heaven says, Come up here, and they go up into heaven in a cloud while their enemies watch.

That is exactly the pattern the Church herself is asked to live: not a victory that skips over the grave, but one that walks straight through it and comes out standing on the other side, in full view of the very people who were sure it was over.
