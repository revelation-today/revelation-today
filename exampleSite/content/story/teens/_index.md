This isn't the watered-down version. Revelation is about people your age getting frozen out, laughed at, and threatened for refusing to fake it — and about a King who never once asked them to fake it either. No rapture charts, no code-cracking, no scary-movie gimmicks. Just the real story: real pressure, real cost, real ending. Ten minutes, start to finish, and you'll never read "the end of the world" the same way again.

**Best for:** a first, unfiltered pass through the book — read it before anyone hands you a diagram.

## Production notes

Intended for readers roughly age 13-18. Tone: YA-novel register — sharper and more visceral than a general-audience retelling, unflinching about persecution, martyrdom, and social/economic exclusion, but never gratuitous. Uses restrained direct-address moments to draw explicit parallels to modern teen experience (peer pressure, social media conformity, being the "weird one" for having convictions), without turning the retelling into a sermon.
