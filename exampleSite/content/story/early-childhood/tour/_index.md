Here is the story of Revelation, told just for you.

# In the beginning

God made everything. The sky, the sea, the animals, and people. He made a garden called Eden. He made Adam and Eve. Everything was good.

But Adam and Eve did not listen to God. They believed a lie, and that made the whole world sad.

God did not stop loving them. He had a plan. He would send someone to fix everything and make it all good again.

# God's people

God picked a friend named Abraham and grew his family into a whole nation, called Israel. God loved them so much.

But Israel forgot God too, just like Adam did. Still, God did not give up. He promised: "One day, I will send a King. His kingdom will never end, and it will be for everyone."

# John sees Jesus

There was a man named John. He loved Jesus so much that people sent him far away, all alone, to a little island.

One day, John heard a big voice, loud like a trumpet. He turned around — and there was Jesus! Jesus was so bright and so strong that John fell down in surprise.

But Jesus was gentle. He put his hand on John and said, "Don't be afraid. I was dead, but look — I am alive forever! I win over death."

Jesus wins, and he loves us.

# Letters to friends

Jesus wrote letters to his churches, like a teacher writing notes to her class.

To some he said, "I am so proud of you." To some he said, "I love you, but let's do better." He was gentle with every single one, and he never stopped loving them, even when they made mistakes.

# The best room ever

Then John saw heaven. It was the most beautiful room ever, with a shining throne and everyone singing.

Right in the middle stood a Lamb. This Lamb was Jesus. Everyone sang because Jesus is the hero of the whole story — the only one strong enough and good enough to make everything right.

Jesus wins, and he loves us.

# The bad guy tries something scary

Down on earth, some scary things started to happen. A pretend king tried to trick people. There was fighting, and not enough food to go around.

But gently, God put his mark on all his people, like a special sticker, so everyone would know: these ones are mine. Nothing could ever take them away from him.

Jesus wins, and he loves us.

# Being brave

Loud trumpets blew, and the world got shaky and hard. But God's people did not need to be scared, because they belonged to God.

Then an angel gave John a little scroll to eat. It tasted sweet. It was a beautiful secret: God's story was true, and it was going to come out right in the end.

# Two brave helpers, and a baby who wins

Two brave helpers told everyone about Jesus. The bad guys tried to stop them, and for a little while it looked like the bad guys had won. Then — surprise! — the helpers stood right back up again, because that is how Jesus's story always goes.

Next, John saw a mommy and her new baby. A big dragon tried something scary — he wanted to hurt the baby. But God swooped the baby right up to heaven, safe and sound! The dragon fought in heaven, and he lost. Down he went, far away.

The dragon tried more scary tricks, with two make-believe monsters. But they were only pretend, and pretend things can never really win.

Jesus wins, and he loves us.

# Singing on the sea of glass

God's people stood on a sea that sparkled like glass, and they sang and sang, because they had stayed close to Jesus.

The bad guy's plans all fell apart, one by one, like sandcastles washing away in the rain.

Jesus wins, and he loves us.

# The pretend queen

There was a pretend queen who dressed up fancy and sparkly. She tried to trick people into following her instead of Jesus.

But her tricks did not work. Everything false and fake came falling down, and the way was finally clear for the real King.

# The wedding and the new home

At last, it was time for the best part. A beautiful city came down from heaven, dressed up like a bride on her wedding day.

A big, warm voice said, "Look! I will live with my people now. I will wipe away every tear. No more crying. No more owies. No more goodbyes — ever again."

The city sparkled like jewels, with gates made of pearl and streets like gold. A river of clear water ran right through the middle, with trees that never stopped growing yummy fruit.

Everybody who loves Jesus gets to live there, safe and happy, forever and ever. Nobody will ever have to say goodbye again.

Jesus wins, and he loves us. Always and forever.
