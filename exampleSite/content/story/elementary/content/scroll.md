Here's the thing about hard times: they can crack open almost anything in this world — except a heart that's already decided what it wants. The first strike didn't work. So what happens next isn't another disaster. It's something completely different.

A mighty angel comes down from heaven wrapped in a cloud, a rainbow over his head, his face shining like the sun and his legs like pillars of fire. He plants one foot on the sea and one on the land, like he owns the whole world — because he does. In his hand is a little scroll, lying open.

A voice from heaven tells John: go take that scroll and eat it.

So he does. And it's exactly like he was told it would be: sweet as honey in his mouth — and then his stomach turns sour.

This is what real truth about God is like. It's wonderful to hear, and hard to carry. It's not a quick fix. It's not a magic trick that solves everything instantly. It's something you have to take in and let change you, slowly, from the inside — sweet and hard at the same time.

Judgment alone couldn't crack open a single stubborn heart. But this — truth, really understood, really swallowed — this can.
