Now the story rewinds, and the curtain pulls back to show what was really happening behind the scenes all along.

A sign appears in the sky: a woman clothed with the sun, the moon under her feet, and a crown of twelve stars circling her head. She's pregnant, and crying out in the pain of having a baby.

Then the sky splits open again, and something monstrous appears across from her: an enormous red dragon with seven crowned heads and ten horns, and a tail so massive it sweeps a third of the stars right out of the sky and flings them to earth. He plants himself in front of the woman like a predator, waiting. The second her baby is born, he means to swallow the child whole.

The baby is born anyway — a son who will one day rule every nation on earth — and before the dragon's jaws can even close, the child is snatched up to safety, all the way to God's own throne.

War breaks out in heaven. Michael and his angels against the dragon and his — and the dragon loses, badly. There's no room left for him up there. He gets hurled down to earth, and a voice rings out: he's beaten, thrown down by the blood of the Lamb and the plain courage of people who wouldn't save their own skin just to escape him.

Furious, and running out of time, the dragon turns his rage on the woman — and when he can't reach her, he goes after everyone who belongs to her family instead.

Here's the good news buried inside all that danger: beating the dragon was never your job. Jesus already did that part, up in heaven, before the fight ever reached down to you.
