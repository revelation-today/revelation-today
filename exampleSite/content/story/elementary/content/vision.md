John was in trouble — the kind of trouble that gets you sent away forever.

He'd refused to bow down to anyone except Jesus, and for that, his enemies shipped him off to Patmos: a lonely, rocky island stuck out in the middle of the sea. No friends. No church. No way home. If you wanted to get rid of someone quietly, this is exactly what you'd do.

It should have been the end of John's story. Instead, it was the beginning of the biggest adventure anyone has ever had.

One ordinary Sunday, a voice cracked behind him like a whip of thunder. John spun around — and what he saw nearly knocked him flat. Seven golden lamps floated in the air, and standing right in the middle of them was someone almost too bright to look at. His eyes blazed like fire. His feet glowed like metal pulled straight from a furnace. His voice roared like a waterfall crashing over rocks. And a sword, sharp on both edges, seemed to shine right out of his mouth.

John didn't just kneel. He dropped, like someone had switched him off.

Then he felt a hand on his shoulder — steady, warm, not scary at all.

"Don't be afraid," the voice said. "I am the First and the Last. I died — and look, I'm alive forever! I hold the keys to Death itself."

It was Jesus.

And he had news that changed everything: the kingdom everyone had been hoping for had already begun. John — exiled, alone, forgotten by the world — was actually royalty in it.

But first, Jesus had seven letters to send. He needed to know: were his people ready to shine?
