Picture the two bravest, most stubborn heroes you can imagine — two witnesses, standing right in the middle of a hostile city, telling the truth about Jesus no matter what it costs them. They're not backed by an army. They're not protected by walls. All they have is the truth, and they refuse to stop saying it.

For years, nothing can touch them. Fire seems to guard them against anyone who tries to hurt them. They can shut up the sky so it doesn't rain. They seem completely unstoppable.

Then, finally, the beast rises up out of the pit and does what nothing else could: it kills them. Their bodies lie in the street, and the whole world — genuinely, gleefully — throws a party. People celebrate. They send each other gifts, because these two brave witnesses had been such an irritation, and now, finally, they're gone for good.

For three and a half days, it looks like the villains have won completely. Total defeat. Game over.

Then breath enters the witnesses again. They stand up on their own feet, right in front of everyone who came to gloat — and terror replaces celebration in an instant. A voice from heaven calls, "Come up here," and they rise into the sky in a cloud, while their enemies watch, stunned.

This is the pattern every one of Jesus' followers is asked to live: not a victory that skips around suffering, but one that walks straight through the worst of it — and comes out the other side, standing.
