Here's a secret about this whole adventure: it was over long before it looked that way. The whole time everyone thought the underdogs were losing — exiled, outnumbered, even killed for staying loyal — they were actually reigning. Not with armies or thrones anyone could see. With something quieter and stronger: loyalty that never broke.

The dragon gets bound and locked away, unable to trick the nations for a thousand years, while everyone who stayed faithful reigns instead. It's the world turned right-side up at last — the ones who looked weakest the whole story through turn out to have been the real royalty all along.

When the dragon gets loose one final time, he tries one last desperate attack — and it fails instantly, like it was never really a threat at all. He's thrown down for good this time, no more comebacks, no more scoreboard to check. It's finished.

Then comes the last, biggest moment: every person who has ever lived stands in front of God's throne, and the record of everything gets opened. This is the final reckoning — the moment it's decided forever who belongs with God and who, all along, chose not to.

No more waiting. No more wondering how the story ends. It's decided, for good.
