And then: a wedding.

A whole city comes down out of heaven, dressed like a bride on the happiest day of her life, and a voice booms out from the throne — the words this entire adventure has been racing toward from the very first page: "Look! God's home is now among his people. He will live with them. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death, no more sadness, no more crying, no more pain — because all of that has already passed away, for good."

The city itself is almost too much to describe. Walls made of jasper. Streets of gold so pure they look like glass. Foundations set with every color of jewel imaginable. Twelve gates, each one carved from a single giant pearl, and they never, ever close — because there's no more night left to shut them against.

There's no temple anywhere in this city, because God himself and the Lamb are the temple now. There's no need for the sun or the moon either, because the glory of God lights up everything, with the Lamb as the lamp.

Right through the middle of it all runs a river, clear as crystal, flowing straight from God's own throne. On either side stands a tree bursting with fruit every single month, its leaves healing every nation that has ever bled or suffered.

The curse that started it all, all the way back in that very first garden, is gone. Completely. Forever. God's people will see his face at last, wear his name proudly, and reign with him without end.

The bride and the groom, together for good. God moves in with his people — no more distance left between them, ever again.

And now that you know how the story ends — go and live like you already believe it.
