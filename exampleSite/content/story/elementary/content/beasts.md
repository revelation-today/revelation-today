Let's check the dragon's scoreboard so far. He tried to kill Jesus — failed. He tried to keep his place in heaven — thrown out. He tried to wipe out God's people — failed again.

Losing three times in a row would stop most villains. Not this one. He just changes tactics. He calls up two new monsters to do his dirty work.

The first beast crawls up out of the sea: all muscle, all menace, covered in crowns it stole and never earned. It roars and demands that everyone bow down and worship it instead of God. Anyone who refuses gets crushed.

The second beast creeps up out of the earth. This one doesn't look scary at all — it even has horns like a lamb, gentle-looking, harmless. But when it opens its mouth, it sounds exactly like the dragon. It works fake miracles and tricks people into trusting the first beast, marking anyone who falls for it.

Here's the trick hiding underneath the trick: put the dragon and his two beasts together, and you get a cheap knockoff of God, Jesus, and the Holy Spirit — close enough to fool almost anybody at first glance.

But look closer, and the copy is already falling apart. This gang can only win by hurting people and lying to them. They can't build one single thing that actually lasts. Meanwhile, the real King they're copying has already won — permanently, and for good.
