Now something that's been building the whole story finally breaks loose: seven angels carry seven bowls, full to the brim, and pour them out over the earth, one after another. This is God, finally setting right everything that's been wrong for so long.

Each bowl lands exactly where it's aimed. Painful sores strike everyone who chose the beast's mark over God. The sea turns to blood. The rivers turn to blood. The sun blazes hotter, scorching everyone — and instead of turning back to God, people just shout curses at him instead. Darkness falls over the beast's whole kingdom, and still, nobody says sorry.

The final bowl empties out over the air itself, and a voice thunders from the throne: "It is done."

Meanwhile, the kings of the whole earth gather their armies for one last stand, at a place with a name that has become a byword for total disaster: Armageddon. They march in, certain they're about to win the biggest battle in history.

Here's the twist nobody on that battlefield can see yet: they're already finished. The war was decided a long time ago, on a hill outside Jerusalem, at a cross. These armies just haven't found out yet.
