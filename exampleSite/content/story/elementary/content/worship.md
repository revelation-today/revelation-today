So how do you get ready for the biggest adventure of your life? Jesus has one answer: look up.

A door opens in the sky, and John is pulled straight through it into the throne room of heaven itself — the one place where everything and everyone finally lines up in its true place. The door never closes. It's always open, for anyone brave enough to walk through.

Inside, it's dazzling: light like jewels, thunder rolling from the throne, creatures singing "Holy, holy, holy" without ever stopping, and rows of crowned elders who keep taking their crowns off and laying them down at God's feet, again and again.

That's the catch about this throne room — it isn't a quick fix or a magic trick. Walking in means giving everything, your whole self, no keeping anything back.

Then someone steps forward that everyone in the room has been waiting for: a scroll needs opening, a plan needs unlocking, and no one anywhere is found worthy to do it. John starts to cry — until someone tells him to look.

Standing right at the center of the throne is a Lamb — and he looks like he's been through the worst battle imaginable and won anyway. He's the one. The true hero of this whole story, strong enough to open what needs opening, brave enough to have already paid the price.

Once you see him and follow him through that door, you're never quite the same person again.
