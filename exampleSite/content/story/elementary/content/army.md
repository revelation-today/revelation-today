Right before things get even harder, something amazing happens: God stops everything and marks his own people. A seal, right on their foreheads — not something you can always see with your eyes, but real all the same. It means: this one is mine. Nothing gets through to them that I haven't allowed.

Thousands and thousands are marked — a number so huge it's really a way of saying everyone who belongs to God, from every tribe, every corner of the earth. Then John looks again, and the crowd is even bigger than that: an uncountable army, from every nation and language there is, standing in front of the throne, wearing white, waving palm branches like it's already a party.

Here's the strange part: this is God's army — the toughest, bravest fighting force there ever will be — and not one of them is carrying a sword.

Their weapons are different. Worship that never quits. Loyalty that doesn't crack, no matter what the enemy throws at them. They've come through "the great trouble," as the elders call it, and they came through it not by fighting back with fists, but by refusing to let go of Jesus.

Whatever storms come next, one thing about these underdogs is already settled forever: everyone will know exactly whose side they're on.
