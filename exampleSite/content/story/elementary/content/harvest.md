Jesus already won the real battle — back at the cross — and everyone who's kept faith with him through all of this has been part of that victory the whole time. Now it starts to show. The enemy's forces, which looked so terrifying a moment ago, scatter like smoke in the wind.

On what looks like a sea of glass mixed with fire, a huge crowd stands and sings — the ones who never gave in, never took the easy way out, never bowed to the beast even when it would have been simpler. A hundred and forty-four thousand of them stand with the Lamb on Mount Zion, singing a song nobody else can learn, because nobody else lived it. Their only qualification for being there was staying loyal, all the way through.

But not everyone gets to sing this song.

An angel calls out with a warning, loud enough for the whole earth to hear: the time has come. Two sickles swing down across the earth, like at harvest time. One gathers in a harvest of good grain — everyone who stayed loyal to the very end. The other gathers grapes for judgment, for everyone who played it safe and sided with the beast to dodge the cost.

This is the moment every choice made throughout the whole story finally, unavoidably, counts.
