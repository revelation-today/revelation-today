The Lamb starts breaking seals on the scroll, one at a time — and each one lets something loose on the world.

The first seal breaks, and a white horse gallops out. Its rider carries a bow and wears a crown, and for one second, it looks like a win. It isn't. This rider is a fake, a copycat dressed in the color of the true King's horse, out to steal loyalty that isn't his.

Strip the costume away, and here's what's really underneath: trouble. A blood-red horse charges out next, and peace gets ripped off the earth like a bandage — people turning on people. Then a black horse, its rider holding a scale, weighing out food that's suddenly too expensive for regular people to afford. And last, a horse the color of ash, ridden by Death himself, with the grave trailing right behind him.

One trouble leads to the next: conquest, then war, then hunger, then death.

The fifth seal breaks, and it's not an army this time — it's an altar, and underneath it are the souls of people who stayed loyal to Jesus no matter the cost, even when it cost them their lives. They call out together: How long, Lord, until you make this right? They're told: soon — just wait a little longer.

Then the sky itself answers back. The sun goes dark. The moon turns blood-red. Stars fall like fruit shaken loose from a tree, and the sky peels back like a scroll rolling shut. The world is drowning in the mess it made — and Jesus' people are right there in the middle of it, waiting for what happens next.
