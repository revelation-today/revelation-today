One deception still waits to be unmasked before the story can end - and unmasking it reveals what this was always about: not simply defeating evil, but God binding himself to his people forever, like a marriage.

Here is the deception: a woman, dazzling, draped in jewels that should have belonged to the bride, promising prosperity to anyone who will drink from her cup. Pull back the dress, though, and there is the beast underneath, teeth and all. She is no bride. She is a harlot - and plenty inside the Church itself have fallen for the act, because her cup is genuinely, seductively full: gold, pearls, easy wealth for anyone willing to trade loyalty for access to her markets.

Then the vision does something remarkable: it makes her victims testify against her, in their own words. The merchants of the earth weep - not for the people her economy consumed, but for the profits it stopped generating. Their cargo list, read aloud in mourning, runs through gold and silk and spice and fine wood and ivory, on and on, before ending, almost as an afterthought, with "human souls." That single detail unmasks the entire system: an economy so thoroughly organized around extraction that it had learned to price a human being as one more line item among the ivory and the wine. This is not God resenting anyone's wealth as such. It is exploitation - treating people as commodities, mourning lost profit louder than lost lives - finally being named out loud, in public, where the people it consumed can finally hear it named.

Her final performance looks flawless. It is not. The very powers the devil sends to destroy the Church turn instead and tear the harlot apart, strip her, burn her - his own weapon, used against him. This is not an act of violence God has to import from outside and inflict on her. Systems built on exploitation and violence have always, eventually, turned on the people who profited from them too, because an economy that treats everything as consumable eventually consumes its own architects as well. The vision does not need to invent Babylon's collapse. It only needs to show the collapse that exploitation was always going to produce, arriving on schedule, in full view of everyone she once seduced.

The road is finally clear to finish him for good - and the armies gathered against the rider on the white horse are dealt with by nothing more than the word from his mouth, because the only weapon violence has ever really had was fear, and fear has nothing left to work with once its victims stop believing the performance.
