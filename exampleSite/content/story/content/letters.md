Let's look at the seven churches.

We start with Ephesus, the hard-working church. They work, endure, and keep the teachings of Jesus - but the main thing, love, the reason why they exist, is missing. This church has no purpose, and because of this, it would be better if she did not exist!

The next church, Smyrna, is very different. This small town is overwhelmed by an ungodly society; the church is persecuted and hated. Yet it is one of only two churches that receive nothing but praise from Jesus.

Pergamon is under high pressure too, since the throne of Satan itself is there. They endure a great deal - even martyrdom - but they allow wrong teaching to lead people away from God.

In Thyatira things are worse still: here the trouble comes from the leadership itself.

Sardis is not much better. They think they can manage everything without God's help, and they nearly always completely fail.

Philadelphia also receives only praise from Jesus. Yet this is still no easy church to belong to, since on top of everything else, its members are financially exhausted.

That leaves Laodicea, the only church that receives no praise at all - because the church is very impressed with itself, though Jesus is not. Still, he does not give up on them. Instead he gives loving advice, warnings, and promises.
