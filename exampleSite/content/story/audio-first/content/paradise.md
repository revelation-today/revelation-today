And then: a wedding. A city comes down out of heaven, dressed like a bride on her wedding day. A voice from the throne says what everyone has been waiting the whole story to hear: "Look — God's home is now among his people. He will live with them. He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death, no more mourning, no more crying, no more pain, because all of that has already passed away."

The city itself is almost too much to describe. Walls of jasper. Streets of gold, so pure it looks like glass. Foundations set with every color of jewel. Twelve gates, each one carved from a single pearl. There is no temple anywhere in it, because God himself, and the Lamb, are the temple now. There is no sun and no moon, because they are simply not needed anymore. The glory of God is the light. The Lamb is the lamp. And the gates never close, because there is no more night left to shut them against.

Through the middle of the city runs a river, clear as crystal, flowing straight from the throne of God. A tree stands on either bank, bearing fruit every month, with leaves for healing every nation that ever bled. The curse — the one that started all the way back in a different garden — is gone for good. God's people will see his face at last. They will wear his name. They will reign with him forever.

The bride and the groom, together at last, for good. God himself moves in with his people. There is no more distance left to close.

And now that you know the story — go and live like you believe it.
