Picture the scene. An old man is alone on a rocky island out in the sea. He was exiled there for refusing to bow to anyone but Jesus. It is a Sunday morning, like any other — until a voice cracks behind him like a trumpet, and everything changes.

He turns around, and the vision breaks over him all at once. Seven gold lampstands burn in the air. Someone stands among them — impossible to describe, impossible to look away from. His eyes are like fire. His feet glow like bronze pulled from a furnace. His voice roars like a waterfall. His face is too bright to look at, like staring straight into the sun. Seven stars rest in his right hand. A sword, sharp on both edges, comes from his mouth.

John does not stay standing. He falls down like a dead man.

Then a hand touches his shoulder. Words come that undo the fear before it even finishes forming. "Don't be afraid," the voice says. "I am the First and the Last, the Living One. I died — and look, I am alive forever. I hold the keys of Death and of the Grave itself."

The word breaks through like sunrise: it is true. The kingdom you have been waiting for has already arrived. And you — yes, you — are a king and a priest in it.

But before the celebration, one urgent thing has to be said. This vision cuts straight to the heart of what the Church is for. She is meant to shine, like those seven lampstands. She does not burn on her own fuel. She is held alight by the very one standing among them. Nothing more is asked of her. Nothing less will do.

So — is she ready?
