The seals break, one by one. Each one lets something loose.

First, a white horse gallops out. Its rider carries a bow. He is handed a crown. He is already bent on conquest. For one heartbeat, it looks like victory. It is not. Jesus is not the only one who wants your allegiance, and this rider is a counterfeit. He borrows the color of the true King's horse to sell a lie.

Strip away the costume, and violence is all that is left. Behind the white horse comes a blood-red one, and peace is torn off the earth like a scab. People turn on people with drawn swords. Behind the red horse comes a black one. Its rider holds a pair of scales. A day's wages buys a day's bread, while oil and wine stay safe for those who can still afford them. And behind the black horse comes a horse the color of ash. Its rider's name is Death, and the grave rides at his heels.

Conquest gives way to war. War gives way to famine. Famine gives way to death.

Then a seal breaks that reveals not armies, but an altar. Underneath it are the souls of those killed for staying loyal. They cry out with one voice: how long, Lord — holy and true — until you judge the earth and avenge our blood? They are told to wait, just a little longer.

The sky itself answers next. The sun goes black. The moon turns to blood. The stars fall like fruit shaken from a tree. The sky peels back like a scroll being rolled up. The world is already drowning in what it has brought on itself, and the Church stands in the middle of the wreckage, crying out too.
