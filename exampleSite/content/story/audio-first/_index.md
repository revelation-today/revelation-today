This is Revelation told as one continuous story, written specifically to be read aloud rather than read in silence. It is for listeners — anyone who takes in scripture by ear rather than by eye, whether blind, low-literacy, or simply someone who prefers to listen. Every sentence is shaped for spoken clarity: short, direct, easy to follow without ever seeing a word on a page. The content is the same rich, full story as the written version. Only the rhythm has changed, to fit the ear instead of the eye.

**Production notes**

If you are recording this as audio, a few practical notes. Pace it unhurried and clear — there is no need to rush; let each sentence land before the next one begins. A single narrator's voice is enough. No cast of characters is needed, since the prose itself carries every scene along without help. Light ambient music under key transitions — the opening of the throne room, or the arrival of the New Jerusalem — could work well, but it is entirely optional. The words are written to stand on their own, with or without it.
