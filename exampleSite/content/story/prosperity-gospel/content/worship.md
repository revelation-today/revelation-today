So it falls to you - to all of us - to get up and get ready for what is coming. But how do you prepare for something this big?

Jesus says: look up. The answer is not a strategy, not a formula, not a five-step plan for securing your future. It is a throne room. Heaven opens, and there it is: the place where everyone and everything finds its true position before God. The door stands open, always, to anyone willing to walk through it.

Do not mistake worship for an easy fix, though. It costs something. Twenty-four elders lay down their own crowns before the throne - real authority, real standing, surrendered rather than clutched. It means giving God every part of your life, without exception, including whatever security you had built for yourself before you got here.

And in that room, one figure stands out from all the rest: not a lion charging in on strength, though that is what everyone expected, but a Lamb, looking as if it had been slain. The most valuable thing in the whole throne room is a wound that never fully closed. That is what qualifies him to do what nothing and no one else could: he is the hero of this whole story - worthy, when no one else was found worthy in heaven or on earth or under it, to open what needed opening and rule what needed ruling.

The whole room erupts around him: elders, living creatures, thousands upon thousands of angels, and finally every creature in heaven and on earth and under the earth, all singing the same thing - worthy is the Lamb who was slain. Not the Lamb who avoided the cross. The one who walked into it and came out the other side holding the keys.

Once you follow him through that door, there is no going back to who you were before. You have seen where true worth actually comes from, and it was never a matter of what you could accumulate. It was always a matter of who was willing to be slain for you first.
