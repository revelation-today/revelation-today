Now the same story is told again, pulled back to reveal the frame around it - a very different Christmas story, played out across the sky itself. A woman appears, clothed with the sun, the moon under her feet, twelve stars circling her head like a crown. She is pregnant, and crying out in the pain of labor. Then the sky tears open again, and something far worse appears: an enormous red dragon, seven heads crowned, ten horns, a tail vast enough to sweep a third of the stars out of heaven and hurl them to the earth. He plants himself directly in front of the woman, waiting - the instant the child is born, he means to swallow him whole.

The child is born anyway - a son who will rule every nation with an iron scepter - and before the dragon's jaws can close, the boy is snatched up, safe, to God's own throne. War breaks out in heaven: Michael and his angels against the dragon and his, and the dragon loses. There is no place for him there anymore. He is hurled down to the earth, and a loud voice rings out: the accuser is thrown down at last, beaten by the blood of the Lamb and the plain testimony of people who refused to save their own lives to escape him.

Furious, and running out of time, the dragon turns on the woman - and when she slips out of his reach into the wilderness, carried there and cared for, he turns his rage on everyone who belongs to her instead: the rest of her offspring, "those who keep the commandments of God and hold to the testimony of Jesus."

Take heart anyway: defeating the dragon was never your job. Jesus already did that, in heaven, before it ever reached you. Look at the dragon's scoreboard so far: he tried to kill Jesus, and failed. He tried to keep his place in heaven, and was thrown out. He tried to wipe out Israel, and failed again. Every weapon he has left is a weapon that has already lost. Yours is simply to keep drawing people toward the one who beat him - even while the dragon, knowing exactly how little time he has left, rages harder than ever and reaches for new, more convincing disguises.
