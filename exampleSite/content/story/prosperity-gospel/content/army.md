Before it gets any worse, something happens: God's people are sealed. Marked. Claimed, in the middle of the storm, as unmistakably his - a hundred and forty-four thousand, drawn from every tribe, standing in for a number too complete to count. Notice what the mark is not. It is not a mark that guarantees a full harvest or an empty grave never touches your door. It is a mark that guarantees whose you are, no matter what the harvest looks like this year.

Set that mark next to the other one this book keeps circling back to later - a very different mark, stamped on the hand and the forehead, sold as the price of being able to buy or sell at all. Two marks, two kinds of security on offer. One is bought fresh every day from a system that owns you the moment you take it. The other was paid for once, in full, by someone else, and can never be taken back.

Then John looks again, and the scene widens past counting: a crowd no one could number, from every nation, tribe, people, and language, standing before the throne and before the Lamb, dressed in white, palm branches in their hands, crying out with a loud voice: salvation belongs to our God who sits on the throne, and to the Lamb. Someone asks who these people are and where they have come from. The answer: these are the ones who have come out of the great tribulation, who have washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb. Not people who avoided hardship. People who came through it, wearing the only currency that ever actually counted for anything there.

This is the army of God - though it fights like no army you have ever seen. No swords, no borrowed crowns, no counterfeit victories handed out to whoever conquers loudest. Just relentless worship and a loyalty that outlasts everything the enemy can throw at it, including hunger, including thirst, including every hard season this world can manufacture. "They will hunger no more, neither thirst anymore," the voice from the throne promises them - not because their circumstances never pinched, but because the Lamb himself will be their shepherd, and will lead them to springs of living water, and God will wipe away every tear from their eyes.

Whatever comes next, one thing is already settled: you know whose side you are on, and you know what kind of army you actually enlisted in - not the kind that wins by taking, but the kind that already has everything it needs in the one leading it.
