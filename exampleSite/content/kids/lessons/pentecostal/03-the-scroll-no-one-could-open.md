# The Scroll No One Could Open
### Revelation 5

## 1. Starting question: The unopenable box

Bring a locked box or a jar with a stuck lid. Let a few kids try to open it. Ask: "What would
it take to open this? Who do you think could do it?"

Say: "In today's story, there's something even harder to open than this box — a special
scroll in heaven that only one person in the whole universe can open."

## 2. The scroll and the search

In heaven, John sees a scroll with seven seals on it — sealed shut, super important, maybe even
God's whole plan for the world rolled up inside. A voice calls out, "Who is worthy to open it?"
And nobody answers. Not one angel, not one person anywhere, is able to. John is so sad about
this that he starts to cry.

## 3. The big surprise

Then someone tells John, "Don't cry! The Lion of Judah has won — he can open it!" John must
have thought, "A lion! Now that's someone strong enough!" But when he turns to look — it's not
a lion at all. It's a Lamb. And this Lamb looks like it has already been hurt very badly.

Ask: "Were you expecting a lion, and did you get a lamb instead? What do you think that means?"

Explain: The Lion and the Lamb are actually the same person — Jesus! He's called the Lion
because he's strong and wins. But he wins in a surprising way: not by fighting with weapons,
but by giving up his life for us on the cross, and then coming back to life. That's why he's
strong enough to open the scroll that nobody else could touch.

## 4. Why this matters

Sometimes we think winning means being the biggest, loudest, or toughest. Jesus shows us a
different way to win: through love, through giving himself for us. And because Jesus already
holds that scroll and already opened it, we don't have to be afraid of anything that happens
next in the story of Revelation — Jesus is already in charge.

## 5. Memory verse

"Do not weep! See, the Lion of the tribe of Judah... has triumphed." *(Revelation 5:5)*

## 6. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are strong enough to open every locked door and every hard
question in our lives. Thank you that you won by loving us, not by being scary. Help us trust
that you're already in charge of everything that happens. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The locked box/jar demonstration described above.

**Craft idea:** Kids make a small paper "scroll" (rolled paper tied with string) and write or
draw "Jesus already won" on the inside.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think everyone expected a lion but saw a lamb instead?
2. Can you think of a time when someone won by being kind instead of by being tough?
3. How does it feel to know Jesus is already in charge of everything?

**Song ideas:** A song about Jesus as the Lion and the Lamb, or about his victory on the cross.
