# The Christmas Story You've Never Heard
### Revelation 12

## 1. Starting question: The scariest villain

Ask: "What's the scariest villain you can think of from a movie or story?" Let a few kids
answer.

Say: "Today's Christmas story has a villain even scarier than that — a huge, angry dragon. And
it's actually about the birth of Jesus, told in a totally different way than you've heard
before."

## 2. The scene

In Revelation 12, John sees a woman about to have a baby, and a giant, terrifying red dragon
standing right in front of her, waiting to eat her baby the second it's born.

Ask: "If you were watching this happen, would you think the baby has any chance?"

## 3. Who's who

The dragon is Satan — the same enemy from the Garden of Eden, way back at the beginning of the
Bible. The baby is Jesus. And the woman represents God's people, waiting for hundreds and
hundreds of years for the Messiah to be born.

## 4. The biggest surprise

Just when it looks totally hopeless, the story says the baby is "snatched up to God and to his
throne." Jesus escapes every attack — as a baby escaping Herod, as a grown man resisting
temptation in the desert, and even dying on the cross and coming back to life. The dragon tries
everything. The dragon loses every single time.

## 5. Why this matters for us

This is the pattern all through the Bible: the one who looks weakest is actually the one who
wins. A helpless baby beats a giant dragon. A crucified Jesus rises from the dead. Sometimes
when we feel small, scared, or like we're losing, that doesn't mean we actually are. God's power
often looks like weakness right up until the moment it wins.

## 6. Memory verse

"She gave birth to a son... and her child was snatched up to God and to his throne."
*(Revelation 12:5, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that no matter how scary things look, you always win in the end. Help us
trust you even when we feel small or afraid. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The "scariest villain" question described above, connecting it to how big
and scary the dragon in Revelation 12 is.

**Craft idea:** Kids draw the scene: a small, safe baby on one side and a huge dragon on the
other, with an arrow showing the baby being lifted safely up to heaven.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think God let the story be told with such a scary dragon in it?
2. Can you think of a time you felt small or scared, and God helped you anyway?
3. What does it mean that "the one who looks weakest often wins" in God's story?

**Song ideas:** A Christmas or Advent song about Jesus' birth, or a song about God's power in
weakness.
