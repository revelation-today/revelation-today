# Being Faithful Is Already Being a King
### Revelation 20:1-6

## 1. Starting question: The secret king

Ask: "If someone dressed in plain, everyday clothes, with no crown and no fancy palace, could
secretly be a king or queen — how would you know?"

Say: "Today's story is about people who don't look like kings and queens at all, but who the
Bible says are already ruling with Jesus."

## 2. A strange detail

In Revelation 20, John talks about people who were treated badly for following Jesus, and it
says they "came to life and reigned with Christ." Long ago in Rome, only really important,
high-ranking people were treated a certain way when they were executed. So when the Bible uses
that word here, it's making a point on purpose: dying for Jesus doesn't make someone a loser —
it makes them like a king.

## 3. So does this apply only to certain people?

No! The Bible says something amazing back in chapter 1: Jesus has already made all of us who
follow him "a kingdom and priests." That means you don't have to wait until some future day, far
away, to find out if being faithful "counted." If you stay honest when it's hard, if you're kind
when others aren't, if you keep loving Jesus even when it costs you something — you are already
reigning with him, right now.

## 4. What about the "thousand years"?

Some people believe the "thousand years" in this chapter is a future time still to come. Others
believe it describes the whole time between Jesus' first coming and his second coming — which
would mean we're living in it right now! Christians who love Jesus and take the Bible seriously
believe different things about this, and that's okay. What matters most is the part everyone
agrees on: Jesus already reigns, and being faithful to him is never wasted, whenever it happens.

## 5. The best part

Whether the "thousand years" is happening right now or still coming, one thing is certain:
Satan's power to stop the good news about Jesus from reaching every part of the world was
already broken when Jesus died and rose again. That's why missionaries can go anywhere, why
Bibles can be translated into every language, why the gospel keeps spreading. Jesus already won
the decisive battle.

## 6. Memory verse

"You have made them to be a kingdom and priests to serve our God." *(Revelation 5:10, adapted)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that being faithful to you is never a waste, even when it's hard. Help us
remember that we already belong to your kingdom, right now. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The "secret king" question described above.

**Craft idea:** Kids make a simple paper crown with "Faithful = Already a King/Queen" written on
it, to reinforce that faithfulness (not visible power) is what Jesus calls royal.

**Gentle conversation note:** If kids have heard about a "future thousand-year kingdom" from
other sources, it's fine to simply say Christians hold different views on the timing, and to
keep the focus on the shared, positive truth: Jesus is already ruling, and faithfulness always
counts.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's a time it was hard to stay kind or honest, and you did it anyway?
2. How does it feel to know that counts as "reigning with Jesus" right now?
3. Why do you think Jesus cares more about faithfulness than about looking important?

**Song ideas:** A song about Jesus as King, or about faithfulness and courage.
