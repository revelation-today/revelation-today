# Hard Times That Jesus Already Knows About
### What is the "Great Tribulation"?

## 1. Starting question: Rain that keeps coming back

Ask: "Do you think of rain as one single storm that happens once, or as something that comes
and goes many times?" Let kids answer.

Say: "Some people think the 'Great Tribulation' — a Bible phrase for really hard times — is
like one single, huge storm that hasn't happened yet and is still far away in the future. But
what if it's more like rain that's been falling off and on for a really long time already?"

## 2. Where the phrase comes from

A long time ago, in the book of Daniel, God's people heard about a coming "time of trouble like
none before it." Jesus used those exact words when he talked to his own followers about hard
times ahead.

## 3. Jesus already told us

Here's something important: Jesus told his friends, before he even died on the cross, "In this
world you will have trouble." Not "someday, far in the future, you might have trouble" — he
said it plainly, to real people, about their own lives. And it happened! The first Christians —
like Paul and Peter — went through real hardship: being made fun of, losing jobs, even prison,
just for loving Jesus.

## 4. So when is it happening?

That's the exciting part: the "Great Tribulation" isn't only something way off in the future
that we're waiting for. It describes the whole stretch of time between Jesus' first coming and
his second coming — which means it includes hard times Christians have faced for two thousand
years, and it includes hard times some Christians are facing right now, in other parts of the
world, just for following Jesus.

Some people read this differently and think of the Great Tribulation as one specific, shorter
time still coming in the future — and that's a fair way to read it too. Either way, the important
part doesn't change.

## 5. Why this is good news, not scary news

If you've ever been made fun of for believing in Jesus, or felt like following him is hard —
that's not a sign that something has gone wrong. Jesus told us ahead of time that this would
happen, so we wouldn't be caught by surprise. And right after he warns us, he says something
even more important:

"I have told you these things, so that in me you may have peace. In this world you will have
trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world." *(John 16:33)*

## 6. Memory verse

"In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world."
*(John 16:33)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for telling us ahead of time that hard times would come, so we wouldn't be
scared or surprised. Help us remember that you have already won, no matter what we face. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** Show a picture of repeated rain clouds (not one giant storm) as a visual
for tribulation being ongoing rather than a single future event.

**Gentle conversation note:** Some children's ministries have used "left behind" style material
before. If kids bring up specific fears about a future tribulation, gently affirm that
Christians have different views on the exact timing, and steer back to the reassurance: Jesus
already knows, already warned us, and has already overcome the world.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has anyone ever teased you or made it hard for you because you follow Jesus?
2. How does it help to know Jesus told us ahead of time that this might happen?
3. What does "I have overcome the world" mean to you?

**Song ideas:** A song about courage or about Jesus overcoming the world.
