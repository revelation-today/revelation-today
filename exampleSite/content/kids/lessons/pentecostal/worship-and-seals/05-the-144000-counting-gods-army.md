# The Army You Can't Count
### Revelation 7

## 1. Starting question: Guess how many

Fill a jar with a large number of small items (buttons, beans) and have kids guess how many are
inside. Reveal the actual number. Ask: "Was it easy or hard to guess exactly?"

Say: "In today's story, John hears an exact number of God's people — but then something amazing
happens when he actually looks."

## 2. The number John hears

An angel tells John that 144,000 people from Israel's tribes have been marked with a special
seal, like a stamp that shows they belong to God. It sounds like a very specific, exact army —
all counted and organized.

## 3. The surprise when John looks

But then John actually turns to look — and instead of a small, exact army, he sees a
gigantic crowd, so big that nobody could ever count it, made up of people from every country and
every language in the whole world, all standing in front of God's throne, worshiping together.

Ask: "Why do you think the Bible tells it this way — first a specific number, then a crowd too
big to count?"

Explain: They're the same group! It's teaching us that God knows each one of us by name and
counts us carefully, like the exact number — but at the same time, God's family is so huge and
so diverse that it can't really be counted at all. Both are true at once.

## 4. You belong in this crowd

If you love Jesus, you're part of this crowd — a family so big it includes people from every
country, every language, every background, all standing together worshiping God. You're not
invisible to God. You're seen, known, marked as his own — and you're part of something huge.

## 5. Why this matters

Even when things are hard, even when we feel small or alone, this story reminds us: we are
already counted by God, already sealed as his own, and already part of an enormous family that
nothing can shrink.

## 6. Memory verse

"A great multitude that no one could count, from every nation, tribe, people and language,
standing before the throne." *(Revelation 7:9, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you know us by name and that we belong to your huge, uncountable
family. Help us remember that we're never alone, even when things are hard. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The "guess how many" jar game described above.

**Craft idea:** Kids each add a handprint or sticker to a large shared poster titled "God's
Uncountable Family" to visualize the huge crowd from every nation.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. How does it feel to know God knows your name specifically, out of such a huge crowd?
2. Why do you think people from every country and language are in this picture together?
3. Who is someone very different from you that you might meet in this "uncountable crowd"?

**Song ideas:** A song about belonging to God's family or about worship "from every nation."
