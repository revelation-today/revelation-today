# The Room That Never Stops Singing
### Revelation 4-5

## 1. Starting question: What's your favorite worship moment?

Ask the kids: "What's it like in here when we're all singing loudly together, hands up, really
meaning it? How does that feel?" Let a few kids answer.

Say: "Today we're going to look at a room in heaven where that never stops. Not for a minute,
not for a break — ever."

## 2. What John sees

John gets to peek into heaven, and he sees the most amazing worship service ever. Wild
creatures with eyes all over them keep saying "Holy, holy, holy!" Twenty-four elders fall down
and throw their crowns in front of God's throne. And then — more people than anyone could ever
count, from every country and every language, all singing together to a Lamb standing right in
the middle of it all.

Ask: "Does that sound like anywhere you've been?" (Let them connect it to your own church's
worship times — loud, joyful, all-in.)

## 3. Why worship comes first

Here's something cool: in the story, this worship scene happens *before* anything else big
happens in the book. Everything that comes later flows out of this room. That's a clue for us
too — worship isn't just something we do to get ready for the "important stuff." Worship *is*
some of the most important stuff.

## 4. The big surprise: a Lion who is a Lamb

In the story, everyone is looking for someone strong enough to open a special scroll — like
someone strong enough to unlock a treasure chest that no one else can open. John hears
someone say "The Lion has won! He can open it!" But when John turns to look — it's not a lion
at all. It's a Lamb, and this Lamb looks like it had already been hurt.

That's the biggest surprise in the whole Bible: Jesus wins, not by being the biggest and
strongest and scariest, but by giving himself up for us on the cross. The "Lion" and the "Lamb"
are the same person — Jesus — and he's stronger than anyone, precisely because of how he won.

## 5. Why this matters for us

When we worship loudly, with our whole hearts, we're joining in with something that never
stops in heaven. And we get to remember: Jesus is the strongest one of all, and he showed that
strength through love, not through scaring people.

## 6. Memory verse

"Worthy is the Lamb, who was slain, to receive power and wealth and wisdom and strength and
honor and glory and praise!" *(Revelation 5:12, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are the Lion and the Lamb — strong enough to win, and loving
enough to give yourself for us. Help us to worship you with our whole hearts, like the people
in heaven do. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** Play a short, energetic worship song and let the kids sing/move freely,
then transition into the lesson by connecting it to Revelation 4-5.

**Craft idea:** Kids draw or color a "throne room" scene with the Lamb in the middle, surrounded
by worshippers of "every nation."

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What does it feel like when we worship loudly together?
2. Why do you think Jesus is pictured as both a Lion and a Lamb?
3. What's one way you can worship God this week, even outside of church?

**Song ideas:** Any energetic, participatory worship song your congregation already uses
regularly — the point is to connect this session to worship they already know.
