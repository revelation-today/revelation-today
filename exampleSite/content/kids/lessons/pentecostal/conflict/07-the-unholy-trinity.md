# The Copycat Team
### Revelation 13

## 1. Starting question: Spot the copy

Show two very similar-looking items — a real brand-name snack and a store-brand copycat version
with almost the same packaging. Ask: "What's different if you look closely? Why do companies
make copycat packaging like this?"

Say: "Today's story is about the biggest, sneakiest copycat team in the whole Bible — a team
trying to look just like God."

## 2. The copycat team

In Revelation 13, John sees a dragon and two "beasts" (scary creatures) working together. Here's
the sneaky part: they're deliberately set up to look like a copy of God the Father, Jesus, and
the Holy Spirit — three-in-one, just like the real thing. The dragon gives power to the first
beast, like the Father gives power to Jesus. The first beast even seems to come back to life
after being hurt, like a fake copy of Jesus rising from the dead. The second beast points
everyone's attention to the first beast and does amazing "signs," like a fake copy of the Holy
Spirit.

## 3. How can you tell it's fake?

Here's the big clue the Bible gives us: the second beast "had two horns like a lamb, but it
spoke like a dragon" (Revelation 13:11). It *looked* gentle, like Jesus (the Lamb) — but when it
opened its mouth, it sounded like the enemy.

Ask: "If something looks nice on the outside but says or does things that don't match Jesus,
what should we do?"

## 4. Jesus' sheep know his voice

Jesus tells us the secret to not being fooled: "My sheep listen to my voice... they will never
follow a stranger" (John 10:4-5, 27). The way to spot a copycat isn't just looking at the
outside — it's knowing Jesus so well, through prayer, worship, and reading his Word, that we can
tell right away when something doesn't sound like him, even if it looks impressive.

## 5. What Jesus never does

The copycat team tries to scare people into following it. Jesus never does that. Jesus loves
people into following him. That's the biggest difference of all: real power from God always
looks like love, even when it seems weak. Fake power always looks like fear and force, even
when it seems strong.

## 6. Memory verse

"My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me." *(John 10:27)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us know your voice so well that we can always tell when something is trying to
copy you but isn't really you. Help us follow real love, not fear. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The "spot the copycat brand" comparison described above.

**Craft idea:** Kids make two columns on paper: "What Jesus is really like" and "What a copycat
might look like" and fill them in together as a group.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's something that looked good on the outside but wasn't really good?
2. How do you get to know Jesus' voice better?
3. Why do you think love is stronger than fear, even though fear can seem more powerful?

**Song ideas:** A song about knowing Jesus' voice or about God's love casting out fear.
