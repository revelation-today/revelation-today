# The Rider Who Looked Like a Winner
### Revelation 6

## 1. Starting question: Fool's gold

Show the kids a piece of pyrite ("fool's gold") next to a (fake or real) gold coin, or just
describe it: "There's a shiny rock that looks exactly like gold but isn't gold at all. It even
fooled miners looking for real gold! How could you tell the difference?"

Say: "Today's story is about someone who rides out looking exactly like a winner — but he's
not who he seems."

## 2. Four riders

When Jesus opens the first four seals in heaven, four riders come out on four different colored
horses: white, red, black, and pale. They bring conquest, war, hunger, and death.

## 3. The tricky one

The first rider, on the white horse, looks like good news at first — a crown, a bow, riding out
like a winner. Some people might even think, "Hey, that looks like Jesus!" But look what
happens next: right after he rides out, everything falls apart — war, hunger, and death follow
right behind him.

Ask: "If someone promises they're going to make everything great, but everything they touch
falls apart instead — do you think they were telling the truth?"

## 4. Spotting the difference

This rider is a fake — someone dressed up to look like a winner, like Jesus, but he isn't Jesus
at all. The Bible teaches us that Satan is sneaky like that — he doesn't usually show up looking
obviously bad. He shows up looking shiny and impressive, promising good things, and then leaves
a mess behind.

That's why it's so important to learn how to tell the difference between something that
*looks* good and something that *really is* good — the same way you already learn to ask,
"Does this really match what Jesus is like?" whenever something seems impressive.

## 5. The real test

The real test isn't "does it look powerful?" It's "does it act like Jesus?" Jesus heals, Jesus
loves, Jesus gives his life for others. Anything that promises big things but leaves
brokenness and hurt behind is not from him — no matter how shiny it looked at first.

## 6. Memory verse

"Satan himself masquerades as an angel of light." *(2 Corinthians 11:14)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to look past shiny, impressive things and ask whether they really act like
you. Give us wisdom to tell the difference between what's real and what's fake. Amen.

## Appendix: Preparation ideas

**Opening activity:** The fool's gold / real gold comparison, or any "spot the fake" object
game.

**Craft idea:** Kids make two drawings side by side — "looks good" and "really is good" — and
list what makes something really good (kindness, honesty, love) versus just looking impressive.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Can you think of something that looked good but wasn't really good?
2. How can you tell if something really comes from Jesus?
3. Why do you think it's important to look past first impressions?

**Song ideas:** A song about trusting Jesus and knowing his voice.
