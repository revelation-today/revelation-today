# God Hears "How Long?"
### The cry under the altar

---

## 1. Opening: Have you ever asked "how long"?

*(Ask: Has anyone ever waited a really long time for something — waiting for a parent to come home, waiting for good news, waiting for something hard to end? How did it feel to keep asking "how long"?)*

Today we're going to see that even people in heaven ask that same question — and God does not tell them to stop asking.

---

## 2. A cry from under the altar

John sees, in his vision, people in heaven who died because they loved Jesus and wouldn't stop telling others about him. And they cry out with a loud voice: "How long, Sovereign Lord, holy and true, until you judge the inhabitants of the earth and avenge our blood?" (Revelation 6:9-10).

This might surprise you — these are people already in heaven, with God, and they're still asking "how long?" That tells us something important: asking God "how long" is not a bad question, or a sign you don't trust him enough. It's in the Bible, said by faithful people, right in front of God himself.

---

## 3. God answers — he doesn't stay silent

What does God do when they ask? He doesn't ignore them. "Each of them was given a white robe, and they were told to wait a little longer" (Revelation 6:11). God gives them something special right away — a white robe, which means honor — and he tells them the truth: a little longer.

That's not the answer we might wish for. But it is an answer. God isn't silent. He hears. He responds. And he tells them the truth instead of pretending.

---

## 4. If you've ever asked "how long"

Maybe you've wondered "how long" about something hard in your own family or your own community — something that's been hard for a really long time with no end in sight. This part of the Bible tells you: that question belongs in your prayers. You are not doubting God by asking it. You're praying exactly the way faithful people in heaven already pray.

---

## 5. God is not a God who doesn't care

Later in this same chapter, some people are so afraid of God's justice finally arriving that they want to hide from it (Revelation 6:15-16). But this fear comes from their own guilt — from people who have hurt others and don't want to face what they've done. It's not because God is scary to his own people. For those who love him, God's justice finally coming means the wrongs done to them are finally being made right.

---

## 6. What this means for us

If something has felt unfair for a really long time — if you've wondered why God hasn't fixed something yet — remember the people under the altar. They asked "how long," and God answered them with honor and with truth. He will answer you too.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"How long, Sovereign Lord, holy and true, until you judge the inhabitants of the earth?" *(Revelation 6:10)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, sometimes we want to ask you "how long" about hard things in our lives and in our world. Thank you that this is okay to ask. Help us trust that you hear us, that you care, and that you will make things right, even when we have to wait. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Give each child a small piece of paper to write (or draw, for younger kids) their own "how long" question to God. Collect them in a basket as a prayer offering, or let kids keep them as a reminder God hears their honest prayers.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Have you ever wanted to ask God "how long" about something? What was it?
2. Why do you think it's important that even people in heaven ask this question?
3. How does it feel to know God answers, even when the answer isn't "right now"?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about God hearing our prayers
- Any children's song about waiting on God or trusting him
