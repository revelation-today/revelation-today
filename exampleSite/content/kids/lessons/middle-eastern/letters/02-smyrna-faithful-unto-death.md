# The Church Jesus Had Nothing Bad to Say About
### The letter to Smyrna

---

## 1. Opening: A report card with no corrections

*(Hold up a pretend report card or paper.)*

Imagine getting a report card where the teacher didn't write a single "try harder" or "needs improvement" anywhere — just good things. That would feel amazing, right? Today we're looking at one of only two churches in the whole book of Revelation that Jesus gives a perfect report to. And it's not the rich church, or the safe one. It's the poorest, most pressured church of all seven.

---

## 2. A church with real trouble

The church in a city called Smyrna had real problems — not problems they caused, problems that came from outside. They were poor. People said mean, untrue things about them. Some of them were about to be put in prison. If you have family or friends who have had a hard time just for following Jesus — maybe trouble at school, trouble finding work, or worse — this church knows exactly what that feels like.

---

## 3. Jesus already knows what it's like to die

Before Jesus says anything else to this church, he says something important about himself: "These are the words of him who... died and came to life again" (Revelation 2:8). Jesus doesn't say this from far away, like someone who's never faced anything hard. He says it as someone who already went through the worst thing — death itself — and came out alive on the other side.

---

## 4. Poor and rich at the same time

Jesus tells them: "I know your afflictions and your poverty — yet you are rich!" (Revelation 2:9). Isn't that interesting? Jesus doesn't pretend they aren't poor. He just adds something true right alongside it: by heaven's counting, they are actually rich. Both things are true at the same time.

---

## 5. Don't be afraid, even of the hardest thing

Then Jesus says something that takes real courage to hear: "Do not be afraid of what you are about to suffer... Be faithful, even to the point of death, and I will give you life as your victor's crown" (Revelation 2:10). Jesus doesn't promise them nothing bad will happen. He tells them the truth — some hard things are coming — and he tells them what to do: don't be afraid, and stay faithful to me.

A "victor's crown" back then was given to a winner — like a gold medal today. Jesus is telling this poor, struggling church: what you're doing right now, staying faithful even when it's hard, that's not losing. That's what winning actually looks like to me.

---

## 6. What this church teaches us

Out of all seven churches Jesus writes letters to in Revelation, this poor, struggling, threatened one is one of only two that gets zero corrections. Not the safest church. Not the richest. The one going through the hardest things, faithfully, together with Jesus.

If your family or your church has faced real hardship for following Jesus, this letter is written especially for you. Jesus isn't disappointed in you for struggling. He is calling your faithfulness through hard things exactly what it is: a crown.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"Be faithful, even to the point of death, and I will give you life as your victor's crown." *(Revelation 2:10)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you understand hard things, because you already went through the hardest thing yourself. Help us to stay faithful to you even when it's difficult, and help us to trust that you see everything we go through and call it precious, not a failure. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Have each child make a simple paper crown. On the inside, write "faithful" — talk about how staying faithful through something hard is what Jesus calls a crown, even if nobody else sees it that way.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has anyone in your family ever had something hard happen because they follow Jesus? You don't have to share details if you don't want to — just think about it.
2. Why do you think Jesus told this poor, hurting church that they were "rich"?
3. What does it mean to be "faithful" even when things are scary?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about being brave or being strong in Jesus
- "I Am a Child of the King" or a similar song about our true identity in Jesus
