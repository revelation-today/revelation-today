# A Door No One Can Shut
### The letter to Philadelphia

---

## 1. Opening: The locked door

*(Stand in front of a door, and pretend to try many keys that don't work.)*

Have you ever needed to get through a door, but couldn't find the right key? Today's church, Philadelphia, was small and weak — but Jesus told them about a very special key: one that opens doors nobody else can shut, and shuts doors nobody else can open.

---

## 2. A shaky, uncertain city

The city of Philadelphia had earthquakes all the time — the ground itself wasn't steady under their feet. And even when the people were loyal to the powerful rulers around them, those rulers didn't always treat them fairly back. It's a bit like knowing your ground isn't steady, and that being loyal to the people in charge doesn't always protect you. Some of you know exactly what that feels like.

---

## 3. Little strength is enough

Jesus says to this church: "I know that you have little strength, yet you have kept my word and have not denied my name" (Revelation 3:8). Isn't that interesting? Jesus doesn't say, "You're so strong." He says the opposite — you have little strength — and he treats that as completely fine. What mattered wasn't how strong they were. It was that they didn't stop belonging to Jesus.

---

## 4. The open door

Then Jesus makes a huge promise: "I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut" (Revelation 3:8). This door means a way forward — a chance to keep telling people about Jesus, a way for the church to keep going — that no government, no unkind neighbor, no powerful person can permanently close, no matter how hard they try.

If it ever feels like every door around you is closing, this promise is for you: the doors that matter most to Jesus, he keeps open himself.

---

## 5. Kept through it, not taken away from it

Jesus also promises: "I will keep you from the hour of trial that is going to come on the whole world" (Revelation 3:10). This doesn't mean Jesus removes them from every hard thing. It means something even better: he protects them and stays with them right through the hard thing, the same way he protected Daniel's three friends inside the fire, not by keeping them out of it.

---

## 6. A permanent home, finally

"The one who is victorious I will make a pillar in the temple of my God. Never again will they leave it" (Revelation 3:12). For a city that knew shaking ground and broken promises, this is enormous: a home with God that never shakes, never gets taken away, and that they never have to leave again.

---

## 7. What this means for us

If your strength ever feels small compared to the hard things around you, remember: Jesus didn't ask Philadelphia to be strong. He asked them to stay his. And that was enough for him to promise them a door nobody could shut, and a home that would never be taken away.

---

## 8. Memory verse

"I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut." *(Revelation 3:8)*

---

## 9. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that our strength doesn't have to be big for you to keep your promises to us. Thank you for the doors you keep open, even when everything around us seems closed. Help us to trust you to keep us, even in hard times, instead of just taking us away from them. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Cut out a simple paper door that opens. On the inside, write "No one can shut this door — Revelation 3:8." Let children decorate it.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What does it feel like when you have "little strength" but still try to do the right thing anyway?
2. Can you think of a "door" in your life right now that feels closed? What would it mean to trust Jesus with it?
3. Why do you think Jesus promises to keep his people "through" hard times instead of always taking them away?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about doors, keys, or Jesus opening the way
- Any children's song about trusting God in hard times
