# The Dragon Who Couldn't Win
### Christmas, told a different way

---

## 1. Opening: The Christmas story you know

*(Ask kids to quickly retell the Christmas story they know: shepherds, baby Jesus, the star.)*

You know that story well. But did you know Revelation tells the Christmas story in a totally different way — as a huge, scary battle between a helpless woman and an enormous dragon?

---

## 2. A woman about to give birth, and a dragon waiting to attack

John sees a woman, about to have a baby, crying out in pain (Revelation 12:1-2). And right in front of her: "an enormous red dragon with seven heads and ten horns," waiting to eat her baby the moment he's born (Revelation 12:3-4). If you only watched this moment, you'd think: there's no way this baby survives.

---

## 3. Who are they?

The dragon is named directly: "that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan" (Revelation 12:9) — the very same enemy from the garden of Eden, all the way back at the beginning of the Bible.

The woman represents God's people, waiting a very long time for the promised Savior to be born — waiting during a hard time, when a powerful empire ruled over them and crushed anyone who fought back.

And the baby? He's described as the one who will "rule all the nations with an iron sceptre" (Revelation 12:5) — that's Jesus.

---

## 4. Born right into danger — and rescued anyway

Jesus wasn't born into safety. He was born right into danger, and a king even tried to kill him as a baby (Matthew 2). But God rescued him. Later, the devil tried again to trip Jesus up (Matthew 4). And even later, Jesus was killed on the cross — which looked, for a moment, like the dragon had finally won.

But he hadn't. Jesus came back to life and went up to heaven, snatched away from the dragon's reach for good (Revelation 12:5).

---

## 5. The pattern for the whole book

This is the pattern for the entire book of Revelation: things that look weak turn out to win, and things that look unbeatable turn out to lose. The lion turns out to be a lamb. The two witnesses look defeated and come back to life. The scary dragon and his helpers look unstoppable but are actually already doomed.

---

## 6. What this means for us

If the "dragons" in your own life — things that feel too big, too scary, too powerful — seem unbeatable right now, remember this story. The woman looked helpless. The dragon looked unstoppable. But the baby survived, grew up, and won — and that same pattern is God's promise for every hard thing you face too.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"She gave birth to a son, a male child, who 'will rule all the nations.' And her child was snatched up to God and to his throne." *(Revelation 12:5)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for sending Jesus even into a dangerous, scary world. Thank you that the dragon didn't win, and that things that look scary and powerful don't get the last word. Help us trust you with the "dragons" in our own lives. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Draw a big dragon on one side of a paper and a small baby on the other. Talk about how the baby looked weaker but actually won.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What do you think the woman must have felt like, facing such a huge dragon?
2. Why do you think Jesus was born right into a dangerous time instead of a safe one?
3. What "dragon" in your own life do you want to trust God with today?

**Song suggestions:**
- A Christmas song about Jesus as King, sung any time of year
- Any children's song about God's power over evil
