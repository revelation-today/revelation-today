# Every Tear, Wiped Away
### Heaven and the New Jerusalem

---

## 1. Opening: The best homecoming

*(Ask: What's the best "welcome home" you can imagine, after being away a long time?)*

Today we get to slow down and really enjoy the best promise in the whole Bible: a home with God where every hard thing is finally, completely over.

---

## 2. A whole new world

John sees "a new heaven and a new earth" (Revelation 21:1) — not just patched up, but completely new and never going to wear out or break again. And there's "no longer any sea" — remember, the sea was where scary, dangerous things came from in this book. No more sea means no more place left for anything scary to hide.

---

## 3. God, right there with us

"God's dwelling-place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them... God himself will be with them and be their God" (Revelation 21:3). Not visiting sometimes. Living right there with his people, forever, close up, no more distance at all.

---

## 4. Every single tear

Here is one of the most beautiful verses in the whole Bible: "He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain, for the old order of things has passed away" (Revelation 21:4). Not just that tears will stop — God himself, personally, wipes each one away. If you or your family have cried real tears over hard, sad, scary things, this promise is exactly for you: every single tear, wiped away, by God's own hand.

---

## 5. The promises finally coming true

Remember the church in Smyrna, promised they'd never be hurt by the "second death" (Revelation 2:11)? And Philadelphia, promised to be a permanent pillar who never has to leave God's presence (Revelation 3:12)? In this new world, those promises come completely true. Nothing was too good to actually happen.

---

## 6. A city shaped like the most special room ever

The new city is shaped like a perfect cube — and the only room in the whole Old Testament shaped like that was the "Most Holy Place," the one room in the temple where only one priest could go, only once a year, because it was so full of God's presence. In the new city, that most special, most holy shape becomes the whole city — meaning everyone gets to be as close to God as that one priest used to be, all the time, forever.

---

## 7. What this means for us

Whatever tears you carry, whatever your family has gone through, whatever feels sad or scary or unfair right now — this is God's honest promise: a new world with no more of it, God himself right there with you, and every tear wiped away by his own hand.

---

## 8. Memory verse

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain." *(Revelation 21:4)*

---

## 9. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for promising a home with you where every tear will be wiped away. Thank you that you are close to us now, and that one day you will be even closer, forever. We trust you with everything sad or hard in our lives right now. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Give each child a paper teardrop shape. Let them write or draw something sad or hard on it, then "wipe it away" with a tissue as a reminder of God's promise, or place it in a basket as a prayer.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's a tear you'd love for God to wipe away one day?
2. Why do you think it matters that God does the wiping himself, instead of just saying tears will stop?
3. What do you look forward to most about the new heaven and new earth?

**Song suggestions:**
- A quiet, comforting song about heaven or God's presence
- Any children's song about God wiping away tears or being close to us
