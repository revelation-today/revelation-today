# The Room Where Every Promise Comes True
### Worship in heaven's throne room

---

## 1. Opening: The best surprise room

*(Ask: If you could open a door and find any wonderful surprise on the other side, what would it be?)*

Today, John opens a door — a real door, standing open in heaven itself (Revelation 4:1) — and what's on the other side is more wonderful than anything we could imagine: God's own throne room, full of worship.

---

## 2. The door we were already promised

Remember last time, Jesus promised the church in Philadelphia "an open door that no one can shut" (Revelation 3:8)? Right after that promise, John sees an open door into heaven. That's not an accident! It's Jesus showing us: the door I promised you leads here — right into my own presence.

---

## 3. Everything promised, already true

Look what John sees around God's throne: people wearing crowns — just like the crown promised to the church in Smyrna (Revelation 2:10). People wearing white clothes — just like the promise given to other churches. People sitting on thrones — just like another promise. Everything Jesus promised the struggling churches, John sees already happening around God's throne. That's the whole point: what's promised to you is not far away or make-believe. It's already real in heaven, waiting for you.

---

## 4. God's glory is his mercy

John sees flashes of lightning and hears thunder from the throne — so much glory it's hard to even describe. But there's also a rainbow around the throne (Revelation 4:3). Remember the rainbow God gave Noah? It was a promise — a sign that God would show mercy. So even in the middle of this powerful, almost scary vision, there's a reminder: God's power is always wrapped up with his mercy.

---

## 5. The sea that isn't scary anymore

John also sees something like a sea of glass in front of the throne (Revelation 4:6) — like a sea that's frozen solid, completely still. The sea in the Bible often stands for something scary or dangerous. But here, right at God's throne, that scary sea is completely calm and still. Whatever feels big and scary in your own life, this picture reminds us: near God's throne, even the scariest things are made calm.

---

## 6. One big worship team

Around the throne are twenty-four elders and four amazing living creatures, and later, more angels than anyone could count, and they never stop worshiping God together (Revelation 4-5). It's one huge, united family, worshiping together — a picture of what God's people, from every background and every nation, really are underneath all our differences.

---

## 7. Why worship helps with everything

Every hard thing the seven churches were dealing with — wrong ideas, feeling too proud, feeling too discouraged — gets one answer in this vision: worship. The more we see how amazing God really is, the less scary and confusing everything else becomes.

---

## 8. Memory verse

"Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come." *(Revelation 4:8)*

---

## 9. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for showing us your throne room, full of worship and full of your promises already coming true. Help us to worship you even when things around us feel scary, because you are bigger than all of it. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening activity:** Play some worship music and let the kids imagine (with eyes closed) what it might be like to stand in a room full of endless worship.

**Craft idea:** Draw a simple picture of a throne with a rainbow around it. Write "God's power and God's mercy go together" underneath.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What do you think it would feel like to see God's throne room the way John did?
2. Why do you think a calm, frozen sea is such a good picture for something that used to feel scary?
3. What's one thing about God that makes you want to worship him today?

**Song suggestions:**
- "Holy, Holy, Holy" (simplified for kids)
- Any joyful children's worship song
