# The Beautiful Trap
### Who is the Harlot Babylon?

---

## 1. Opening: The pretty poison berry

*(If possible, show a picture of a beautiful but poisonous berry or plant.)*

Some things that look beautiful and inviting are actually dangerous. Today's part of Revelation shows us a woman dressed in gold and jewels, looking rich and glamorous — but she's actually one of the most dangerous pictures in the whole book.

---

## 2. A woman named Babylon

In Revelation 17-18, John sees a woman covered in gold, jewels, and fine clothes, called "Babylon." She sits on a scary beast, and kings and powerful people love her and want to be near her. For the people who first read Revelation, this represented the huge, powerful empire ruling over them at the time — a government that looked strong, rich, and impressive, and demanded everyone bow down to it.

---

## 3. The trap she offers

Babylon offers people something that looks wonderful: safety, wealth, and being accepted by everyone — but only if you're willing to go along with her and stop being fully loyal to Jesus first. That's the trap. It's not that she looks scary. It's that she looks so good that it's tempting to make the trade.

---

## 4. It's not just one thing, one place, or one time

Here's something important: Babylon isn't just about one specific country or ruler from long ago. It's a pattern that can show up anywhere, anytime, whenever something powerful asks God's people to trade away their loyalty to Jesus in exchange for safety or wealth or fitting in. That means it's worth thinking carefully, prayerfully, about where this pattern might show up in our own lives and our own world — with the help of the grown-ups you trust, rather than us telling you exactly who or what to think of.

---

## 5. She falls down, suddenly

Here's the encouraging part: even though Babylon looks so powerful and unshakeable, she is suddenly destroyed — and by the very same people who used to love her (Revelation 17:16)! Anything built on being powerful and rich instead of being built on God can fall apart very suddenly, even when it looks like it will last forever.

---

## 6. "Come out of her"

God tells his people: "Come out of her, my people" (Revelation 18:4). This doesn't always mean physically leaving a place. It means not making the trade Babylon offers — staying loyal to Jesus first, even while living in a hard or tempting place.

---

## 7. What this means for us

If something around you ever seems really powerful, really tempting, and asks you to put loyalty to Jesus second — remember Babylon. She looked unbeatable, and she fell anyway. What's built on God lasts. What isn't, doesn't, no matter how strong it looks right now.

---

## 8. Memory verse

"Come out of her, my people, so that you will not share in her sins." *(Revelation 18:4)*

---

## 9. Prayer

Dear God, help us to notice when something looks good on the outside but asks us to love it more than we love you. Thank you that anything built without you cannot last, even if it looks strong right now. Help us stay close to you first, always. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Craft idea:** Draw two towers — one labeled "built on gold" and one labeled "built on Jesus." Talk about which one lasts, even in a storm.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Can you think of something that looks really good or exciting, but might pull us away from loving Jesus first?
2. Why do you think Babylon fell so suddenly, even though she looked so strong?
3. What does "come out of her" mean, even if we can't move away from a place?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about building your life on Jesus (like the wise and foolish builders)
- Any children's song about choosing God first
