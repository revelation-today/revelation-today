# Jesus Is Standing Right There With You
### Starting the book of Revelation

---

## 1. Opening: Who is holding your hand?

*(Ask a few children to hold hands in a line, and gently try to pull the last one away while the others hold on.)*

When someone is holding your hand tightly, it's hard to fall, even if you stumble. Today we start a whole new series about the last book of the Bible, Revelation, and the very first thing it shows us is Jesus holding on to his people tightly — even when things around them are hard and scary.

---

## 2. A letter from someone far from home

The man who wrote Revelation is named John. He tells us something important about himself right away: he was sent away, alone, to an island called Patmos, far from his church and his friends — because he wouldn't stop talking about Jesus. Some of you know grown-ups, maybe even in your own family, who have had to leave home, or who are far from people they love, because of their faith. John knew exactly what that felt like. He wasn't writing from somewhere safe and far away — he was writing from right in the middle of it.

---

## 3. A vision too big to describe

While John was on that island, God gave him an amazing vision. He saw Jesus — but not like a picture in a book. Jesus's eyes were like fire, his voice like rushing water, his face bright like the sun. It was so much that John fell down like he'd fainted!

But here's the most important part: Jesus was standing right in the middle of seven lampstands — and we find out those lampstands are seven churches, real churches with real people who were going through hard things, just like some of us do. Jesus wasn't standing far away from them. He was right there in the middle.

---

## 4. Don't be afraid — I already beat death

John was so amazed and scared that he fell down. But Jesus said something wonderful to him: "Do not be afraid. I am the Living One; I was dead, and now look, I am alive for ever and ever! And I hold the keys of death" (Revelation 1:17-18).

Jesus telling John "don't be afraid" wasn't Jesus pretending everything was easy. It was Jesus saying: I have already been through death, and I came out the other side. Whatever you're scared of, I've already faced something worse, and I won.

---

## 5. Held in his hand

Then we learn something beautiful about those seven lampstands and the seven stars Jesus is holding: "the seven stars are the angels of the seven churches, and the seven lampstands are the seven churches" (Revelation 1:20). Jesus holds the stars — that's the churches — in his hand. Held. Not just watched from far away. Held, the way you hold something you won't let fall.

A lamp only stays lit if there's oil in it. A star can only give light if it's in Jesus's hand. On our own, we can't shine. But held by Jesus, we can.

---

## 6. What this means for us

Maybe your family, or your church, knows what it's like to face hard things because you follow Jesus. This first picture in Revelation is for you specifically: Jesus isn't watching from somewhere safe and far away. He is standing right in the middle of wherever you are, holding you in his hand, and he has already gone through the hardest thing — death itself — and come out alive.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"Do not be afraid. I am the Living One; I was dead, and now look, I am alive for ever and ever!" *(Revelation 1:17-18)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are not far away from us, even when things are hard. Thank you that you already won over death, and that you are holding us in your hand right now. Help us to remember that we are held, even when we're scared. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening activity:** The hand-holding line game from section 1 — talk afterward about how it felt to be held on to.

**Craft idea:** Give each child a small paper star. On it, write or draw something they're carrying right now — something hard, or something they're scared of. Then have them place their star into a drawn "hand" on a poster to remind them Jesus is holding them.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What does it mean to you that Jesus was standing right in the middle of the churches, not far away from them?
2. Has your family ever had to be apart from someone you love because of following Jesus, like John was?
3. What is something you want Jesus to hold onto for you this week?

**Song suggestions:**
- A simple song about Jesus as King or Jesus holding us
- Any children's song about not being afraid
