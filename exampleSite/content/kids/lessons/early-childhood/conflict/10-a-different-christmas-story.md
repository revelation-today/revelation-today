# A Different Christmas Story

## The Picture

Here's a Christmas story you may not have heard before. John saw a beautiful woman about to
have a baby — and a big, scary dragon waiting nearby, hoping to hurt the baby the moment he was
born.

But guess what? The baby was Jesus, and the dragon could not stop him. The moment Jesus was
born, God swooped in and kept him safe, and the dragon lost. Jesus grew up, and he wins — every
single time — over anything that ever tries to hurt him or hurt us.

Even when someone tries to stop God's plans, they can't. Jesus always wins, and he keeps us
safe too.

## Our Refrain

**"Jesus wins, and he loves us."**

Ask: "Could the dragon stop baby Jesus?" (No! Jesus always wins!)

## Activity

**Safe and Sound Game:** One child (or the leader) gently "reaches" toward a soft baby doll or
stuffed animal representing baby Jesus, while the rest of the children say "Jesus is safe!" and
mime scooping the doll up into a protective hug. Repeat a few times so the ending — safety and
victory — is what sticks.

*Coloring option:* Color a simple picture of a bright star over a baby in a manger, with the
words "Jesus is safe" underneath.

## Simple Prayer

"Jesus, thank you that nothing can ever stop you. Thank you for keeping me safe too. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

This is the session most likely to include a "dragon," so keep it extremely brief and
non-visual: mention the dragon only as "a bad guy who tried and failed," with zero description
of appearance or menace. Move to safety and victory as quickly as possible. The point is
Christmas-story-familiar and entirely comforting: baby Jesus is safe, and the bad guy loses.
