# Faithful Even When It's Hard

## The Picture

John saw a picture of a harvest — like when a farmer gathers up all the good, ripe fruit from
the field. Jesus was gathering up everyone who stayed close to him, everyone who kept loving
him no matter what.

That's the most important thing of all: staying close to Jesus, even on the days it's hard, or
boring, or when nobody else around us is doing it. Just like a farmer gathers the good fruit
carefully and happily, Jesus gathers up everyone who stays faithful to him, and he is so glad
to have them.

## Our Refrain

**"Jesus wins, and he loves us."**

Ask: "What matters most of all?" (Staying close to Jesus!)

## Activity

**Harvest Gathering Game:** Scatter some soft play-fruit, fruit-shaped cutouts, or balls of
crumpled yellow/red paper around the room. Have children "harvest" them into a basket while you
say, "Just like we gather the fruit, Jesus gathers everyone who stays close to him!" Celebrate
with cheering once the basket is full.

*Coloring option:* Color a simple picture of a full basket of fruit, with a smiling sun above
it.

## Simple Prayer

"Jesus, help me stay close to you every day, even when it's hard. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Skip the imagery of the "winepress" later in Revelation 14, which is vivid and not appropriate
for this age. Keep entirely to the harvest-gathering picture: Jesus happily and carefully
gathers everyone who stays faithful to him. The lesson is simple loyalty and love, framed
positively as being "gathered in," not judgment.
