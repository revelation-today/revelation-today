# Welcome, Parents and Teachers

This is the youngest track in our Revelation Today teaching series, written especially for
children ages 4 to 7. It sits alongside our elementary track (`/kids/lessons`), which is
written for older children who are ready for a bit more detail, structure, and discussion.
This track is much simpler on purpose.

## Our approach

Little children don't need — and shouldn't get — a full map of the book of Revelation. What
they need is to feel safe, loved, and excited about Jesus. So each of these 16 short sessions
(about 10 minutes each) takes just **one picture** from Revelation — Jesus standing with his
church, a beautiful throne room, a gentle Lamb, a lion who is really a lamb, a new home where
nobody ever has to say goodbye — and lets a child sit with that one picture for a while.

There are no timelines to memorize, no numbers to calculate, and no debates about how to
interpret symbols. Those things belong to later, older stages of learning. Here, we simply
tell the story and let the child feel what it's meant to make them feel: safe, loved, brave,
and excited for what's coming.

## One phrase, all the way through

Every session repeats the same simple line, in different moments — as a call-and-response, a
song-like chant, or a closing line before prayer:

**"Jesus wins, and he loves us."**

Repetition is a gift to young children, not a shortcut. By the end of the series, they will
know in their bones that Jesus wins and Jesus loves them, long before they can explain how or
why. That's exactly right for this age.

## What we leave out on purpose

You will not find beasts, dragons, plagues, or anything frightening described in vivid detail
here. When Revelation's imagery includes something scary, we simply say "the bad guy tries
something, and it doesn't work" and move on to the good news. Nothing here is meant to give a
small child nightmares. Everything here is meant to help them fall more in love with Jesus.

## What's inside each session

Every lesson includes one simple story, an easy activity (coloring, a simple action, or a
short game), our repeated phrase, and a one-line prayer your child can say on their own.
Sessions are numbered 1 through 16 and are meant to be used in order, but each one also stands
alone if you need to skip around.
