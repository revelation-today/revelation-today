# The Copycat Team

### The dragon and his two "beasts"

## 1. Getting started: the knockoff

Have you ever seen a knockoff toy or snack — something made to look almost exactly like a famous brand, but it isn't the real thing? Sometimes the packaging looks so similar you almost don't notice the difference at first. That's the idea behind today's story.

## 2. Three villains, one team

In Revelation 13, John describes a dragon and two "beasts" working together. The dragon is Satan, the enemy behind everything. The first beast is a stand-in for the powerful Roman emperor of that time — someone who demanded that everyone treat him almost like a god. The second beast is a stand-in for all the everyday people who helped make that happen — organizing parades, building statues, making sure nobody spoke up against it.

## 3. Copying God on purpose

Here's the clever, sneaky part: this trio is deliberately built to *look like* a copy of God — Father, Son, and Spirit. The first beast even appears to come back to life after being wounded, copying Jesus' resurrection. It's not an accident. It's a counterfeit, made to look enough like the real thing that people might not notice the difference.

## 4. Why the second beast is actually the sneakiest

Here's the part worth noticing most: the second beast — the ordinary people helping the system along — doesn't look scary or obviously evil at all. It just looks like everyone going along with normal life: attending the festival, keeping the peace, not making waves. That's exactly what makes it dangerous. The most convincing tricks rarely look like tricks. They look like "just going along with everyone else."

## 5. Why this matters

The good news is that Revelation doesn't leave us confused for long — it keeps showing us who the real thing is, over and over, so we get better at spotting the copy. The best way to avoid being fooled by a copycat isn't just suspicion — it's knowing the real Jesus well enough that a fake one stands out immediately.

## 6. Memory verse

"Be very careful, then, how you live — not as unwise but as wise." *(Ephesians 5:15)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, help us to know you so well that we can spot a copycat, even when it looks convincing. Give us courage to stand out instead of just going along with the crowd when something isn't right. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Bring two similar-looking snack packages — a name brand and a lookalike — and let kids spot the differences.

**Craft idea:** Kids make two drawings: one of "the real thing" (kindness, honesty) and one of a "copycat" that looks similar but isn't (fitting in vs. doing what's right).

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's something that looked like "the real thing" to you but turned out to be a copy?
2. Why do you think the sneakiest tricks are the ones that look normal, not obviously bad?
3. How can you get to know Jesus better this week so it's easier to spot a fake?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about knowing Jesus' voice
- A song about courage to stand apart from the crowd
