# A Letter, a Warning, and a Comic — All in One Book

### Why Revelation sounds so different from the rest of the Bible

## 1. Getting started: three ways to say something important

*(Bring a real letter in an envelope, a picture of a road sign or a comic-book panel, and a whistle or air horn.)*

Imagine I want to tell you something really important. I could do it three totally different ways:

1. Write you a **letter**: "Dear friends, I want to tell you something..."
2. Blow a **whistle** and shout: "Watch out! Pay attention, or something's going to happen!"
3. Show you a **comic panel** with a scary dragon threatening someone.

Pretty different, right? Here's the wild thing about the book of Revelation: it does **all three at the same time**. That's part of why it's tricky to understand — but once we know what to look for, it gets much easier.

## 2. First: a letter

Revelation starts and ends like a normal letter — the same way other letters in the Bible do. John really did write it to seven real churches, real people, with real problems, in real cities. That means what he wrote needed to make sense to those people **back then** — not just two thousand years later.

**What does that mean for us?** When we read Revelation, we shouldn't only ask "what happens far in the future?" We should first ask, "what was John trying to tell those Christians back then?" And most of the time, that turns out to matter for us too.

## 3. Second: a warning (prophecy)

When we hear the word "prophet," we often picture someone predicting the future like a fortune-teller. But prophets in the Bible mostly do something different: they tell people, honestly, where they're going wrong right now — and call them back to God, today, not later.

A prophet is a bit like a friend who tells you, "Hey, you're headed the wrong way — turn around before it ends badly." That's exactly what Revelation does: it comforts people who are being treated unfairly, and it wakes up people who are doing something wrong.

## 4. Third: a comic with a secret message (apocalypse)

The third and strangest ingredient: Revelation is an "apocalypse" — a fancy word that just means a book full of **big, wild pictures** that point to a deeper truth.

*(Show a political cartoon or comic panel with a dragon or monster representing something bad.)*

Look at a picture like this. Do you think a dragon like this actually exists? No! But every one of you understands right away what the picture means: something dangerous, something bad, is at work here. That's exactly how the pictures in Revelation work — the dragon, the monster with many heads, the Lamb. These aren't photographs of the future. They're pictures meant to send a message straight to your heart.

And that message is always the same kind of choice: are you on God's side, with the Lamb — or on the dragon's side? The book doesn't offer a third option.

## 5. Why this matters

Some people read Revelation only like a weather forecast for the future — "this happens, then that happens." But if we forget that it's first a letter and a warning to real people, we miss the most important question the book is asking us today: **whose side are you on — today, right now, in your everyday life?**

## 6. Memory verse

"The revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave him to show his servants what must soon take place." *(Revelation 1:1, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for this exciting, mysterious book. Help us not just to read it like a puzzle about the future, but to hear what you want to tell us today. Show us whose side we want to be on. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Bring a letter, a whistle, and a comic/cartoon picture, and let the kids guess what each one might have to do with the book of Revelation.

**Craft idea:** Each child draws their own "comic symbol" for something good (a light, a lamb, a shield) and something they want to avoid (a monster for lying or meanness). Discuss: what does your picture really mean?

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think God didn't just write this book in a plain, normal way, like the other books of the Bible?
2. Which picture from Revelation (dragon, lamb, golden city...) have you already heard of? What do you think it means?
3. What would you say to a friend who thinks Revelation is just a scary prediction about the future?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about God's Word / the Bible
- A song about being bold and brave (focus: making a clear choice)
