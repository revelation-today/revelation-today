# God Doesn't Look Away From What's Unfair

### The wrath of the Lamb

## 1. Getting started: the water balloon

Picture a water balloon slowly being filled up. At first, nothing seems to happen. But eventually, once it's completely full, just one more drop makes it pop. That pop isn't sudden or random — it's the result of something that built up over a long time.

That picture helps us understand something in Revelation called "the wrath of the Lamb."

## 2. A cry for fairness

After the four riders bring trouble to the earth, John sees the souls of people under God's altar in heaven — people who were treated unfairly because of their faith. They cry out, "How long, holy and true Lord, until you make things right?"

That's not a wish for revenge out of anger. It's a cry for **justice** — like a kid who's been treated unfairly calling out for a grown-up to come make it right.

## 3. People hide

Afterward, there's a huge shaking — the sun, moon, and stars all change — and the most powerful people on earth hide in fear, crying out to be hidden "from the wrath of the Lamb!" Here's something interesting: that fear doesn't come from Jesus attacking anyone. It comes from people realizing, themselves, what they've done wrong, and feeling ashamed — a lot like Adam and Eve hiding from God in the garden a long time ago.

## 4. What does "wrath" mean here?

The word used for "wrath" here describes something that builds up slowly over time — like the water balloon. It's not a sudden, out-of-control outburst. God has been patient for a long, long time, giving chance after chance — but eventually there comes a point where unfairness doesn't get ignored anymore.

## 5. Why this is actually good news

We might think it's strange that God has anything like "wrath" at all. But imagine: if someone keeps hurting others, lying, or taking advantage of people — wouldn't it actually be unfair if God just watched and did nothing? God's wrath is God's serious answer to real unfairness — which is genuinely good news for anyone who has ever been treated unfairly.

## 6. Memory verse

"How long, Sovereign Lord, holy and true, until you judge?" *(Revelation 6:10, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you don't just ignore unfairness. Thank you that you care about everyone who has ever been treated badly. Help us to be patient and to trust that you make things right in the end. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Fill a water balloon slowly (with supervision, ideally outside) and talk about the moment it finally pops.

**Discussion idea:** Gently talk about times the kids have felt treated unfairly, and how it feels when nobody does anything about it.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. When have you felt treated unfairly?
2. Why do you think it matters that God doesn't just ignore unfairness?
3. What does it mean to you that God's "wrath" is a serious, fair answer rather than a sudden outburst?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about God's justice
- A song about patience and trusting God in hard times
