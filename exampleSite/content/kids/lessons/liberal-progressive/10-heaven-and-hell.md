# Where All the Good Things Come From

### What heaven and hell are really about

## 1. Getting started: the flashlight

Remember a flashlight without batteries? No matter how good the flashlight is, without its power source, no light comes out. Today we're talking about a big question using a similar idea: what happens when someone is completely cut off from their power source — from God?

## 2. Heaven isn't a reward for being "good enough"

Lots of people think heaven is a prize for people who were "good enough," and hell is a punishment for people who were "bad enough." The Bible actually describes it differently. Heaven mostly means one thing: being **completely close to God**. Revelation 21 says: "Look! God's dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them... God himself will be with them." No more sadness, no more tears, no more death — because God is right there.

## 3. Where do good things actually come from?

Think about it: where do things like hope, joy, peace, and forgiveness actually come from? We sometimes think we make those things ourselves. But the Bible shows that everything truly good has its source in God — like sunlight giving us light and warmth, without us having to do anything to earn it.

## 4. So what is hell, then?

The simplest way to describe hell is this: a life completely **without** a relationship with God — forever. And because all good things come from God, a life without him means missing out on real peace, real hope, real forgiveness. That's what makes it such a hard thing to picture — not because God wants to be cruel, but because without him, all the good things we usually take for granted just aren't there.

## 5. God doesn't force anyone

Here's something important: God doesn't force anyone to stay close to him. If someone truly doesn't want to be with him, God lets them go — but the result is a life missing all the good gifts that come from him.

## 6. Why this matters

This isn't meant to scare us — it's meant to invite us. God wants to be close to us right now, today. The closer we get to him now, the more we already experience a little bit of what heaven is really about: real peace, real joy, real hope.

## 7. Memory verse

"God will wipe away every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death." *(Revelation 21:4, shortened)*

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that every good thing in our lives comes from you. Help us to grow closer to you today, so we can already experience a little bit of heaven now. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Bring back the flashlight-with-and-without-batteries idea, now connecting it to heaven and hell.

**Discussion idea:** Together, list good things in life (joy, friends, music, good food...) and talk about where they ultimately come from.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What "good thing" in your life has surprised you by ultimately coming from God?
2. Why do you think God doesn't force anyone to stay close to him?
3. What's one thing you can do today to grow closer to God?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about God's closeness as the source of all good things
- A song about hope and eternal life
