# Who Is Really in Charge?

### A story from the time when Revelation was written

## 1. Getting started: the silly game

Let's play a quick game. The rule: every time I raise my hand, you have to jump up, clap, and shout, "You're the best!" Anyone who doesn't join in has to sit out a round.

*(Play once or twice, then ask:)*

- How did that feel?
- Did the rule seem fair to you?
- What if you had to do that every single day — even if you didn't actually think I was "the best"?

The first Christians had to play a game just like that — except it was real, not for fun. That's what today is about.

## 2. Over 2,000 years ago: the emperor wants everyone to say "yes"

Back when the last book of the Bible was written, almost the whole known world belonged to the Roman Empire. At the very top was one man: the emperor.

The emperor said: "I take care of everything — safety, enough food, peace, a good life. All I ask in return is one thing: everyone has to show that I'm the greatest — almost like a god."

You could see it everywhere:
- His face was on every coin.
- There were huge statues of him.
- At festivals and celebrations, people had to raise a toast to him and praise him.

Anyone who didn't join in got into trouble — sometimes very serious trouble.

## 3. The problem: the Christians already had a King

The Christians thought: "Wait a minute. We already have a king — Jesus! And food, safety, and a good life come from God, not from the emperor." So they couldn't honestly shout, "The emperor is the greatest!"

That's a bit like if someone in your class suddenly decided, "From now on, everyone laughs at the new kid, or you're not part of the group anymore." Some kids laugh along — not because they think it's funny, but because they want to belong. That's exactly the choice the first Christians faced, every single day:

1. **Go along with it completely** and pretend they didn't believe in Jesus anymore.
2. **Say no completely** — even if that meant real trouble.
3. **Go along a little bit while secretly still believing in Jesus** — showing one thing on the outside while something different was true in their heart.

## 4. John — someone who said no

The man who wrote Revelation is named John. He tells us himself: because he wouldn't go along with it, he was sent away to a lonely island called Patmos — a kind of exile, far from home.

But right there, all alone, John is shown an enormous, encouraging picture from God: Jesus is alive, Jesus really reigns — far more than any emperor ever could. The whole book John writes afterward is meant to give Christians courage to stay with Jesus, no matter what happens.

## 5. And us today? Who is our "emperor"?

Today, nobody has to cheer for a statue of an emperor. But the same question is still around, just wearing new clothes: **who do you trust the most? Who do you most want to make happy?**

A few things to think about:

- Is it more important to you that other kids think you're cool than that you do the right thing?
- Do you feel safer with your phone or your game console than when you're talking with God?
- Do you go along with something you actually think is wrong, just so you'll belong?

Those are our "emperors" today — things or people we trust more, or care more about pleasing, than Jesus, without even fully noticing it.

## 6. Why this matters

The good news: we don't have to hide, and we don't have to go along just to belong. Jesus is already the true King — much stronger than any emperor, any trend, any pressure to fit in. He invites us to belong to him honestly, to trust him, and not to hide him.

## 7. Memory verse

"No one can serve two masters." *(Matthew 6:24)*

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you really are King — much stronger than anything that scares us or anything we want to seem "cool." Help us to stand up for you honestly, even when it's hard. Show us when we're secretly trusting something else more than you. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening game:** The "you're the best" game from section 1 — afterward, talk together about how it felt.

**Craft idea:** Each child draws a small "crown." On one side, they write or draw what sometimes acts like an "emperor" in their everyday life (phone, being cool, sweets, sports...). On the other side, they write "Jesus." Discuss: whose crown should really be the biggest?

**Questions for the kids:**
1. When is it hard for you to say "no" when others are doing something wrong?
2. What would you do if someone made fun of you for believing in Jesus?
3. What's sometimes more important to you than Jesus, even though you don't really want it to be?

**Song suggestions:**
- A simple song about trusting God with your time
- A song about being a child of the King
- A familiar movement/action song about trust and courage
