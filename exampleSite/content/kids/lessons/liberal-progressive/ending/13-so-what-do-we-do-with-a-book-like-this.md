# So, What Do We Do With This Book Now?

### Wrapping up our whole Revelation journey

---

## 1. Getting started: remember where we began?

At the very start of this series, we asked: what kind of book is Revelation, anyway? We said it uses pictures and symbols, like a cartoon, to tell us something true — not a secret code, and not just an old, dusty story that doesn't matter anymore. Now that we've gone through the whole book together, let's ask: so what do we actually do with it?

## 2. Two easy answers we're not going to give

Some people say, "Revelation was just for people a long, long time ago — it has nothing to do with us." Others say, "Revelation is a secret code, and if we're clever enough, we can figure out exactly when the world will end." We've learned that neither of those is quite right.

## 3. What we actually found

Instead, every single picture in this book turned out to be about something real, still happening today:
- The worship we talked about changes how we treat every other problem.
- The wrong kind of "wealth and power" that Babylon stood for is still tempting people today.
- The promise that God notices unfairness and injustice is still true right now.
- The beautiful ending — God living close with his people forever — is a promise, not just a nice old story.

## 4. One more look in the mirror

Remember Laodicea, the church that thought it was doing great but wasn't? That's this series' biggest lesson for all of us: it's easy to feel so comfortable and so sure we're doing fine that we stop noticing where we need to grow. Revelation invites us to look honestly at ourselves, not just at other people.

## 5. Why this matters

We don't need to be scared of this book, and we don't need to ignore it either. It's a book about God's big, unstoppable love winning in the end — and an invitation to live like that's actually true, starting today.

## 6. Memory verse

"He who was seated on the throne said, 'I am making everything new!'" *(Revelation 21:5)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for this whole journey through Revelation. Help us take it seriously, not by being scared, but by living like your love and your justice really are winning. Help us look honestly at ourselves, the way Laodicea needed to. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Look back together at any notes, drawings, or memory verses from earlier sessions in this series — let kids share what they remember most.

**Discussion idea:** Ask kids to name one thing from the whole series that changed how they think about the future, or about being kind and fair to others now.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's one picture or story from Revelation that you'll remember?
2. What does it mean to look honestly at ourselves, like the Laodicea lesson talked about?
3. What's one way you want to live differently because of what we've learned?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about God making all things new
- A closing song that celebrates the whole series
