# The Golden City With Room for Everyone

### The New Jerusalem

## 1. Getting started: the best room in the house

Think of the most special room you can imagine — maybe a room you're not usually allowed into, or a room that only one special person gets to enter. What would it feel like if suddenly that room became open to everyone, all the time?

## 2. A city coming down

At the very end of Revelation, John sees something wonderful: a whole new, glowing city called the New Jerusalem, coming down from heaven, "prepared as a bride beautifully dressed." It's described with gold, with beautiful jewels, with gates that never close.

## 3. God, right in the middle

The best part isn't the gold or the jewels. It's this: "Look! God's dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them... There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain." Every tear gets wiped away. Everything sad from before is over.

## 4. A very special shape

Here's a detail worth knowing: the city is shaped like a perfect cube. In the whole Old Testament, there's only one other place shaped exactly like that — the innermost room of the temple, called the Holy of Holies, where God's presence was so strong that only one priest could go in, and only once a year. Now, in the New Jerusalem, that same amazing closeness to God isn't reserved for one special person on one special day anymore. Everyone who belongs there gets to be that close, all the time.

## 5. Room for everyone

The gates of this city are never shut. People from every nation are welcomed in. It's not a locked, exclusive club — it's an open, welcoming home, big enough for everyone who wants to come.

## 6. Why this matters

This is where the whole story of Revelation has been heading all along: not fear, not endless disaster, but a bright, welcoming, golden home where God lives right in the middle of everything, and everyone gets to be as close to him as anyone could ever be.

## 7. Memory verse

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain." *(Revelation 21:4)*

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing a home where you'll be close to us forever, and where every sad thing will finally be over. Thank you that there's room in your city for everyone who wants to come. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Talk about a "special room" (like a principal's office or a locked museum room) that few people get to enter, then reveal that in the New Jerusalem, that kind of closeness to God is open to everyone.

**Craft idea:** Kids design their own golden city on paper, including open gates and people from many different places.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What do you imagine when you picture "every tear wiped away"?
2. Why do you think it matters that the gates of this city are never shut?
3. What's one way you can help make others feel welcome, the way this city welcomes everyone?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about heaven as our true home
- A song celebrating people from every nation gathered together
