# A Crowd Too Big to Count

### The 144,000 and the great tribulation

## 1. Getting started: hearing versus seeing

Have you ever heard about something before you actually saw it, and it turned out different than you expected? Maybe you heard a movie was scary, and then it was actually kind of funny. Or you heard a new kid was "weird," and then you met them and they turned out to be really nice. What we hear about something isn't always what we actually see when we look for ourselves.

## 2. What John hears

In Revelation 7, John *hears* a number: 144,000 people, sealed and protected by God, lined up like a great army — 12,000 from each of the twelve tribes of Israel. It sounds like a specific, countable group getting ready for battle.

## 3. What John sees

But then John actually *looks* — and what he *sees* is completely different from what he heard: "a great multitude that no one could count, from every nation, tribe, people, and language," all worshiping God together. Not a small, exact army. An enormous, uncountable, joyful crowd from absolutely everywhere.

This happens elsewhere in Revelation too — John hears about a fierce lion, and then sees a gentle lamb. The pattern keeps showing up: what sounds small, exact, or fierce turns out, when you actually look, to be much bigger and gentler than expected.

## 4. What is "the great tribulation"?

The Bible sometimes talks about a hard time called "the great tribulation." It's not one single scary event still waiting to happen far in the future. It describes something bigger: the whole stretch of time between Jesus' first coming and his return — which means it includes hard times Christians have faced throughout all of history, right up to today, whenever following Jesus costs something.

## 5. Why this is good news

If tribulation is something the whole church has faced across all of history, then whenever we or others go through something hard for following Jesus, we're not alone and we're not the first. And the promise of the 144,000 becomes something wonderful: however small or alone we might feel sometimes, the real number of people who love and worship God is enormous — bigger than anyone could ever count, gathered from absolutely everywhere.

## 6. Memory verse

"After this I looked, and there before me was a great multitude that no one could count." *(Revelation 7:9, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that we're never really alone, even when things feel hard. Thank you for the enormous, uncountable family of people who love and follow you all around the world. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Play a quick game where kids guess the number of items in a jar (candy, buttons) based only on a description, then actually count together — talk about the surprise of hearing versus seeing.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw themselves as part of a huge, colorful crowd of people from all different places, worshiping together.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has something you heard about ever turned out different — in a good way — once you actually saw it for yourself?
2. Why does it help to know that Christians everywhere, across all of history, have gone through hard times too?
3. How does it feel to know that God's family is too big for anyone to count?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about being part of God's big family
- A song celebrating people "from every nation, tribe, and tongue"
