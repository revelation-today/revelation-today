# The Room Where Everything Starts

### Worship in God's throne room

## 1. Getting started: what comes first?

Think about baking cookies. What has to happen before anything can go in the oven? *(Let kids answer — mixing the ingredients.)* If you skip that first step, nothing else works right. Today we're looking at the first thing that happens in a big part of Revelation — and it turns out everything else depends on it.

## 2. A room full of worship

Before any of the hard, stormy events happen in Revelation — before any trouble starts — John is shown something else first: a room in heaven, with God on the throne, surrounded by worship. Twenty-four elders, four wild and wonderful living creatures representing the whole of creation — a lion, an ox, a creature with a human face, and an eagle — all worshiping together. It's loud, it's joyful, and it comes *before* anything difficult in the story.

That order matters. Whatever comes later in the book, it comes out of this room — not the other way around.

## 3. Why worship comes first

Here's the neat part: right before this scene, seven churches had all sorts of problems — some believed the wrong things, some thought too highly of themselves, some had lost track of what mattered most, some were just plain worn out and discouraged. And the very next thing John sees is this one room, with one answer for every different problem: worship.

Why would worship fix so many different problems? Because when you really see how amazing God actually is, it gets a lot easier to notice what matters and what doesn't, to see yourself honestly, and to stop being so shaken by hard things — because you're standing next to something that can never be shaken.

## 4. A rainbow and a very calm sea

Two little details in this scene are worth noticing. First, there's a rainbow around the throne — the same sign God gave Noah as a promise. Second, there's a "sea of glass" in front of the throne — calm, still, like glass instead of choppy waves. In the Bible, stormy seas often stand for danger and chaos. Here, in front of God's throne, the sea is completely still. That's a picture of safety: whatever storms come later in the story, God's people are being kept safe near him.

## 5. Why this matters

Whenever we get to a hard or scary part of Revelation later on, we can remember: it all started in this room, with worship, with a rainbow of promise, and with a calm, stilled sea. Nothing that happens later erases that starting point.

## 6. Memory verse

"Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come." *(Revelation 4:8, shortened)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that everything starts with you and with worship. When things feel stormy or scary, help us remember the calm, safe room where this whole story begins. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Bring cookie ingredients (or a picture sequence) and talk about why the first step matters before anything else can happen.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw or color a rainbow with the word "promise" on it, to keep as a reminder of God's care.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What's something that helps you feel calm when things around you feel stormy?
2. Why do you think worship could be the answer to so many different kinds of problems?
3. What would it look like for you to put worshiping God "first" in a normal day?

**Song suggestions:**
- "Holy, Holy, Holy" or a simple children's version
- A song about God's promises (rainbow theme)
