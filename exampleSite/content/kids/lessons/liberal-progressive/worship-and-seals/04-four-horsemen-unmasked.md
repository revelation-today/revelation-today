# The Rider Who Wasn't Who He Looked Like

### The four horsemen unmasked

## 1. Getting started: the trick present

Imagine someone hands you a beautifully wrapped present — shiny paper, a big bow, your name on the tag. You open it up excited... and it's empty, or worse, full of something you didn't want at all. Looked amazing on the outside. Wasn't what it seemed.

## 2. Four riders appear

In Revelation 6, John sees four riders on four horses, one at a time: a white horse, a red horse, a black horse, and a pale horse. The white horse's rider looks like a winner — he's given a crown and rides out "to conquer." Sounds pretty good so far, right? A crown, a victory... it almost sounds like it could be Jesus!

## 3. The unmasking

But look at what actually follows him: war, unfair prices that hurt poor people while rich people's luxury stays untouched, and finally, death. That doesn't sound like Jesus at all — it sounds like a promise of glory that turns into disaster. The white horse's rider looked like triumph. He was actually a trick.

That's the big lesson of this whole scene: something can look amazing on the outside — a shiny crown, a big promise, a "sure win" — and still be heading somewhere bad. The way to tell the difference isn't how good something looks wrapped up. It's what it actually leaves behind.

## 4. Why this matters

Right after this scene, people cry out, "How long, God?" — they've seen through the trick, and they want things made right. That's actually a hopeful thing to notice: Revelation isn't telling us to be afraid of tricky promises forever. It's helping us learn to spot them early, and reminding us that God sees through every counterfeit, even when we don't at first.

## 5. What helps us

The best defense against a trick like this isn't cleverness — it's staying close to the real thing. The more familiar you are with what Jesus actually looks like — patient, kind, honest, self-giving — the easier it becomes to spot an imitation, no matter how shiny its wrapping paper is.

## 6. Memory verse

"Watch out that no one deceives you." *(Matthew 24:4)*

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to know you so well that we can spot a fake promise when we see one. Thank you that you always keep your word, even when things that look shiny don't. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Actually wrap an empty box beautifully and let a child open it, then talk about the surprise.

**Craft idea:** Draw two pictures side by side: something that looks good but isn't, and something that looks ordinary but is actually good and true (a kind word, a small honest choice).

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Can you think of a time something looked exciting but turned out to be a letdown?
2. How can we get better at telling the difference between a real promise and a shiny trick?
3. What's one way you can get to know Jesus better this week, so his voice becomes more familiar to you?

**Song suggestions:**
- A song about knowing and following Jesus' voice
- A song about truth and trust
