# God Wants to Live Close to You
### From the garden to the altar to you

---

## 1. Opening: Closer and closer

*(Point to the altar area and the icon screen if visible, or describe it.)*

In our church, have you ever noticed how everything is arranged to lead your eyes and your steps toward the altar? That's not by accident. Today's story is about how, from the very beginning of the Bible to the very end, God has been trying to get closer and closer to us.

---

## 2. The garden — God right there with us

At the very start of the Bible, God made a garden and walked with Adam and Eve there. God wasn't far away in the sky — he was right there with them, close, like a friend. But Adam and Eve disobeyed God, and they couldn't stay in the garden with him anymore. That's the saddest moment in the whole story.

---

## 3. God builds a tent to be near us again

Did God give up? No! When God rescued his people from Egypt, he told them to build a special tent — and later a temple — so he could live close to them again, even though they had to leave the garden. Deep inside that temple was a super holy room where God's presence lived. Only one priest could go in, and only once a year.

That's a bit like our altar area today — it's set apart because it's extra holy, a place where heaven and earth meet.

---

## 4. Jesus is the real Temple

Here's the amazing part: Jesus said his own body was like a temple (John 2:19-21). When Jesus died on the cross, the curtain in front of that super-holy room in the temple tore in half — top to bottom! That was God's way of saying: "Now everyone can come close to me, not just one priest, one day a year."

---

## 5. We get to be close to God now — for real!

This is why we believe something beautiful and wonderful in the Orthodox Church: God doesn't just want to forgive us and leave us alone. He wants us to actually share in his life — to become like him, full of his light and love. That's called *theosis*, becoming more and more like God by his grace. It's the whole point of the story that started way back in the garden!

---

## 6. Memory verse

"Look! God's dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them." *(Revelation 21:3)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for wanting to be close to us so much that you sent Jesus. Thank you that we can come near to you now. Help us become more like you every day. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** If possible, briefly show or describe the layout of the church (nave, icon screen, altar) as a visual for "closer and closer to God."

**Craft idea:** Have children draw three pictures side by side: the garden of Eden, the temple, and a church — connecting the dots of God always finding a way to be close to his people.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think Adam and Eve couldn't stay in the garden after they disobeyed?
2. What does it mean that Jesus's body is like a "temple"?
3. What's one way you can feel close to God this week?

**Song suggestions:**
- A children's song about God's presence or "Emmanuel, God with us"
- A simple hymn connected to Communion or nearness to God
