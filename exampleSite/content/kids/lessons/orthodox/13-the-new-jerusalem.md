# The Whole City Is Holy
### Our forever home with God

---

## 1. Opening: The most special room

In the Old Testament temple, there was one super-special room called the Holy of Holies, where God's presence lived. Only the high priest could go in, and only once a year! Everyone else had to stay outside. Today's lesson is about a city where that's not true anymore — where EVERYONE gets to be as close to God as that one priest used to be.

---

## 2. A city shaped like the holiest room

John sees a beautiful new city coming down from heaven — the New Jerusalem. It's shaped like a perfect cube. Guess what else was shaped exactly like a cube? The Holy of Holies! That's not an accident. It means the whole city is as holy and close to God as that one special room used to be.

---

## 3. Everyone gets to be close

This is the amazing part: in the New Jerusalem, there's no "closer" or "farther" from God anymore. Everybody who's there gets to be as close to God as the high priest once was. No more waiting outside, no more staying far away — everyone is welcomed all the way in!

---

## 4. No more tears, no more sea

The Bible says there will be "no more death or mourning or crying or pain" (Revelation 21:4), and also "no more sea" — because the sea was where scary, dangerous things came from in the Bible. In the New Jerusalem, there's nothing left to be afraid of.

---

## 5. Gates that never close

The city's twelve gates never shut, because there's no more danger, no more night, nothing bad left outside trying to get in. It's completely safe, forever.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain." *(Revelation 21:4)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing a forever home where we can be close to you always. Help us look forward to that day, and help us live close to you now too. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** Use a small box (a cube) to show the shape, comparing it to descriptions of the Holy of Holies and then the New Jerusalem.

**Craft idea:** Have kids build or draw a simple "city" using cube shapes (sugar cubes, blocks, or drawn squares), decorating it with "gold and jewels" (glitter, stickers) to represent the New Jerusalem.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think the New Jerusalem is shaped like the Holy of Holies?
2. What does it mean that "everyone" gets to be close to God there, not just one priest?
3. What are you most looking forward to about heaven?

**Song suggestions:**
- A children's song about heaven or our forever home with God
- "When We All Get to Heaven" or similar, adapted simply
