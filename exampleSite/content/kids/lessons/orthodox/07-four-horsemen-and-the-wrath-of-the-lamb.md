# The Trick Rider and the Patient Lamb
### Four horses and a big misunderstanding

---

## 1. Opening: Guess who?

*(Play a simple guessing game: describe someone dressed like a hero — cape, crown, sword — and ask kids if that automatically means the person is good. Then reveal a "trick" — sometimes a costume fools you!)*

Sometimes someone can look like a good guy on the outside but not be one at all. That's exactly what happens with the first of four horsemen in Revelation.

---

## 2. Four horses ride out

When Jesus (the Lamb) opens the first four seals on the scroll, four horses ride out one at a time: white, red, black, and pale (a sickly color). Each rider brings trouble: the first rides out "to conquer," the second brings fighting, the third brings unfairness (rich people keep their fancy food while poor people can barely afford bread), and the fourth brings death.

---

## 3. The first horse is a trick

Here's the surprising part: even though the first horse is white — a color we usually think of as good — this rider is NOT Jesus in disguise. He's actually pretending to be like Jesus! He rides out looking like a winner and a hero, but he's actually the start of trouble, not the solution to it. It's a costume, a trick — just like Jesus warned: "watch out, so that no one deceives you."

---

## 4. Why "the wrath of the Lamb"?

After all this trouble, people are terrified, and they beg mountains to fall on them to hide from "the wrath of the Lamb." But here's something important to notice: this is what scared, guilty people *think* Jesus is going to do to them — not necessarily what he actually does. Jesus doesn't ride out with a sword like the scary horsemen. His judgment is serious, but he doesn't act like the tricksters people just watched cause so much harm.

---

## 5. God hears "how long?"

Right after the horsemen, some people who died for loving Jesus cry out, "How long until you make things right, God?" And God doesn't ignore them. This matters a lot for people who are treated unfairly or hurt because of their faith — including Christians in some parts of the world today. God sees. God hears. And God will make things right, even if it takes time.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"How long, Sovereign Lord, holy and true, until you judge... and avenge our blood?" *(Revelation 6:10)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to see through tricks that look good but aren't really good. Thank you that you hear us when we cry "how long?" and that you always keep your promises. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** A simple "costume vs. reality" guessing game as described above, to set up the theme of the first horseman being a trick.

**Craft idea:** Draw four horses of different colors and label what each one brings, discussing which one is the sneaky one.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think the first horse looked good but wasn't?
2. How can we tell the difference between something that looks good and something that really is good?
3. Do you know anyone who might be praying "how long, God?" right now? How can we pray for them?

**Song suggestions:**
- A children's song about God's justice or faithfulness
- A simple song about waiting on God
