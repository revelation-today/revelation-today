# The Copycat Trinity
### Learning to spot a fake

---

## 1. Opening: Real or fake?

*(Bring two similar-looking items — a real coin and a toy coin, or a real flower and a plastic one — and ask kids to guess which is real.)*

Sometimes a fake looks a lot like the real thing at first glance. Today's story from Revelation 13 is about the biggest fake of all: a copycat version of God, made by the devil, that tries to trick people into worshiping it instead.

---

## 2. A quick word about icons

Before we go further, let's be really clear about something: the "images" and "worship" that Revelation 13 warns against are NOT about our icons at church. We honor icons because Jesus really became a real, physical person — that's totally different from what Revelation is warning about, which is a statue of a Roman emperor that people were forced to bow down to as if he were God. Our icons point us truly to Jesus and the saints; the beast's statue was a lie pretending to be powerful.

---

## 3. The dragon and two copycat "helpers"

In Revelation 13, the devil (the dragon) works with two "beasts" — kind of like helpers. Together, the three of them try to copy the real Holy Trinity:
- The dragon gives power to the first beast, like the Father gives power to Jesus.
- The first beast looks like it died and came back to life, copying Jesus's resurrection.
- The second beast makes everyone worship the first beast, copying how the Holy Spirit points people to Jesus.

It's a costume — a fake trinity trying to look like the real one!

---

## 4. What was this really about?

For the people who first read Revelation, this wasn't confusing at all — they knew exactly what it meant! The Roman Emperor demanded to be worshiped like a god, and there were people all over the empire helping enforce that — through parades, statues, and pressure to join in. If you didn't go along with it, you could lose your job, your friends, or worse.

---

## 5. How do you spot the fake?

Here's the secret test: the second beast "had horns like a lamb, but it spoke like a dragon" (Revelation 13:11). It LOOKED gentle like Jesus, but its voice gave it away. Jesus says his sheep know his voice (John 10:4-5). So how do we spot a fake today? We listen carefully for love versus fear. God's way always involves real love, never forcing people through fear or tricks.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me." *(John 10:27)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to know your true voice and not be fooled by things that only look powerful or good on the outside. Thank you for showing us love instead of fear. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** Real-vs-fake object guessing game as described above, to introduce the "copycat" theme.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw a simple triangle labeled "Father, Son, Holy Spirit" next to a scribbled-out triangle labeled "dragon, beast, false prophet" to visualize the parody.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think the devil tries to copy God instead of just being obviously different?
2. What's the difference between an icon that helps us worship Jesus and the emperor's statue that demanded worship for itself?
3. How can we practice listening for Jesus's true voice this week?

**Song suggestions:**
- "My Sheep Know My Voice" or similar
- A children's song about following Jesus
