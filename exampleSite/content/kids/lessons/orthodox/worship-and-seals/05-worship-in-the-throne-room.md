# The Icon Come to Life
### The throne room you already know

---

## 1. Opening: Have you seen this before?

*(Show an icon of Christ enthroned, or the "Christ in Majesty" type icon, if one is available in your parish.)*

Look at this icon. Do you see Jesus on a throne? Maybe creatures around him, or rays of light? Here's something amazing: John, who wrote the book of Revelation, saw this exact kind of scene — for real, in a vision from heaven. Our icons have been showing us John's vision this whole time!

---

## 2. What John saw

John looked through a door into heaven and saw God on a throne, shining like jewels, with a rainbow above it. All around were twenty-four elders wearing crowns, and four amazing living creatures — one like a lion, one like an ox, one like a man, and one like an eagle. Everyone was singing, "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord!" This sounds a lot like what we sing and see in our own church, doesn't it?

---

## 3. Why worship first?

Right before this vision, Jesus had just finished telling seven churches about their problems — some had stopped loving him first, some had let bad teaching sneak in, some just looked good on the outside. But here's the answer to every single one of those problems: **worship**. When we truly worship God and see how amazing he is, our other problems start looking smaller, and we remember who's really in charge.

---

## 4. The Lion who is a Lamb

Here's the most surprising part. John hears someone say, "The Lion has won!" So he looks for a lion... but instead he sees a Lamb — one that looks like it was hurt, but is alive and standing. The Lion and the Lamb are the same person: Jesus! He won not by being the strongest fighter, but by giving up his life out of love. That's why in so many of our icons, Christ is shown gentle and glorious at the same time.

---

## 5. A rainbow of promise

Do you remember Noah? God gave a rainbow as a promise never to flood the earth again. In John's vision, there's a rainbow over God's throne too — even before anything scary happens later in the book. That's a clue: everything that happens afterward happens under God's promise of mercy. God is not out of control, and he's not just angry — he's still keeping his promises.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come." *(Revelation 4:8)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for showing us your throne room through your Word and through our icons. Help us remember to worship you first, before we worry about anything else. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** Bring an icon of "Christ in Majesty" / Christ enthroned, or a picture of one, as the central visual anchor for this whole lesson.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw their own simple "throne room" picture with a lamb, a rainbow, and worshipers, using the icon as inspiration.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What part of our church worship reminds you of John's vision in heaven?
2. Why do you think Jesus is shown as both a Lion and a Lamb?
3. What's one way you can put worship "first" this week, before worrying about a problem?

**Song suggestions:**
- "Holy, Holy, Holy" or the Trisagion
- A children's song about the Lamb of God
