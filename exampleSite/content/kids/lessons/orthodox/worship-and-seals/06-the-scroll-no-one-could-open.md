# The Lion Who Was a Lamb
### The biggest surprise in the whole Bible

---

## 1. Opening: A locked scroll

*(Bring a rolled-up piece of paper "sealed" with a sticker or piece of tape, and pass it around.)*

Imagine a very important scroll — like a king's special letter — sealed shut with seven seals so no one could open it without permission. In John's vision, he sees exactly this kind of scroll in heaven. A voice asks: "Who is worthy to open this scroll?" And... no one can. Not one single person, not even an angel. John starts crying because it seems like nothing good can happen next.

---

## 2. Someone can!

Then someone tells John, "Don't cry! The Lion of Judah has won — he can open the scroll!" John must have thought, "A Lion! That sounds powerful and scary — perfect for winning a big battle!"

But when John actually looks... he doesn't see a lion at all. He sees a Lamb — one that looks like it was hurt, but is standing up, alive. Surprise! The Lion and the Lamb are the same person: **Jesus**.

---

## 3. Winning by giving, not by fighting

This is one of the most important surprises in the whole Bible. Everyone expects a hero to win by being the strongest or the scariest. But Jesus won differently — by giving up his own life out of love, and then rising from the dead. That's why in our icons, we sometimes see Jesus as both gentle, like a lamb, and glorious, like a king — because he's both at the same time!

---

## 4. Prayers that are heard

When the scroll starts to open, John sees something else important: people who died because they loved Jesus are asking God, "How long until things are made right?" And God doesn't ignore them — he tells them to wait a little longer, and he gives them white robes, a sign that he heard them and cares.

If you know people who suffer for following Jesus — maybe in places far away, or even people from your own family's story — this is a good reminder: **God hears every prayer, even the ones that feel like they take a long time to be answered.**

---

## 5. Memory verse

"Do not weep! See, the Lion of the tribe of Judah... has triumphed." *(Revelation 5:5)*

---

## 6. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for winning not by being scary, but by loving us all the way to the cross and back to life. Help us trust that you hear our prayers, even when we have to wait. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** Bring a "sealed" scroll (rolled paper with tape/stickers as seals) to open dramatically at the right moment in the story.

**Craft idea:** Have kids fold a small paper in half — draw a lion on one side and a lamb on the other — to remember that Jesus is both.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think everyone expected the "Lion" to be scary and strong, and were surprised to see a Lamb?
2. What does it mean that Jesus won by giving his life instead of fighting?
3. Can you think of someone who is waiting for God to answer a prayer? How can we pray for them?

**Song suggestions:**
- "Behold the Lamb of God" or similar
- Any children's song about Jesus as both King and Savior
