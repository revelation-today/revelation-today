# Close to God, or Far Away
### What heaven and hell are really about

---

## 1. Opening: Near or far?

*(Have kids stand close together, then have them slowly walk far apart. Ask: "Which feels warmer and safer — being close together or far apart?")*

Being close to someone who loves you feels good. Being far away and alone can feel scary. Today's lesson is about heaven and hell — and it's really about that same idea: being close to God, or being far from him.

---

## 2. Heaven isn't just a "nice place"

A lot of people think heaven is just a pretty place you get if you're good enough. But the Bible describes heaven mostly as being close to God — really, truly close, like being married to him (that's why the New Jerusalem is even called "the Bride"). It's not about a reward far away from God. It's about finally being as near to him as possible, forever.

---

## 3. Hell isn't a punishment room

Hell isn't described as a "punishment machine" God builds to hurt people. It's simpler and sadder than that: hell is just what it's like to be far away from God — with none of the good things that come from him, like joy, peace, hope, and forgiveness.

---

## 4. We can't fully explain everything — and that's okay

Here's something wonderful about being Orthodox: we don't try to explain every single detail about what happens after we die. We trust God with the mystery, and we focus on what we do know clearly: God wants to be close to us, and he never forces anyone to love him back.

---

## 5. Never a scare tactic

Some people use "hell" just to scare others into believing — like a threat. But that's not how our Church teaches it. God doesn't want us to follow him out of fear. He wants us to want to be close to him because we love him, the same way we love our families.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"God's dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them." *(Revelation 21:3)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for wanting to be close to us forever. Help us to love and follow you because we want to be near you, not because we're scared. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** The "close together / far apart" movement activity described above works well as a simple physical metaphor before discussing heaven and hell.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw two pictures: one of themselves close to Jesus (holding hands, sitting together) and talk about how that feels compared to being alone.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think being close to someone who loves you feels different from being far away and alone?
2. What are some good things that come from being close to God (joy, peace, hope)?
3. How do you want to grow closer to God this week?

**Song suggestions:**
- A children's song about God's nearness or presence
- "Emmanuel, God With Us" or similar
