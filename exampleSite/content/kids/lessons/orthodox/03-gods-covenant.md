# The Promise God Kept for Both of Us
### A story about a very unusual promise

---

## 1. Opening: A promise between two people

Imagine two people making a big promise to each other — like "I will always help you, and you will always be loyal to me." Long ago, this kind of serious promise was called a covenant. If you broke a covenant, something bad was supposed to happen to you.

There was even a strange, scary way people used to make these promises: they would cut animals in half, lay the pieces in two lines, and then walk between them. It meant: "If I break this promise, let what happened to these animals happen to me."

---

## 2. God makes a promise with Abraham

In the book of Genesis, God made a covenant with Abraham exactly this way — animals cut in half, laid out in two rows. But here's the surprising part: it wasn't Abraham who walked between the pieces. **It was God.**

Think about that. God was the strong one. Abraham was the one who might break the promise (and he did, more than once!). But God said, in a sense, "If this promise gets broken, I will take the punishment — not you."

---

## 3. This points to Jesus

Does that remind you of anything? It should! Later, God's own Son, Jesus, took on himself the punishment we deserved for breaking God's rules. That's exactly what we remember every time we receive Communion — Jesus's blood, given for a promise (a covenant) that God keeps even when we don't.

---

## 4. God rescues first, then gives the rules

When God gave his people the Ten Commandments, notice the order: God rescued them from slavery in Egypt **first**. Only after that did he give them the rules to live by. That's important! God didn't say "follow these rules and then I'll rescue you." He rescued them because he loved them — and then invited them to live in a way that fit their new freedom.

That's exactly how it works for us too. God loves us first. Then we get to respond by living for him — not to earn his love, but because we already have it.

---

## 5. A special Bible word: hesed

There's a Hebrew word, *hesed*, that means something like "faithful love that doesn't give up." Even when God's people broke their promises again and again, God kept being faithful. That's the same faithful love God has for you.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"This is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many." *(Mark 14:24)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for keeping your promise even when we break ours. Thank you for rescuing us first and loving us before we do anything to deserve it. Help us love you back. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for preparation

**Opening idea:** Act out (with two willing adults, not real cutting!) the idea of two people making a serious promise, perhaps using a simple handshake ceremony, to set up the "who walks through the middle" surprise.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw a cup (chalice) and write "God's Promise Kept" underneath, to connect the ancient covenant idea to Communion.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think it was so surprising that God, not Abraham, walked through the animal pieces?
2. Can you think of a time someone kept a promise to you even when it was hard for them?
3. How does knowing God rescues us first, before we're "good enough," make you feel?

**Song suggestions:**
- A simple song about God's faithfulness or steadfast love
- A Communion-themed hymn appropriate for children
