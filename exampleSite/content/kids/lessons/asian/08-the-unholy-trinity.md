# The Copycat Villain
### The dragon and his two beasts

---

## 1. Warm-up: Copycats

Ask the kids: Has anyone ever copied something you did, but did it in a mean or fake way — like pretending to be your friend but actually not being nice? Today's story is about the biggest copycat villain in the whole Bible.

---

## 2. Three against one family

After the dragon (Satan) fails to catch the baby Jesus, he goes after Jesus's family — that's us, the church! He brings along two helpers: one beast that comes "from the sea" and one that comes "from the earth." Together, the dragon and the two beasts try to look just like God, Jesus, and the Holy Spirit — but it's all fake, a copy of the real thing.

---

## 3. What the copy looks like

The first beast looks like it can't be killed — just like how, back then, when one emperor died, a new one always took his place, so "the beast" seemed to live forever. Everyone was amazed and followed it. The second beast makes people worship the first beast and puts a mark on their hands and heads — but it's really just a fake copy of the real mark of belonging to God.

---

## 4. How can you tell the difference?

Here's the trick: from a distance, the fake trinity and the real one can look alike. But the Bible tells us exactly how to spot the difference — "it had two horns like a lamb, but it spoke like a dragon" (Revelation 13:11). The voice gives it away. Jesus says his sheep know his voice (John 10:1-5). The real question isn't which one looks stronger or safer — it's which voice you actually listen to and follow.

---

## 5. Why this matters for us

Sometimes there are rules or pressures around us that want everyone to look the same, act the same, and give up something important just to fit in or stay safe. That's the same trick this copycat villain uses — looking normal, even reasonable, "just go along with it." Revelation reminds us: we don't have to be tricked. We can learn to recognize Jesus's true voice, even when a very convincing copy is standing right next to it.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me." *(John 10:27)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us learn to recognize your real voice, even when something fake is trying to look just like you. Give us courage to follow you, even if it means not going along with the crowd. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Play a simple "spot the fake" game — show two very similar objects (a real fruit and a plastic one, for example) and let kids guess which is real.

**Craft idea:** Draw two crowns side by side — one labeled "Jesus," one labeled "Copycat" — and talk about what's different underneath the surface, even if they look similar.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has anyone ever tried to talk you into something that seemed okay but felt wrong deep down?
2. How do you think you can tell the difference between a "good voice" and one that just sounds good?
3. What helps you remember to listen to Jesus, especially when it's hard?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about listening to God's voice
- A song about standing firm and not being fooled
