# A Promise That Never Breaks
### God's covenant — a story of amazing grace

---

## 1. Warm-up: What makes a family a family?

Ask the kids: What are the different ways someone becomes part of a family? *(Being born into it, being adopted into it, marrying into it...)* Today we're talking about a fourth way the Bible describes — a very special kind of promise called a "covenant."

---

## 2. How ancient promises worked

Long ago, when a strong king made a peace agreement with a weaker nation, it worked like this: the strong king promised to protect the weaker nation, and the weaker nation promised to obey the strong king's rules. To make the promise extra serious, they would sometimes cut animals in half and lay the pieces in two rows. Then the weaker side would walk between the pieces — as if to say, "If I break this promise, let this happen to me."

---

## 3. God does something surprising

When God made his special promise — his covenant — with Abraham, the same kind of ceremony happened: animals cut in half, laid out in rows. But here's the surprising part: it wasn't Abraham who walked between the pieces. It was God himself, shown as a fire, who walked through. God was saying: "If this promise gets broken, let it cost me — not you."

And that's exactly what happened. When people broke God's promise again and again, it was God's own Son, Jesus, who took the punishment instead of us.

---

## 4. God, our Father who keeps promises

God calls himself the Father of his people (Exodus 3:6) — and even though he had every right to walk away when his people broke their promises, he stayed faithful. The Bible has a special word for this: "hesed" — it means love, loyalty, and mercy all mixed together, the kind of love that doesn't quit.

---

## 5. Why this matters for us

Many of you know just how important family is — not just as a nice idea, but as the thing that holds your whole life together. Maybe some of you have even faced a hard cost for following Jesus within your own family — someone who was upset, or a relationship that got harder. If that's you: God's covenant means you have not lost your family. You have gained the most faithful Father there is — one who has already promised, and already paid, to keep you close forever.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"I will be their God, and they will be my people." *(based on Leviticus 26:12)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for being a Father who never breaks his promises. Thank you that Jesus took the punishment we deserved so we could always belong to you. Help us trust you like family — because we are. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Talk together about promises kids have made or received (a pinky promise, a family rule) — what makes a promise easy or hard to keep?

**Craft idea:** Make a simple paper chain with two links joined together, labeled "God" and "me" — a picture of the covenant that can't be broken.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has anyone ever broken a promise to you? How did that feel?
2. Why do you think it matters that God kept his promise even when we didn't?
3. What does it mean to you that God calls himself your Father?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about God as our Father
- A song about God's faithfulness or promises
