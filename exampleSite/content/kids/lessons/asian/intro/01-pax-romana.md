# Who Is Really the Boss?
### A story from the time when Revelation was written

---

## 1. Warm-up: The "You're the Best" Game

Let's play a quick game. The rule: every time I raise my hand, you have to jump up, clap, and shout "You're the best!" Anyone who doesn't join in has to sit out a round.

*(Play once or twice, then ask:)*

- How did that feel?
- Did the rule feel fair?
- What if you had to do that every single day — even on days you didn't feel like it was true?

That's a game. But long ago, real Christians had to play something like it for real. That's what we're talking about today.

---

## 2. Long ago: the emperor wants everyone to say "yes"

When the last book of the Bible — Revelation — was written, almost the whole known world belonged to one empire, ruled by one man: the emperor.

The emperor said: "I take care of everything — safety, food, peace, a good life. All I ask in return is that everyone shows that I'm the greatest — almost like a god."

You could see it everywhere: his face on every coin, huge statues of him, toasts to him at every big meal or celebration. Anyone who didn't join in could get into serious trouble.

---

## 3. The problem: Christians already had a King

The Christians thought: "Wait a minute. We already have a King — Jesus! And our food, our safety, our good life come from God, not from the emperor." So they couldn't honestly shout, "The emperor is the greatest!"

That's a little like if someone in your class suddenly announced, "From today, everyone laughs at the new kid, or you're not part of our group anymore." Some kids might laugh along anyway — not because it's funny, but because they want to belong. That was the choice Christians faced, every single day:

1. **Fully join in** and pretend they didn't believe in Jesus anymore.
2. **Fully say no** — even if that meant real trouble.
3. **Join in a little bit, and secretly keep believing in Jesus** — showing one thing on the outside while something different is true in their heart.

---

## 4. John — someone who said no

The man who wrote Revelation is named John. He tells us himself: because he wouldn't join in, he was sent away to a lonely island called Patmos — far from home, all alone.

But right there, all alone, God gives John an enormous, encouraging vision: Jesus is alive, Jesus really rules — far more than any emperor ever could. The whole book John writes afterward is meant to give Christians courage to stay with Jesus, no matter what happens.

---

## 5. And us today? Who is our "emperor"?

Today, nobody makes us cheer for a statue of an emperor. But the same question is still here, wearing new clothes: **Who do you trust the most? Who are you most trying to please?**

Some things to think about:

- Is it more important to you that other kids think you're cool than that you do the right thing?
- Do you feel safer with your phone or your games in hand than when you're talking with God?
- Do you go along with something you actually think is wrong, just so you'll still belong?

Those are our "emperors" today — things or people we end up trusting more, or caring more about pleasing, than Jesus — often without even noticing it.

For some of you, this isn't just a game or a "someday" question. Maybe your family gathers quietly to worship, or maybe you're the only Christian kid in your class, in a place where most people follow a different faith. If that's you: what John learned on that lonely island is for you too. Jesus is really King, even when you have to be quiet about it.

---

## 6. The frog in the warming water

Do you know the story about the frog in water? Drop a frog straight into boiling water, and it jumps right out. But warm the water up ever so slowly, and the frog might not notice the danger until it's too late. *(This isn't quite true about real frogs, but it's a good picture.)*

It can happen to us the same slow way: nobody forces us to forget Jesus all at once. It happens bit by bit — one little excuse here, one little "just this once" there — until we don't even notice anymore who's really become the "boss" of our lives.

---

## 7. What helps us

Here's the good news: we don't have to hide, and we don't have to join in just to belong. Jesus is already the true King — stronger than any emperor, any trend, any pressure to fit in. He invites us to belong to him honestly, to trust him, and to never hide who we follow.

**Memory verse:** "No one can serve two masters." *(Matthew 6:24)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are really the King — stronger than anything that scares us or anything we want to look cool for. Help us to stand honestly with you, even when it's hard. Show us when we're secretly trusting something else more than you. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening game:** The "You're the Best" game above (section 1) — afterward, talk together about how it felt.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Have each child draw a small crown. On one side, they write or draw what sometimes acts like an "emperor" in their life (a phone, being cool, treats, a game). On the other side, they write "Jesus." Discuss: whose crown should really be the biggest?

**Questions for the kids:**
1. When is it hard to say "no" when others are doing something wrong?
2. What would you do if someone made fun of you for believing in Jesus?
3. What sometimes feels more important to you than Jesus, even though you don't really want it to be?

**Song ideas:**
- A simple song about trusting God with our whole lives
- "I Am a Child of the King" or a similar song about Jesus as King
- A well-known action song about trust and courage
