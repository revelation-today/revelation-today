# Lukewarm Water
### The church in Laodicea

---

## 1. Warm-up: Hot, cold, or lukewarm?

*(Materials: three cups of water — hot, cold, and lukewarm — to look at, not necessarily to drink.)*

Hot water is great for tea or for a healing soak. Cold water is refreshing on a hot day. But lukewarm water? Nobody wants that — you almost spit it back out!

That's the exact picture Jesus uses for a church with a serious problem it doesn't even realize it has.

---

## 2. The richest, saddest church

Laodicea was a very wealthy city — famous for fine wool and good eye medicine. The church there thought, "We're doing great, we don't need anything." But Laodicea is the **only** one of the seven churches that gets **no praise at all** from Jesus.

---

## 3. The problem: lukewarm and blind to it

Jesus tells them plainly: "You say, I am rich... not realizing that you are wretched, pitiful, poor, blind, and naked." They were so sure of themselves that they couldn't see their own problems anymore. They were "lukewarm" — not really cold (openly against God) and not really hot (full of passion for him), just comfortably stuck in between.

---

## 4. A gentle note here

Jesus uses a very strong picture with this church — even mentioning being "naked," which is his way of saying "you have nothing to be proud of, and you don't even know it." That's a strong picture, and we don't need to imagine it in detail or dwell on it. The important part isn't the picture of being exposed — it's the deeper question underneath it: **do I really know what's going on in my own heart, or have I just gotten comfortable?**

That's a question best asked quietly, just between you and Jesus — not something we need to say out loud in front of the group today.

---

## 5. The surprisingly loving invitation

You might expect Jesus to be angry after such strong words. But then comes one of the gentlest sentences in the whole Bible: "Behold, I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in." That's not a threat — it's a loving invitation, like a best friend who just wants to spend time with you again.

---

## 6. Your turn — quietly

Instead of talking out loud about where we might be "lukewarm," let's each take a quiet moment with a card and a pencil. Just for yourself — no one else will read it. You might write or draw:

- One thing I've gotten comfortable about, without really thinking about it.
- One honest sentence I want to say to Jesus right now.

Fold up your card and keep it. You can pray over it this week. This is just between you and Jesus.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"Behold, I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in." *(Revelation 3:20)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, show us honestly, gently, where we've become lukewarm without noticing. Thank you for still knocking on our door with love instead of walking away. We open the door to you. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Show the three cups of water (hot, cold, lukewarm) and have kids explain what each is good for.

**Craft/reflection idea:** Give each child a small card shaped like a door that opens, with Jesus's words from Revelation 3:20 written inside. Pair this with the quiet, private writing time in section 6 — do not ask children to share what they wrote aloud.

**Questions for the kids (for private reflection, not necessarily shared aloud):**
1. When do you feel "hot" (excited) about Jesus, and when do you feel more "lukewarm"?
2. Why do you think it's so hard to notice when we've become too comfortable?
3. What does it mean to you that Jesus knocks with love instead of getting angry and walking away?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about fresh devotion or passion for Jesus
- "Open the Eyes of My Heart" or a similar song
