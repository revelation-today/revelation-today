# Seven Letters, One Big Message
### Jesus writes to seven real churches

---

## 1. Warm-up: A letter just for you

Ask the kids: Has anyone ever written you a letter or card just for you? How did it feel to read something written especially to you? Jesus does exactly that in Revelation — he writes seven real letters to seven real churches.

---

## 2. Who gets the letters?

Each letter starts with "Write to the angel of the church in..." That sounds like it's written to an angel, but it's really just a way of talking to the whole church together, as one. When Jesus writes to "the angel," he's really writing to everyone in that church — together, as a team.

---

## 3. The pattern in every letter

Each of the seven letters follows the same pattern: Jesus says something he likes about the church, then something he's concerned about, and then he makes a promise. He does this because he loves these churches and wants them to keep shining like lights, the way he's called them to.

---

## 4. A gentle word before we go further

In the next few weeks, we'll look closely at some of these seven churches — especially one called Laodicea, which had a big problem it didn't even know about. Jesus is very direct with these churches — he says exactly what's wrong so they can fix it before it's too late, like a doctor who has to tell you clearly what's making you sick.

But here's something important: Jesus's goal was never to embarrass anyone in front of everyone else. His goal was to help each church see clearly and get better. So when we look at our own hearts using these letters, we're going to do that quietly — just between you and Jesus — instead of saying out loud in front of everyone what we think we're doing wrong. That's not because it's a secret to be ashamed of. It's because some things are between you and Jesus first.

---

## 5. Why this matters

Jesus knows everything about every church — the good and the hard parts — and he still calls each one "the one I love" (Revelation 3:19). That's true for your church too, and it's true for you.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"Those whom I love, I reprove and discipline." *(Revelation 3:19)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for caring enough about your churches to write to them honestly. Help us to look at our own hearts honestly too, quietly, just between us and you. Thank you that you love us even when you see the parts that need to change. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Bring a real envelope addressed to "you" (the group) and read it aloud as if Jesus wrote it, then transition into today's topic.

**Reflection idea:** Give each child a small card and a pencil. Ask them to write or draw, privately, one thing they'd want to tell Jesus about their own heart this week. They keep the card — no one else needs to see it.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think Jesus writes to churches instead of just individual people?
2. Why do you think it matters that Jesus tells the truth kindly, instead of just being nice or just being harsh?
3. Is there something you'd want to say to Jesus privately today, using your card?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about God's love and truth together
- A song about being known and loved by Jesus
