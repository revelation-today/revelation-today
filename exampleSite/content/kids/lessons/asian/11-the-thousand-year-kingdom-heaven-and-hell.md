# Kings and Priests, Starting Now
### The thousand years, and what heaven and hell really are

---

## 1. Warm-up: What does a king do?

Ask the kids: What do you picture when you imagine a king or queen — a crown, a throne, ruling over a kingdom? Today we're going to find out that Revelation says something surprising: God's people are already reigning as kings and queens, right now, even if it doesn't look like it.

---

## 2. The thousand years

In Revelation 20, John sees believers reigning with Jesus for a thousand years. That number isn't meant to be counted out on a calendar — it's a way of describing the whole long stretch of time between Jesus's first coming and his second coming, which is the time we're living in right now.

Who gets to reign? The passage describes people who stayed faithful to Jesus no matter what it cost them — even those who lost their lives for it. But it also includes John, sent away to a lonely island, and "all of us, made kings and priests" (Revelation 1:6). That means anyone who stays loyal to Jesus is already reigning with him — whether that loyalty costs you everything, or costs you something smaller, like standing out at school or in your family.

---

## 3. What is heaven really like?

Heaven isn't just a reward for being good. Revelation describes heaven as the New Jerusalem — a place where God lives with his people forever, close up, with nothing in between. It's shaped like a perfect cube, just like the most holy room in the old temple, the one place only a priest could enter, and only once a year. In heaven, everyone gets to be that close to God, all the time.

---

## 4. What is hell really like?

Hell isn't described as a place God enjoys sending people to. It's simply what happens when someone chooses to live without God forever — and everything good we enjoy now (hope, joy, peace, forgiveness) actually comes from him in the first place. God never forces anyone to be close to him. But choosing to stay far from him means missing out on everything good that comes from being near him.

---

## 5. Why this matters for us

If following Jesus is costing you something right now — maybe it's hard at home, hard with friends, hard to be different — Revelation says something amazing: you are already reigning with Christ, this very moment, not just someday far away. Your faithfulness isn't wasted while you wait. It's already a kind of royal life.

---

## 6. Memory verse

"You have made them a kingdom and priests to our God, and they shall reign on the earth." *(Revelation 5:10)*

---

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that we don't have to wait to matter to you. Help us remember that staying faithful to you, even when it's hard, is already a kind of reigning with you. Thank you for wanting us close to you forever. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Let kids try on a simple paper crown and talk about what it means to reign or rule — then connect it to Revelation 5:10 and 20:4-6.

**Craft idea:** Make a simple picture of the New Jerusalem as a glowing cube-shaped city, with God's throne at the center, to help kids picture heaven as nearness to God rather than just a reward.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What surprises you about the idea that faithful believers are already "reigning" with Jesus, right now?
2. How would you describe heaven in your own words after today?
3. Is there something costly about following Jesus in your own life right now? How does today's lesson encourage you about that?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about being children of the King
- A song about heaven and God's presence
