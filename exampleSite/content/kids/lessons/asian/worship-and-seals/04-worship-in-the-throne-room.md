# The Room Where Everyone Is Worshiping
### A look into heaven's throne room

---

## 1. Warm-up: The best room you've ever seen

Ask the kids: What's the most amazing room you've ever walked into — a big church, a beautiful building, somewhere that made you go "wow"? Today we're going to look at the most amazing room of all — one John got to see in heaven.

---

## 2. What John sees

After writing letters to seven churches, John suddenly sees something enormous: a room in heaven with a throne at the center, so bright and glorious he can barely describe it. All around the throne, everyone is worshiping — nonstop, forever.

Around the throne is a rainbow, reminding us of God's promise to Noah. In front of the throne is something like a sea of glass, calm and still — a picture that God has already won over anything scary or evil. Four wild, wonderful creatures and twenty-four worship leaders surround the throne, representing all of creation and all of God's people, Jews and non-Jews together, worshiping as one big family.

---

## 3. Why show this now?

Right before this, John wrote down Jesus's letters to seven churches — some doing great, some struggling, one totally lost in its own comfort. And right after all of that, God shows John this incredible worship scene. Why?

Because worship is the answer to every one of those churches' problems. When we really know God and worship him for who he truly is, everything else falls into its right place — our fears get smaller, our priorities get clearer, and we don't get pulled off course as easily.

---

## 4. Worship even when it's hard

For some of you, gathering to worship isn't simple or safe — maybe it means meeting quietly, or being one of very few Christians in your whole school or town. Here's the good news this vision gives us: even a small, quiet, careful gathering of believers is joining in with the very worship happening in heaven's throne room, right now. It's not a lesser kind of worship. It's the real thing.

---

## 5. Memory verse

"Worthy are you... for you were slain, and by your blood you ransomed people for God." *(based on Revelation 5:9)*

---

## 6. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for showing us this amazing room where you are worshiped forever. Help us to worship you with our whole hearts, wherever we are and however we gather — even when it's small, even when it's quiet. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Ask kids to describe the most beautiful or amazing place they've ever seen, then compare it to the throne room described in Revelation 4.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw the throne room scene — the throne, the rainbow, the sea of glass, the worshipers — as a way of picturing what John saw.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What do you think it will be like to worship in that room someday?
2. Why do you think God showed John this beautiful scene right after the letters to the seven churches?
3. Does it help to know that even quiet or small worship counts as real worship, joining in with heaven?

**Song ideas:**
- A joyful worship song about heaven or God's throne
- "Holy, Holy, Holy" or a similar song of adoration
