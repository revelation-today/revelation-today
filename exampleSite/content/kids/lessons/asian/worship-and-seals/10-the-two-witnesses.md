# Two Brave Witnesses
### They looked defeated — but they weren't

---

## 1. Warm-up: Telling the truth even when it's hard

Ask the kids: Has it ever been hard to tell the truth, or to stand up for what's right, even when you knew others might not like it? Today's story is about two witnesses who kept telling the truth about God, no matter what it cost them.

---

## 2. Who are the two witnesses?

In Revelation 11, John sees two witnesses who speak for God for a long time — dressed in rough, plain clothes, doing powerful signs, calling people to turn back to God. Many people who study the Bible carefully think these two witnesses represent the whole church, pictured as two people because, in the Bible, you need two witnesses for testimony to count.

---

## 3. What happens to them

After they finish their work, a terrible beast attacks and kills them. Their enemies celebrate — they even leave the witnesses' bodies lying in the street, right out in the open, as a way of shaming them.

But that's not the end of the story! After three and a half days, God brings them back to life, and they rise up to heaven right in front of everyone who thought they'd won. Their enemies watching this are amazed — and many of them turn to God because of it.

---

## 4. Why this matters for us

For some of you, standing up for Jesus really can cost something big — not just embarrassment, but real danger, in families or communities where being a Christian is risky. This story doesn't pretend that following Jesus is always safe. But it promises something important: even when faithfulness looks, from the outside, like complete defeat, God is not finished with the story. What looks like the end can become the very moment God uses to bring people to himself.

---

## 5. Memory verse

"After the three and a half days, the breath of life from God entered them, and they stood up on their feet." *(Revelation 11:11, adapted)*

---

## 6. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for the two witnesses who stayed faithful even when it cost them everything. Help us to be brave like them, trusting that you are never finished with the story, even when things look hopeless. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Talk about a time it was hard to tell the truth or do the right thing — share an age-appropriate example first to open the conversation.

**Storytelling idea:** Tell the story as a mini-drama: the witnesses' faithful work, their defeat, the celebration, and the surprising resurrection — build the suspense before revealing the twist.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think their enemies celebrated when the witnesses were defeated?
2. What do you think it felt like when the witnesses stood back up?
3. Can you think of a time something looked like a total loss, but God brought something good out of it?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about courage and standing firm for Jesus
- A song about God bringing life out of hard places
