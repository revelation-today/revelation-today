# Our Forever Home
### The city where every tear is wiped away

---

## 1. Warm-up: The best possible ending

Ask the kids: if you could design the happiest possible ending to a story, what would it look like? Today's lesson shows us the real ending God has planned — and it's even better than anything we could imagine.

---

## 2. A brand new, forever city

At the very end of Revelation, John sees a beautiful new city coming down from heaven, called the New Jerusalem. God says he is "making everything new" — not patching up the old, broken things, but starting something completely fresh that will never wear out or fall apart again.

---

## 3. God right there with us

The most amazing part isn't the gold or the jewels described in the city — it's this: "God's dwelling place is now among the people... he will dwell with them" (Revelation 21:3). No more distance. No more waiting. God right there, close, forever.

---

## 4. No more tears

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain" (Revelation 21:4). Whatever has been hard — even things that are hard because of following Jesus — won't be forgotten by God. He promises to make all of it right in the end.

---

## 5. A special reversal

Here's something beautiful: whatever this world has ever used to make someone feel ashamed for loving Jesus, God promises to turn into honor instead — not just erase it, but flip it around completely, forever.

---

## 6. Why this matters for us

If following Jesus ever feels lonely, hard, or costly — at home, at school, anywhere — this is where the whole story is heading: a home with no more tears, no more distance from God, and everything that was ever hard made right.

---

## 7. Memory verse

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain." *(Revelation 21:4)*

---

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing a perfect forever home for us. Thank you that you want to be close to us forever, with no more tears. Help us keep trusting you until that day comes. Amen.

---

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening idea:** Ask kids to imagine and describe "the happiest ending ever" before revealing what Revelation 21 actually describes.

**Craft idea:** Have kids draw or build (with blocks) a glowing, jeweled city, with a big sun or light in the middle representing God's presence.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What part of the New Jerusalem sounds most exciting to you?
2. Why do you think it matters so much to God that there will be "no more tears"?
3. Is there something hard in your life right now that this promise gives you hope about?

**Song ideas:**
- A song about heaven or God's forever home
- A song about God wiping away tears
