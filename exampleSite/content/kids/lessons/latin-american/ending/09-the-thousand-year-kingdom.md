# Kings and Queens Already
### A surprising kind of reigning

## 1. What do you think a king does?

If you were a king or queen, what would you do? Wear a crown? Give orders? Live in a palace?

Revelation talks about God's people reigning with Jesus for "a thousand years." But it's not
quite what you might picture.

## 2. Reigning by staying faithful

The Bible says people reign with Jesus because they stayed faithful to him, even when it was
hard — even when it cost them something. That's surprising! Normally we think reigning means
having lots of power and getting your way. But here, reigning means something different:
staying loyal to Jesus, no matter what, especially when it isn't easy.

## 3. That means... you might already be reigning!

Here's the good news: this isn't only about people who lived long ago, or people who face the
very hardest things. Anyone who stays faithful to Jesus — even in small ways, even when nobody
else notices — is already reigning with him, right now. Telling the truth when it's easier to
lie. Being kind when others aren't. Standing up for someone being treated unfairly. Trusting
Jesus even on a hard day. All of that counts.

## 4. The whole story of God's people

This "thousand years" isn't a faraway future thing — it describes the whole time between Jesus
coming the first time and Jesus coming back. That means it includes right now. It includes you.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"Staying faithful to Jesus is like wearing a crown."**

## Activity

**Paper Crowns:** Give each child a simple paper crown to decorate. On it, have them write or
draw one way they can stay faithful to Jesus this week (being kind, telling the truth, being
brave). Remind them: doing that is like wearing this crown for real, in God's eyes.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"Jesus, help me stay faithful to you this week, even in small ways. Thank you that you see it
and it matters to you. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Skip the millennium debate entirely for this age — there's no need to explain amillennial vs.
premillennial views to children. The one idea worth landing is simple and true to the source
material: faithfulness to Jesus, even in small everyday ways, is itself a kind of "reigning,"
and that's true right now, not just in some faraway future.
