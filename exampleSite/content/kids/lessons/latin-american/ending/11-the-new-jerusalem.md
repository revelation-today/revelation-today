# Our Forever Home
### The city where everything is finally right

## 1. Remember the shiny trap?

A while back, we learned about Babylon — the beautiful trap that looked so rich and secure, but
fell apart fast. Today we see the opposite: a city that's truly beautiful, truly secure, and
lasts forever. It's called the New Jerusalem.

## 2. Everything Babylon promised — for real this time

The New Jerusalem shines with gold and jewels too — but this treasure isn't built on hurting
anyone. It's a free gift from God. Babylon's water cost you everything; in the New Jerusalem,
"the water of life" is given "without cost" — completely free, to anyone who's thirsty
(Revelation 21:6). Babylon fell apart in an hour; the New Jerusalem lasts forever.

## 3. No more sad things

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or
pain" (Revelation 21:4). Every hard thing that's ever made you cry — God says it ends here, for
good, and he wipes it away himself, personally.

## 4. Everyone is welcome close to God

The city is shaped like the most special, holiest room that used to exist in the old temple —
a room only one priest could ever enter, only once a year. Now, everyone who loves Jesus gets
to be that close to God, all the time, forever. Nobody's shut out or kept far away anymore.

## 5. People from everywhere

The New Jerusalem isn't just for one country or one kind of people — it's full of people "from
every nation, tribe, people and language" (a promise we already heard back in chapter 7). That
includes you, wherever you're from.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"Our forever home is with God, and everyone is invited."**

## Activity

**Build the City:** Give children building blocks, colored paper, or craft materials to build
a small "city" together, decorating it as brightly and beautifully as they can. While they
build, remind them: God is building something even better, forever, for everyone who loves him.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"God, thank you for preparing a forever home for me. Thank you that everyone who loves you is
welcome there. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

This is a joyful closing session — let it feel celebratory. There's no need to explain the
detailed measurements or Ezekiel parallels; the core images that matter for this age are: no
more tears, a free gift instead of something bought, everyone welcome close to God, and people
from every nation together forever.
