# Where God Is, Everything Good Is
### What heaven and hell are really about

## 1. What do you picture?

If someone asked you what heaven is like, what would you say? What about hell?

A lot of people picture heaven as a reward for being good, and hell as a punishment for being
bad. The Bible's picture is a little different — and once you see it, it makes a lot of sense.

## 2. Heaven is being close to God

Heaven isn't mainly about a place with nice things. It's about being close to God — as close as
possible, forever. The Bible even describes heaven like a wedding, with God's people as the
bride and Jesus as the groom — that's how close and joyful it is.

## 3. Hell is being far from God

And hell, at its simplest, is a life with no closeness to God at all. Here's why that matters so
much: every good thing we love — feeling peaceful, feeling loved, being able to forgive someone,
having hope — actually comes from God in the first place, even when we don't notice it. So a
life completely without God means a life without any of those good things either.

## 4. God doesn't force anyone

Here's something important: God never forces anyone to be close to him. He invites us, and he
lets us choose. That's how much he respects us — even though he wants every single one of us to
choose him.

## 5. The good news

The good news is: you don't have to wait and wonder. You can choose to be close to God right
now, today — and that closeness is exactly what heaven is.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"Heaven is being close to God, forever."**

## Activity

**Close and Far:** Have children stand far apart from a leader holding a warm blanket or a
favorite comfort object, then walk closer step by step. Talk about how things feel different
up close versus far away — and connect it to being close to God.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"God, thank you that you want to be close to me. Help me choose to be close to you every day.
Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Keep hell language gentle and avoid vivid fear-based imagery for this age group — the point is
"life without God's good gifts," not graphic punishment. Emphasize God's invitation and desire
for closeness rather than threat, matching the source material's own caution against using hell
mainly as a scare tactic.
