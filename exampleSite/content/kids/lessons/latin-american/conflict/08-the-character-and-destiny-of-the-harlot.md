# When the Sandcastle Falls
### Why Babylon's "friends" turned on her

## 1. Remember Babylon?

Last time, we met Babylon — the woman dressed in gold and jewels who looked so rich and
glamorous, but was really built on unfairness. Today we find out what happens to her, and why.

## 2. Everyone thought she'd last forever

Babylon thought she was untouchable. In her own heart she said, "I'll always be a queen. I'll
never be sad" (Revelation 18:7). She was so sure nothing could ever knock her down.

## 3. Her own friends knock her down

But here's the twist: it's her own allies — the very powers who built her up and depended on
her — who suddenly turn around and destroy her. Not enemies from far away. Her own friends.

Have you ever built a sandcastle right at the edge of the water, and thought it would last —
and then a wave came and it was just... gone, fast, before you even had time to fix it? That's
a little bit like what happens to Babylon. Something that looked so solid and permanent
collapsed almost instantly, and the very people who once loved it were the ones who knocked it
down.

## 4. Why does this happen?

People's loyalty can change fast — today's friend can become tomorrow's enemy, especially when
everyone is really just looking out for themselves. But God never changes. He's the only truly
solid ground there is.

## 5. Build on the Rock instead

Remember the story Jesus told about the wise man who built his house on rock, and the foolish
man who built on sand? When the storm came, only the house on the rock stood. Babylon is a
whole city built on sand. Real security — the kind that doesn't get knocked down by the next
wave — only comes from building your life on Jesus.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"Build your life on Jesus — he never changes."**

## Activity

**Sandcastle vs. Rock:** If possible, bring a small tray of sand and a rock. Let children try
building something on the sand and pouring water near it; then show how the rock doesn't move.
Talk about what in our lives is like "sand" (money, being popular, always winning) and what is
like "rock" (Jesus).

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"Jesus, help me build my life on you, because you never change and never let me down. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Keep this session light on the historical Rome/Tyre background and focused on the simple,
concrete picture: something that looks strong and permanent can collapse quickly, especially
when it's built on unfairness or on people's shifting loyalty rather than on God. The
"wise and foolish builder" connection (Matthew 7:24–27) is a natural, already-familiar anchor
for this age group.
