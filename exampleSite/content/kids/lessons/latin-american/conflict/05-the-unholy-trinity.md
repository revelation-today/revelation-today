# The Copycat Team
### Satan tries to copy God — and fails

## 1. Copying isn't always flattering

Have you ever seen someone copy another person, trying to look just like them, but get
everything just a little bit wrong? In today's story, that's exactly what Satan tries to do —
and it doesn't go well for him.

## 2. Three bad guys who copy the real Three

In Revelation 13, John sees a dragon (that's Satan) team up with two beasts — one comes out of
the sea, one comes out of the earth. Together, they try to copy God the Father, Jesus the Son,
and the Holy Spirit — but as a fake, evil version.

- The dragon acts like a fake "father," giving his power to the first beast.
- The first beast acts like a fake "Jesus" — it even looks like it died and came back to life!
- The second beast acts like a fake "Holy Spirit" — it does tricks and signs to make everyone
  worship the first beast.

It's a whole team of copies. But here's the thing about copies: they're never quite right.

## 3. How to spot the fake

Here's the giveaway: the second beast "looked like a lamb, but it talked like a dragon"
(Revelation 13:11). It looks gentle and good on the outside — but the voice gives it away.

Real sheep know their shepherd's real voice (John 10). So do we — if we stay close to Jesus, we
can tell his real voice apart from a counterfeit, even when the counterfeit looks impressive.

## 4. Strong power isn't always the real thing

This whole team looks incredibly powerful and impressive — people are amazed by it, scared of
it, and go along with it. It can feel like: if something looks that strong and unstoppable, it
must be from God, right?

Not necessarily! The dragon's team wins by scaring people and forcing them to go along. Jesus
never works that way. He never forces anyone. He wins through love, even when love looks weaker
than power, for a while. Whenever anyone — even someone in church — tries to scare you or force
you into something instead of loving you the way Jesus does, that's a good clue something isn't
right.

## 5. It's already been beaten

Here's the best part: every single time this copycat team shows up in Revelation, it loses.
Every time. The real King, Jesus, already won — through love, through the cross, through
staying faithful — not through scary tricks.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"Jesus wins through love, not fear."**

## Activity

**Spot the Fake:** Show two simple pictures or objects — one real, one a clear copy (a real
coin and a paper circle, for instance). Talk about how the fake looks similar but isn't the real
thing. Connect it back: the dragon's team looks powerful like God, but it isn't the real thing.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"Jesus, help me know your real voice. Keep me close to you, so I'm never fooled by something
that only looks strong. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Keep the political/empire history in the background for this age — the point that matters most
is the copycat/counterfeit idea and the "voice test" (fear and force vs. love). If children
bring up real-world examples of unfair or scary leaders, it's fine to affirm that God cares
about that unfairness too — but steer the main lesson back to worship and staying close to
Jesus' true voice, rather than turning the whole session into a lesson about politics or
history.
