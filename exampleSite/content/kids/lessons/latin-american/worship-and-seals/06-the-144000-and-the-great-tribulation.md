# God Counts Every Single One of Us
### An army that turns into something even better

## 1. Counting soldiers

John hears a number: 144,000 people, marked and kept safe by God, like an army getting counted
before a battle — some from every tribe of God's people.

That's a big number! But watch what happens next.

## 2. Look again — it's even bigger

John hears "144,000" — but then he actually looks, and what does he see? "A great multitude
that no one could count, from every nation, tribe, people and language, standing before the
throne" (Revelation 7:9). Not a countable army after all — an uncountable crowd of people from
every single country on earth, worshiping God together!

That's you, and me, and children and grown-ups from every country you can think of — all
together, all counted by God, even though there are too many of us to actually count.

## 3. Kept safe in hard times

The Bible calls the hard, difficult time God's people go through "the Great Tribulation" — a
big phrase for a simple idea: life is sometimes really hard for people who love Jesus, and it
has been hard for Christians in every time and place, not just long ago.

But here's the promise: God seals and keeps his people safe *through* the hard time — not by
snatching them away before it happens. He wipes away every tear, he comforts, and he never
leaves.

## 4. You belong to this uncountable crowd

Wherever you're from, however hard some days are, you belong to this enormous, uncountable
group of people who love Jesus — and God knows you by name, personally, right in the middle of
it.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together:

**"God counts me, and keeps me safe."**

## Activity

**Uncountable Crowd:** Have children each make a handprint or fingerprint on a big shared poster
titled "God's Uncountable Family." Point out: there are too many prints to count exactly, just
like the crowd John saw — and every single one matters to God.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"God, thank you for knowing me by name. Thank you for keeping me safe, even on hard days. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Keep the focus on comfort and belonging rather than a technical explanation of tribulation
timelines. The key idea for this age: hard times happen even to people who love Jesus, God
keeps his people safe and close during them (not necessarily away from them), and every single
person who loves Jesus is known and counted by God, even in an uncountable crowd.
