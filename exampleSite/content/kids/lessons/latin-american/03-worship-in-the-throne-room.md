# The Biggest Party in Heaven
### What John saw before anything else happened

## 1. Before anything else happens...

Before any of the scary or exciting parts of Revelation happen — before any dragons, any
trouble, anything at all — John gets shown something first: a room in heaven, with God on his
throne, and everybody worshiping. Singing, shouting, bowing down — the loudest, most joyful
praise you can imagine, forever.

Why do you think God showed John *that* first, before anything else?

## 2. A room full of praise

In this room, John sees:

- A rainbow around the throne — remember, that's God's promise to Noah that he keeps his word.
- Creatures like a lion, an ox, a person, and an eagle, worshiping — representing all of
  creation.
- Twenty-four leaders with crowns, worshiping too.
- And in the middle of it all: a Lamb who was killed but is alive — Jesus! — receiving worship
  from everyone.

Everyone in that room is doing one thing: praising God with everything they have.

## 3. You already know how to do this!

Guess what — a lot of you already know exactly what this feels like! Maybe you've been in
church when everyone's singing loudly together, hands up, and it feels like something bigger
than just a song is happening. Maybe you've felt it during Mass, when everyone says "Holy,
holy, holy" together. That feeling — that's a little taste of the throne room John saw.

That's not just something we do at church because we're supposed to. It's the most important
thing there is. Worship comes *first*, before anything else in the whole story of Revelation.

## 4. Why worship first?

Here's the secret: when we really know how big and good and strong God is, we stop being so
scared of everything else. Whatever trouble comes later in the story, the people who were in
that throne room already know: God is bigger than all of it.

## Our Refrain

Let's say it together, loudly:

**"God is on the throne, and he's worth all our praise!"**

Say it once quietly. Say it once loudly. Say it once while clapping!

## Activity

**Throne Room Circle:** Have the children stand in a circle. Practice different ways people
worship around the world — clapping, raising hands, kneeling, singing quietly. Explain: in
God's throne room, all of these belong together, because everyone who loves Jesus is welcome
there.

*Coloring option:* Give children an outline of a throne with a rainbow over it to color.

## Simple Prayer

*Have each child repeat after you, one phrase at a time:*

"God, you are so good and so big. Thank you that we get to praise you. Help us to worship you
with our whole hearts. Amen."

## Note for the Teacher

Keep this session joyful and physical — let the children actually clap, raise hands, or kneel
during the activity rather than just talking about worship. There's no need to explain the sea
of glass or the fuller Daniel 7 parallels at this age; the point is simply: heaven is full of
joyful praise, that praise comes before anything else in the story, and the kind of worship
they may already practice at church (whichever style their family practices) is a real
foreshadowing of it.
