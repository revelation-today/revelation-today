# Who Is Really the Boss?
### A story from the time the book of Revelation was written

## 1. Let's play a strange game

Here's the rule: every time I raise my hand, everyone has to jump up, clap, and shout "You're
the best!" Anyone who doesn't do it has to sit out a round.

*(Play it once or twice, then ask:)*

- How did that feel?
- Was that a fair rule?
- What if you had to do that every single day — even on days you didn't feel like it was true?

That's a little bit like a game the very first Christians had to play — except for them it
wasn't a game. That's what today is about.

## 2. Long ago: the emperor wants everyone to say yes

When the last book of the Bible — Revelation — was written, almost the whole known world
belonged to one empire, ruled by one man: the emperor of Rome.

The emperor said: "I take care of everything. Safety. Food. Peace. A good life. All I ask in
return is one thing: everyone has to show that I'm the greatest — almost like a god."

You saw it everywhere: his face on every coin, huge statues of him in the city squares, and at
every party or festival, people had to raise a cup and praise him.

Anyone who didn't join in got into trouble — sometimes very big trouble.

## 3. The problem: Christians already had a King

The Christians thought: "Wait a minute. We already have a King — Jesus! And our food, our
safety, our good life come from God, not from the emperor." So they couldn't honestly shout,
"The emperor is the greatest!"

It's a bit like if someone in your class suddenly announced: "From now on, everyone laughs at
the new kid, or you don't belong anymore." Some kids laugh along anyway — not because they
think it's funny, but because they want to belong. That's the exact choice the first Christians
faced, every single day:

1. **Go along with everything** and pretend they didn't believe in Jesus anymore.
2. **Say no completely** — even if that meant real trouble.
3. **Go along a little bit, and secretly still believe in Jesus** — show one thing on the
   outside, while something different was true on the inside.

## 4. John — someone who said no

The man who wrote Revelation was named John. He tells us himself: because he wouldn't go along
with it, he was sent away to a lonely island called Patmos — far from home, all by himself.

But right there, all alone, God showed John an enormous, encouraging picture: Jesus is alive.
Jesus really reigns — so much more than any emperor ever could. The whole book John wrote
afterward was meant to give Christians courage to stay with Jesus, no matter what happened.

## 5. What about us today? Who is our "emperor"?

Nobody today has to cheer for a statue of an emperor. But the same question is still here, just
wearing different clothes: **Who do you trust the most? Who do you want to make happy the
most?**

A few things to think about:

- Is it more important that other kids think you're cool than that you do the right thing?
- Do you feel safer with your phone or your game console than when you're talking with God?
- Do you go along with something you actually think is wrong, just so you'll belong?

Those are our "emperors" today — things or people we trust more, or care about more, than
Jesus, without even really noticing it.

## 6. The frog in warm water

Do you know the story about the frog in water? If you drop a frog straight into boiling water,
it jumps right out. But if you warm the water up little by little, the frog doesn't notice the
danger — until it's too late. *(This isn't really true about real frogs, but it's a good
picture.)*

It can happen to us the same slow way. Nobody makes us forget about Jesus all at once. It
happens bit by bit — a little excuse here, a little going-along there — until we're not even
sure anymore who's really the "boss" of our life.

## 7. What helps us

Good news: we don't have to hide, and we don't have to go along just to belong. Jesus is
already the true King — much stronger than any emperor, any trend, anything "everyone else" is
doing. He invites us to honestly belong to him, to trust him, and to not hide that we're his.

**Memory verse:** "No one can serve two masters." *(Matthew 6:24)*

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for really being King — so much stronger than anything that scares us or
anything we want to be seen as "cool." Help us to honestly stand with you, even when it's hard.
Show us when we secretly trust something else more than you. Amen.

## Notes for the leader

**Opening game:** The "You're the Best" game from section 1 — talk afterward about how it felt.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Have each child draw a small "crown." On one side, have them
draw or write something that sometimes acts like an "emperor" in daily life (phone, being cool,
candy, a favorite game...). On the other side, have them write "Jesus." Talk about: whose crown
should really be the biggest?

**Questions for the kids:**
1. When is it hard for you to say "no" when others are doing something wrong?
2. What would you do if someone laughed at you for believing in Jesus?
3. What's sometimes more important to you than Jesus, even though you don't really want it to
   be?

**Song ideas:** a simple song about trusting God, and a song about Jesus as King.
