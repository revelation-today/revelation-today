# Who Is the Harlot Babylon?

## Game/Hook: The Fancy Fake

Bring a shiny, beautifully wrapped box (or a "jewel" made of foil and plastic gems) — but inside,
put something gross or fake, like a rotten-looking plastic apple, an empty box, or crumpled
newspaper. Pass it around sealed and ask kids to guess what treasure must be inside, based on how
beautiful the outside looks. Then open it and let them see the letdown.

Ask: "Have you ever met something that looked amazing on the outside but was actually not good at
all once you got close?" (Think: a poisoned apple in a fairy tale, or a wolf dressed up in
sheep's clothing.) Today's story is about exactly that kind of trick — except it's not in a fairy
tale. It's in the Bible.

## The Story: The Queen Who Wasn't What She Seemed

In Revelation, John sees a strange and dazzling woman. She's dressed in purple and scarlet,
covered in gold, pearls, and jewels, holding a fancy golden cup. She looks like a queen — maybe
even like someone holy, dressed the way a priest might dress for something sacred. But written
right on her forehead is a name: "Babylon the Great." And the cup she's holding? It's full of
disgusting things, not treasure.

This woman stands for a real, powerful city in John's time called Rome — a city so rich and
important that everybody wanted to be close to it, to trade with it, to be liked by it. Rome
looked glittering and unstoppable. But Rome also wanted people to worship its emperor instead of
God, and it used its wealth and glamour to pull people away from loving God first.

Here's the important part: this beautiful-looking woman is a trick. She looks fancy, even
sacred — but underneath, she's rotten. She represents anything that tries to look wonderful and
trustworthy on the outside while actually pulling people away from God on the inside. The Bible
calls this "Babylon," and it shows up any time money, fame, or fitting in becomes more important
to us than loving and obeying God.

The really important thing to know is that this isn't just a story about one city a long, long
time ago. It's a warning for every single person, in every time, including us — because "Babylon"
can sneak into anyone's life if we're not careful, no matter how good and normal it looks on the
outside.

## What This Means for Us

Sometimes the things that pull us away from God don't look scary at all. They look shiny, fun, or
impressive — like a game that makes us mean to others, or wanting stuff so much we forget to be
grateful, or caring more about being popular than being kind. Revelation is warning us: don't just
look at how shiny or impressive something seems. Ask whether it's actually good, and whether it's
helping you love God and others — or quietly pulling you away.

The good news? God always sees past the shiny wrapping to what's really inside — and he always
wants to help us choose what's truly good, even when the fake, fancy thing is tempting.

## Memory Verse

*"Come out of her, my people, so that you will not share in her sins."*
— Revelation 18:4

## Prayer

Dear God, help us see past things that look shiny and impressive but aren't really good for us.
Help us love you more than money, fame, or fitting in. Give us brave hearts to choose what's
really good, even when the fake thing looks more fun. Amen.

## For Preparation

**Craft/activity ideas:**
- **Decorate a "fake treasure" box:** let kids decorate a small box with foil, sequins, or gems on
  the outside, but write inside the lid "Looks good ≠ Is good" as a takeaway reminder they can
  keep.
- **Sort the cards game:** prepare simple picture/word cards (some show things that look fun but
  aren't good choices, like being mean to look cool; others show things that are truly good, like
  sharing or praying) and have kids sort them into two piles: "Looks Good, Isn't" and "Actually
  Good."
- **Wolf in sheep's clothing skit:** let two kids act out a very short, silly skit — one dressed
  as a "sheep" who's secretly a wolf — to reinforce the looks-vs-reality theme before recapping
  the lesson.

**Discussion questions:**
1. Can you think of something that looks really fun or cool but isn't actually good for you or
   others?
2. Why do you think the woman in John's vision dressed like a queen, or even like something holy,
   instead of looking scary?
3. What's one way you can remember to look past the "shiny wrapping" this week and ask if
   something is really good?
