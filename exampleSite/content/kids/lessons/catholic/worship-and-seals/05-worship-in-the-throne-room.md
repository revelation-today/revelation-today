# Worship in the Throne Room

## Game/Hook: A Peek Through the Door

Ask: "Have you ever peeked through a door at a party and realized you were actually invited?"
Let a few kids share.

Here's a secret: going to Mass on Sunday is like getting a peek into a party that's happening in
heaven *all the time.* Right now, an incredible celebration is going on around God's throne — and
every time we go to Mass, we get to join in.

Play "Open Door": one kid stands as the "door," arms crossed. On "Open!" they fling their arms
wide and everyone rushes in with hands raised, like joining a party. Repeat a few times, building
energy.

## The Story: What John Saw

A man named John got invited through a door into heaven itself, and what he saw was almost too
much for words. In the middle of the room was a throne, and on it sat God, glowing like the most
beautiful jewels anyone had ever seen. Around the throne was a rainbow, like the one God gave
Noah — a reminder that God always keeps his promises and takes care of his people.

Twenty-four leaders sat on thrones nearby, wearing white robes and gold crowns, worshiping God.
Four amazing living creatures — one like a lion, one like an ox, one with a face like a person,
one like an eagle — stood by the throne too, representing every creature God ever made: wild
animals, farm animals, people, and birds, all together thanking God.

Here's the part that sounds familiar: these creatures never stopped singing, "Holy, holy, holy is
the Lord God Almighty!" That's because we sing almost those exact words at every Mass! When your
parish sings the "Holy, Holy, Holy," you are singing the very same song the angels are singing in
heaven right now.

Then something even more amazing happened. A Lamb appeared — one who looked like he had been
hurt, but who was alive and strong. Everyone in that room — every creature, every elder, thousands
of angels — turned and worshiped the Lamb too, right alongside God on the throne. That's Jesus.
Heaven was showing John: Jesus is worthy of the very same worship as God, because Jesus *is* God.

## What This Means for Us

That throne room isn't just a story about the future — it's happening right now, and every time
we go to Mass, we step into it. The priest, the altar, the singing, the "Holy, Holy, Holy" — we
aren't just copying heaven from far away. We are actually joining the same worship that never
stops, the same party that never ends.

Next time you're at Mass and everyone sings "Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God of hosts," picture the
four living creatures singing it too, at that very same moment, in heaven. You're really part of
it.

## Memory Verse

*"Holy, holy, holy, is the Lord God the Almighty, who was and is and is to come!"*
— Revelation 4:8

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for letting us join the party in heaven every time we go to Mass. Thank you
that Jesus, the Lamb, is worthy of all our worship, just like you are. Help us sing "Holy, Holy,
Holy" and really mean it, knowing angels are singing it with us right now. Amen.

## For Preparation

**Craft/activity ideas:**
- **Draw the throne room:** give kids paper and let them draw the rainbow throne, the four
  living creatures (lion, ox, person, eagle), and the twenty-four elders in white with gold
  crowns. Add a Lamb in the center.
- **Practice the Sanctus:** teach or review the sung/spoken "Holy, Holy, Holy" from the Mass and
  practice saying or singing it together as a group, imagining joining the living creatures.
- **Act it out:** assign roles (God's throne, living creatures, elders, the Lamb) and have kids
  act out the scene, ending with everyone bowing and saying "Holy, holy, holy!" together.

**Discussion questions:**
1. What was your favorite thing John saw in the throne room?
2. Why do you think it's exciting that we sing the very same words at Mass that the angels sing
   in heaven?
3. How does it feel to know that going to Mass means joining a party that never stops, instead of
   just watching from far away?
