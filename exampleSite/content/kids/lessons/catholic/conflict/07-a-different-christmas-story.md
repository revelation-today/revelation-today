# A Different Christmas Story

### The Baby, the Dragon, and the Rescue

## Game: "Guard the Baby"

Pick one child to be "the baby" (holding a small toy or soft ball counts as the baby). Pick one child to be "the dragon" who has to try to tag the baby. Everyone else links arms in a circle around the baby to protect it, shuffling together to keep the dragon out. Play for a minute or two, then ask:

- How did it feel to be inside the circle, protected?
- How did it feel trying to get past the circle, if you were the dragon?
- Do you think the baby could have protected itself alone?

Today's story is about the biggest, most important "guard the baby" story of all — one told in a very surprising way.

## The Story: A Christmas Story with a Dragon in It

You know the Christmas story: baby Jesus, a manger, shepherds, a star. But there's another way the Bible tells this same story — and this time, it's told like an action movie in the sky.

In Revelation 12, John sees a woman shining like the sun, with the moon under her feet and twelve stars like a crown on her head. She's about to have a baby. Suddenly, an enormous, terrifying red dragon appears — with seven heads and ten horns — and it plants itself right in front of her, waiting. Why? Because the dragon wants to eat the baby the instant he's born.

That dragon is the devil himself (Revelation 12:9 tells us so plainly). And the baby is Jesus — the Bible says he will "rule all the nations," which is a special way of describing God's own promised King.

Here's what happens: the woman has her baby. The dragon lunges. And then — whoosh — before the dragon can do anything, the baby is snatched all the way up to God's throne, safe. The dragon is too late. He never even gets close.

That single moment is really the whole story of Jesus's life, told in fast motion. Wicked King Herod tried to kill baby Jesus, and God kept him safe (Matthew 2). Later, the devil tried to trick Jesus in the desert, and Jesus said no every time (Matthew 4:1-11). And at the very end, after Jesus died on the cross, God raised him back to life and brought him home to heaven — exactly like the baby being "snatched up" to God's throne. Every single time, the dragon tries, and every single time, he loses.

## Application: The Dragon Never Actually Wins

Isn't it wild that in this story, the dragon looks so scary — huge, red, seven heads — and yet he never manages to actually win? He never even gets to touch the baby.

That's true in your life too. Sometimes things feel scary and out of control — like the dragon towering over the woman in the story. But God is always working, even when we can't see how, to protect and rescue the people he loves. The dragon may look powerful. He is not more powerful than God.

Think about a time you felt scared or small, like something bad might happen. Remember: God was watching over you then too, just like he watched over baby Jesus.

## Memory Verse

"Her child was snatched up to God and to his throne." — Revelation 12:5

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for protecting baby Jesus from the dragon, just like you protect us. When we feel scared, help us remember that you are always stronger than whatever is trying to scare us. Thank you for always winning in the end. Amen.

## For preparation

**Opening game:** "Guard the Baby," described above — a circle-protection game to set up the theme of God's protection.

**Craft idea:** Have children color or make a simple paper crown with twelve stars on it, and a small paper dragon to go with it. Tape the dragon "outside" a circle drawn around the crown, showing the dragon can't get in.

**Discussion questions:**
1. What's the scariest part of this story? What's the best part?
2. Why do you think the dragon couldn't catch the baby in time?
3. Can you think of a time you were scared, but things turned out okay because God was taking care of you?

**Song suggestions:** A gentle Christmas hymn (such as "Away in a Manger" or "Silent Night") paired with a song about God's protection or strength, to connect the familiar nativity story with today's cosmic retelling.
