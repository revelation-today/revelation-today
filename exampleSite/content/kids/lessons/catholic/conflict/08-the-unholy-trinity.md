# The Unholy Trinity

### The Copycat Bully

## Game: "Real or Copy?"

Before the session, prepare a few pairs of objects: something real and something that just looks like it (a real flower and a plastic flower, a real cookie and a play-dough "cookie," a photo of a person and a drawing of the same person). Show each pair and ask the kids to guess which one is real. After each guess, ask: "What gave it away? What made the fake one different once you looked closely?"

Then say: today's story is about the sneakiest copycat in the whole Bible — one who tries to look powerful like God, but is really just a bully in disguise.

## The Story: The Great Copycat

After the dragon (the devil) couldn't stop baby Jesus, he got even angrier and decided to go after everyone who loves Jesus — that's us, the Church! But instead of fighting alone, the dragon calls up two helper monsters, called "beasts," to help him.

Here's the strange part: these beasts don't invent anything new. They just copy things God does — except they get everything backwards.

The first beast pretends to be like Jesus. It even gets a wound that heals up, kind of like how Jesus died and came back to life — except it's all fake, just for show, to make people bow down and worship it instead of God.

The second beast pretends to be like the Holy Spirit. It even does flashy tricks, like making fire fall from the sky, to impress people. But here's its big secret, right there in the Bible: it "speaks like a dragon" (Revelation 13:11). Underneath the show, it's still the bully, just wearing a costume.

And how does this copycat team get what it wants? Not through love. Through threats. Anyone who won't bow down and go along with them gets punished. That's the giveaway. God never forces anyone to love him — love that's forced isn't love at all. But a bully forces people through fear, because fear is the only tool a bully has.

The really good news is this: every old Bible story this bully-monster is copying from — like Daniel and his friends thrown into the fiery furnace for refusing to bow down to a giant statue — ends with the bully losing and God's people walking out just fine. Copycats never win in the end. God does.

## Application: How to Spot a Copycat

You will probably never meet a dragon with seven heads. But you will meet copycat "power" in smaller ways — a friend who threatens you to get their way, someone who tries to scare you into doing something you know is wrong, or someone who says "everyone's doing it, you have to too."

Here's the test that never fails: does it use love, or does it use fear? Jesus never bullies anyone into following him. He invites. He's patient. He gives his own life for us instead of demanding we hand ours over. Real power looks like love. Copycat power looks like threats.

The best way to recognize Jesus's real voice, instead of a copycat's, is to stay close to him — praying, listening at Mass, and learning to notice how gentle and truthful his voice is compared to anything trying to scare or trick you.

## Memory Verse

"Love your neighbor as yourself. There is no commandment greater than these." — Mark 12:31 (see also Matthew 22:36-39)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us instead of trying to scare us. Help us know your real voice so we're never fooled by a copycat. When someone tries to bully us or scare us into doing wrong, give us courage to choose love instead, just like you always do. Amen.

## For preparation

**Opening game:** "Real or Copy?" using paired real/fake objects, described above.

**Craft idea:** Have children draw two masks side by side — a "scary bully mask" and a "kind hero mask" — and write one word under each describing how that character treats people (example: "threatens" vs. "loves").

**Discussion questions:**
1. What gave away the fake objects in our game? What gives away a "copycat" who's only pretending to be good?
2. Why do you think the bully-monster in the story needed threats and fear instead of just asking nicely?
3. Can you think of a time someone tried to pressure you into doing something with threats or fear instead of asking kindly? What did you do?

**Song suggestions:** A children's song about courage or standing up for what's right, paired with a song about God's love and gentleness, to reinforce the contrast between fear-based "power" and real, loving strength.
