# The New Jerusalem: Our Eternal Home

## Game/Hook: Design Your Dream Home

Ask: "If you could design the most beautiful, amazing home in the whole world, what would it have?" Let kids call out ideas — gold walls, jewels, a river, a garden, anything.

Tell them: "Believe it or not, God already showed someone what the most beautiful home ever built actually looks like — and it's better than anything we just imagined. It's called the New Jerusalem, and it's going to be our home forever."

Play "Palace Builder": in small groups, give kids a minute to "build" an imaginary palace out of couch cushions, chairs, or just descriptions shouted out loud — each group adds one new amazing feature until time runs out.

## The Story: The City That Comes Down

In Revelation, John hears about a bride — like someone dressed up beautifully for a wedding — but when he turns to look, he sees a whole city instead, coming down out of heaven, sparkling like the most precious jewel anyone has ever seen (Revelation 21:9-11). That's a clue that John is showing us something wonderful using pictures, not just describing plain facts — like when you say someone's smile is "sunshine." It doesn't mean their smile is literally the sun. It means: pay attention, something beautiful is being described.

This city is shaped like a perfect cube — and remember from last time, the only thing in the whole Old Testament shaped like a cube was the Holy of Holies, the one small room in the temple where God's presence was strongest. Only the high priest could go in, and only once a year. But now, the whole city is that same shape — which means everyone who lives there gets to be as close to God as only one priest used to be allowed to be!

The city has walls made of jewels, streets that shine, and twelve gates named after Israel's twelve tribes, with twelve foundation stones named after Jesus' twelve apostles. There's no temple building inside the city at all — because God and the Lamb (that's Jesus) are the temple now. And a river of clear, sparkling water flows right from God's throne, with the tree of life growing beside it — the same tree from the Garden of Eden, all the way back at the beginning of the Bible! The story comes full circle: what was lost in Eden is given back, better than ever, forever.

## A Little Taste Already

Here's something wonderful: we don't have to wait until we die to get a little taste of that closeness to God. Every time we receive Holy Communion at Mass, God comes to be truly near to us — really and truly present. It's not the whole thing yet — that's still coming, in heaven — but it's a real, true taste of it, right now. Communion is like a little preview of the New Jerusalem: God, close to us, just like he'll be close to us forever in our true home.

## What This Means for Us

We don't have to picture heaven as something cold or far away or hard to imagine. It's a home — the most beautiful home ever — built for us to be close to God forever. And every time we go to Mass and receive Jesus in Communion, we're already getting a little taste of that closeness. That's worth being excited about!

## Memory Verse

*"Behold, God's dwelling is with the human race. He will dwell with them and they will be his people and God himself will always be with them."*
— Revelation 21:3

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing the most beautiful home for us to live in with you forever. Thank you that every time we receive Communion, we get a little taste of being close to you already. Help us look forward to our forever home with you. Amen.

## For Preparation

**Craft/activity ideas:**
- **Build a mini New Jerusalem:** using craft supplies (foil, sequins, glitter glue, colored paper), have kids decorate a small box or paper city with "jewels," a shining street, and a river running through it.
- **Twelve gates, twelve names:** let kids write or decorate twelve paper "gates," each with a tribe or apostle name, and tape them around the room.
- **Communion connection:** with parent/catechist guidance, talk simply about how receiving Communion is a "sneak peek" of being close to God in heaven.

**Discussion questions:**
1. If you could describe the New Jerusalem in three words, what would they be?
2. Why do you think it's exciting that everyone gets to be as close to God as the high priest used to be?
3. How does Communion feel like a "little taste" of our forever home with God?
