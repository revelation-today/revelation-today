# Heaven and Hell: What Really Awaits Us?

## Game/Hook: The Best Hug Ever

Ask: "What's the best hug you've ever gotten — from a parent, a grandparent, someone you love a lot after being away from them?" Let a few kids share.

Now imagine a hug like that... that never had to end. Not because you're stuck, but because you never, ever wanted it to stop. That's the closest thing we have to describing heaven.

Play "Longest Hug Circle": have the group form a circle, arms around shoulders, and see if they can hold the group hug while counting together as high as they can without breaking apart. When they finally break, ask: "What if that hug just kept going forever, with God?"

## The Story: What John Saw

In the book of Revelation, John gets to peek into heaven, and he sees two amazing pictures. In one, a huge crowd of people from every country in the world stands in front of God, and God himself wipes away every tear from their eyes (Revelation 7). In the other, an entire beautiful city comes down from heaven, and a voice says, "Now God lives with his people!" (Revelation 21).

Here's the really important part: both pictures are about the same thing. Heaven isn't a prize you win, like a gold star for being good enough. Heaven is being as close to God as you can possibly be — forever. It's the hug that never ends, because God's presence is the thing that makes everything else good and joyful and safe.

John even tells us heaven's city is shaped like a perfect cube. That might sound like a strange detail, but it's actually wonderful: a long time ago, in the Old Testament temple, there was one small room shaped exactly like a cube, called the Holy of Holies. That's where God's presence was the strongest — and only one priest could go in, only once a year! Everybody else had to stay farther away. But in heaven, the whole city is shaped like that special room. That means everyone gets to be as close to God as only one priest used to be allowed to be. No one has to stay far away anymore.

## What About Hell?

So what's the opposite of heaven? It's not really about fire and monsters — it's simpler and sadder than that. Hell is choosing to be far away from God forever. All the good things we love — feeling loved, feeling peaceful, feeling hopeful, feeling forgiven — those all come from God in the first place, even when we don't notice it. Hell means missing out on all of that, forever, because it means missing out on God.

Here's the good news hiding inside that: God never forces anyone to come close to him. He invites us, gently, our whole lives — kind of like he's courting us, drawing us near with love. And he never uses scary tricks or threats to make people love him — that's not how God acts (that's how the "bad guys" in Revelation act, remember the beasts?). God's people are supposed to be like lights, inviting others toward him with love and truth, not scaring them.

## What This Means for Us

Every day is a chance to say "yes" to being close to God — a little more, and a little more. We don't have to be scared into it. We get to want it, because being close to God really is the best thing there is — better than any hug, any treat, any favorite day. And someday, for everyone who wants to be with him, that closeness will never end.

## Memory Verse

*"He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and there shall be no more death or mourning, wailing or pain, for the old order has passed away."*
— Revelation 21:4

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you that heaven means being close to you forever, like the best hug that never ends. Help us want to be near you every single day. Thank you for inviting us with love, not with fear. Help us love others the same way. Amen.

## For Preparation

**Craft/activity ideas:**
- **Cube craft:** help kids fold or build a small paper cube, and talk about how the Holy of Holies (and later, all of heaven!) was shaped like a cube — a reminder that heaven is about being close to God.
- **Tear-wiping picture:** have kids draw a picture of a time they were sad, then draw God's hand wiping away the tear, based on Revelation 21:4.
- **"Close to God" chart:** brainstorm together simple everyday ways kids can choose to be close to God (prayer, kindness, Mass, listening to parents) versus choosing to drift away.

**Discussion questions:**
1. What's the best hug you've ever had, and how is heaven like a hug that never ends?
2. Why do you think God invites us to be close to him instead of forcing us?
3. What's one way you can choose to be close to God this week?
