# The Thousand-Year Kingdom

## Game/Hook: The Secret Prince or Princess

Play "Secret Royalty": before the session, secretly tell one or two kids they are a "secret
prince" or "secret princess" for the day — they don't tell anyone, but they know it's true. Give
them a small hidden token (a sticker under their chair, a paper crown folded in a pocket). At the
end of the game, ask the group: "If you were secretly a prince or princess, but nobody could tell
just by looking at you, would it still be true?" Let them answer, then reveal the secret royalty
and give them their crown.

Ask: "Do you think you have to wait until you're a grown-up, or wait for something amazing to
happen, to matter to Jesus? Or could it be true right now, even in ordinary, hard, or boring
moments?" Today's story is about exactly that.

## The Story: Kings and Queens Right Now

In Revelation, John sees people who loved Jesus so much that they wouldn't stop following him,
even when it was hard, even when others made fun of them or hurt them for it. And John sees
something amazing: these people are described as reigning with Jesus — like kings and queens —
not someday, far in the future, but starting the moment they stayed loyal to him.

Some Christians think this means there's a faraway future time, a thousand years long, when Jesus
will set up a kingdom on earth. But when you look closely at what John actually wrote, it seems to
mean something even better: Jesus' kingdom isn't just a someday thing. It's happening right now,
through his Church, through every person who trusts and follows him — including you.

Here's the really encouraging part. The Bible says that even when following Jesus costs you
something — when it's hard, when you get teased, when you have to make a tough choice to do the
right thing instead of the easy thing — that moment of staying loyal to Jesus is already you
"reigning" with him, like royalty. You don't have to wait for some big future moment to matter to
Jesus. You already matter to him enormously, right now, every time you choose to stay faithful to
him.

This is actually what the Catholic Church has believed and taught for a very, very long time — all
the way back to a wise, early Christian named Augustine. So this isn't some brand-new idea. It is
what your own Church family has quietly believed for centuries: Jesus reigns now, and everyone who
follows him shares in that reign now, too.

## What This Means for Us

You don't have to do something huge or wait to grow up to matter to Jesus. Every time you choose
kindness when it's hard, tell the truth when it would be easier to lie, stand up for someone who's
being picked on, or keep praying even when you don't feel like it — you are being loyal to Jesus
the same way those brave believers in Revelation were. And Jesus says that loyalty, however small
it looks from the outside, is you sharing in his kingdom right now. You are already treasured and
important to him today.

## Memory Verse

*"You have made them a kingdom and priests to our God, and they shall reign on earth."*
— Revelation 5:10

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you for making us part of your kingdom right now, not just someday. Help us stay
loyal to you even when it's hard — when we're tempted to be unkind, to lie, or to leave you out of
our choices. Help us remember that every time we choose you, we're already sharing in your
kingdom. Amen.

## For Preparation

**Craft/activity ideas:**
- **Make a paper crown:** have kids decorate simple paper crowns and write inside them one way
  they can stay loyal to Jesus this week, even if it's hard (for example: "tell the truth," "be
  kind to someone who's mean to me," "pray even when I don't feel like it").
- **"Royalty right now" cards:** give kids small cards to decorate that say "I already matter to
  Jesus TODAY" to keep in their room or backpack as a reminder.
- **Act it out:** have kids take turns acting out a hard choice (being teased for praying before
  lunch, being honest even if it means getting in trouble) and then "crown" them with a paper
  crown afterward to show that choosing Jesus is already reigning with him.

**Discussion questions:**
1. Have you ever felt like following Jesus was hard or cost you something (even something small,
   like being made fun of)? What happened?
2. Why do you think staying loyal to Jesus, even in small moments, counts as "reigning" with him
   right now instead of just being a future reward?
3. What's one way you can choose to be loyal to Jesus this week, even if it's a little bit hard?
