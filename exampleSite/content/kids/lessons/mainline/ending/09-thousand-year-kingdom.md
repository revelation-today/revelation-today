# Reigning Right Now
### The thousand-year kingdom

## 1. What does a king do?

Ask the kids: what do you picture when you imagine a king or queen reigning? A crown? A
palace? Ruling over people? Now ask: could someone be "reigning like a king" even without
a crown or a palace — just by staying faithful and brave when it's hard?

Today's story is about a "thousand-year kingdom" in Revelation, and it's not quite what
most people picture.

## 2. A confusing number

In Revelation 20, we read about believers reigning with Jesus for "a thousand years."
Lots of people have wondered: is this a thousand actual years sometime far in the future,
separate from everything else?

Here's a big clue: right before this, chapter 20 describes God's enemy, Satan, being tied
up — and that same "tying up" is described elsewhere in the Bible as something Jesus
*already did* when he died and rose again (Mark 3:27 talks about Jesus tying up "the
strong man" first). So this "thousand years" isn't a brand new, separate event far off in
the future — it's describing something that's already happening, right now, in the whole
time between Jesus's first coming and his second coming.

## 3. This has been the church's answer for a long time

Many, many years ago, church leaders looked carefully at this same passage and reached
the same conclusion: the "thousand years" describes the whole time the church has been
living in, right now — not some completely separate future era. That's actually written
down in some very old church confessions still used today! This isn't a new idea — it's
what your own church tradition has taught for a very long time.

## 4. Reigning by staying faithful

So who is reigning during these "thousand years"? Revelation 20 says it's everyone who
stayed faithful to Jesus, even when it was hard — even people who were treated badly for
their faith. That's surprising! It means staying faithful to Jesus, even in hard or
ordinary moments, is itself a way of "reigning" with him — right now, not just someday.

You don't need a crown or a palace to reign with Jesus. You just need to stay faithful to
him today, even in small ways.

## 5. What this means for us

This week, when you choose to tell the truth even though it's hard, or be kind when
someone isn't kind to you, or stand up for what's right — you're actually reigning with
Jesus in exactly the way this passage describes. That's pretty amazing for something that
doesn't look like a crown or a throne at all!

**Memory verse:** "They came to life and reigned with Christ." (Revelation 20:4)

## 6. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that we can reign with you right now, just by staying faithful to
you in small, ordinary ways. Help us to be brave and honest today, even when it's hard.
Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening activity:** The "what does a king do" discussion above — lead kids toward the
idea that faithfulness, not a crown, is what this passage means by "reigning."

**Craft/discussion idea:** Make simple paper crowns, and on the inside write one way each
child can "reign with Jesus" this week by staying faithful in something small.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What surprises you about the idea that staying faithful to Jesus is itself "reigning"
   with him?
2. Can you think of a small, everyday way you could "reign with Jesus" this week?
3. Why do you think it matters that Satan being "tied up" is something Jesus already did,
   not just something that will happen far in the future?
