# The Promise God Keeps Even When We Break It
### God's covenant: a story of amazing grace

## 1. A promise game

Ask a volunteer: "If I promise to give you my dessert every day this week, but you have to
promise to feed my dog every day — and then I break my promise on day two, what should
happen to your promise?" Let the kids discuss. Most fair deals fall apart when one side
breaks their word.

Today we're going to hear about a promise where that's *not* what happens.

## 2. How promises worked long ago

A long, long time ago, when two groups of people wanted to promise to take care of each
other, they had a special ceremony. They would cut some animals in half and lay the
halves in two lines on the ground. Then the person making the promise would walk between
the pieces. It was a scary way of saying, "If I break this promise, let what happened to
these animals happen to me."

## 3. God's surprising promise to Abraham

God made a promise like this to a man named Abraham — he promised Abraham a family and a
land. And in the ceremony, guess who walked between the pieces of the animals? Not
Abraham. God himself, appearing like a burning fire, passed through the middle.

That's backwards from how it normally worked! God was saying: "If this promise is ever
broken, I will take the punishment — not you." And later, when God's people did break
their promises, that's exactly what happened: God's own Son, Jesus, took the punishment
that we deserved.

## 4. God's promise at Sinai

Later, God made another special promise with his people, called Israel, at a mountain
called Sinai. He said, "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of slavery in
Egypt — look at everything I've already done for you! Now here's what I ask: love me
only, and love each other."

God's rules weren't too hard to keep — his people really could have obeyed them. But they
kept breaking their promises anyway.

## 5. Does God give up?

Here's the amazing part: God didn't give up on his people, even when they broke their
promises again and again. The Bible has a special word for this: "hesed" — it means God's
steady, faithful love that keeps showing up no matter what.

Think about it like a parent who keeps loving their child even after that child messes up,
says sorry, and messes up again. That's a small picture of hesed — except God's faithful
love never runs out at all.

## 6. Why this matters for Revelation

This helps us understand Revelation! The letters to the seven churches follow the same
pattern as God's old promises: God says what he's already done for each church, then what
he wants from them, and then what will happen — a blessing if they stay faithful, trouble
if they don't. And just like at Sinai, God keeps giving his people more chances to turn
back to him.

And the whole book of Revelation ends with a wedding — because a promise, in the Bible,
is often pictured as a marriage. God has kept his promise all the way to the very end,
marrying his people forever.

## 7. What this means for us

We break our promises to God all the time — big ones and small ones. But God's promise to
us doesn't depend on us keeping ours perfectly. It depends on Jesus, who already took the
punishment for us. That's amazing grace.

**Memory verse:** "The steadfast love of the LORD never ceases; his mercies never come to
an end; they are new every morning." (Lamentations 3:22–23)

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for keeping your promise to us even when we don't keep ours to you.
Thank you that Jesus took the punishment we deserved. Help us to trust your steady,
faithful love every day. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening activity:** The promise discussion above — use two stuffed animals or props to
act out an "unfair" promise, then reveal how God's promise works differently.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Kids draw a picture with two halves — on one side, something
they've promised to do and sometimes forget; on the other side, a cross, representing that
Jesus already paid for their broken promises.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Has anyone ever kept a promise to you even after you broke yours to them? How did that feel?
2. What does the word "hesed" — God's steady, faithful love — mean to you?
3. Why do you think God's promise to us doesn't depend on us being perfect?
