# Our Forever Home
### Heaven, hell, and the New Jerusalem

## 1. What makes home feel like home?

Ask the kids: what makes your home feel like home — is it the furniture, or the people you
love who are there with you? Most kids will say it's the people. Today we're learning
about our forever home — and what makes it truly "home" isn't buildings or streets of
gold. It's being close to God.

## 2. A beautiful city

At the very end of Revelation, John sees an amazing city coming down from heaven — the New
Jerusalem. It has walls made of jewels, gates made of pearl, and streets like gold. But
the most important thing about it isn't the decorations. Revelation tells us: "God's
dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them" (Revelation 21:3).

The whole point of this beautiful city is that God lives there, right with his people,
forever. That's what makes it heaven — not the jewels, but being close to God.

## 3. A special shape

The city is shaped like a perfect cube — like a giant square box. That might seem like an
odd detail, but here's why it matters: in the old temple in the Bible, there was one very
special room shaped exactly like a cube — the Most Holy Place, where God's presence lived
so powerfully that only one priest could go in, only once a year!

Now, in the New Jerusalem, *everyone* who belongs to God gets to live inside that same
kind of space — as close to God as that one priest used to get, and forever. That's the
biggest reversal in the whole Bible: the door that used to be shut to almost everyone is
now open to all of God's people.

## 4. No more tears

Revelation also promises: "He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more
death or mourning or crying or pain" (Revelation 21:4). Heaven isn't just a place without
bad things — it's a place completely filled with God's good presence, which is exactly why
the bad things can't exist there anymore.

## 5. What about the sad answer — hell?

Revelation also tells us that some people choose to stay far from God forever. That's a
hard thing to think about. But here's an important way to understand it: hell isn't God
being cruel — it's what's left when someone finally, completely refuses to be close to
God. All the good things we love — joy, peace, hope — actually come from God in the first
place. Being without him forever means being without all of that too.

That's exactly why Jesus invites everyone, right now, to come close to him — because this
is the time to choose to be near him forever.

## 6. What this means for us

We don't have to be afraid of the future if we belong to Jesus. Our forever home isn't
about how shiny the streets are — it's about being close to the God who loves us, with no
more tears, forever.

**Memory verse:** "God's dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with
them." (Revelation 21:3)

## 7. Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing a forever home for us where you live with us and wipe
away every tear. Help us to want to be close to you, today and always. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening activity:** The "what makes home feel like home" discussion above.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Have kids draw a picture of the New Jerusalem, but tell them
they must include God in the picture somewhere — to reinforce that God's presence, not the
decorations, is what makes it home.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. What makes a place feel like "home" to you?
2. Why do you think it matters more that God lives in the New Jerusalem than that it has
   gold streets and jewels?
3. How does knowing Jesus invites us close to him now help you think about forever?
