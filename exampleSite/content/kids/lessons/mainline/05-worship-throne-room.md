# The Room Where Everything Starts
### Worship in the throne room

## 1. What comes first?

Ask the kids: before you build a tall tower of blocks, what do you need first? (A strong
base/foundation.) If the base is wobbly, what happens to everything built on top?

Today we're looking at the "base" of the whole book of Revelation — a room in heaven that
everything else in the book is built on top of.

## 2. John sees a throne room

Before any of the scary or exciting events in Revelation happen, John is shown a vision of
heaven — and right at the center of it is a throne, with God sitting on it, surrounded by
worship that never, ever stops. Every single thing that happens later in the book — every
plague, every battle, every ending — comes out of this one room.

That's a huge clue for how to understand the whole book: it all starts with worship, not
with disaster.

## 3. A rainbow and a promise

Around the throne, John sees a rainbow (Revelation 4:3). Does that remind you of another
story? After the flood in Noah's time, God put a rainbow in the sky as a promise: "I will
never destroy the earth with a flood again" (Genesis 9). That same rainbow shows up here,
around God's throne — a reminder that even when hard things happen later in the book, God
keeps his promises and is still in control.

## 4. Everyone joins in

John also sees creatures that represent all of creation — like a lion, an ox, an eagle,
and a human — worshiping around the throne, along with 24 elders wearing crowns. It's a
picture of everyone and everything joining together in worship: all of creation, and all
of God's people, past and present, together.

## 5. Worship answers everything

Here's the important part: in the two chapters before this (Revelation 2–3), Jesus had
just told seven churches about their problems — some had wrong beliefs, some had given up
too easily, some thought they were doing great when they weren't. And now, right after
all that, John sees this throne room of worship. That's because worship is God's answer to
every one of those problems. When we truly see how great God is, we stop being fooled by
wrong ideas, we see ourselves clearly, and our priorities fall into the right place.

## 6. Like our own church

This throne room actually looks a little like what happens in church! We come together, we
confess when we've done wrong, we hear God's Word, and we worship together — just like the
never-ending worship John saw in heaven. When you sing at church, you're joining in with
something that's been happening in heaven this whole time!

## 7. What this means for us

Whatever hard thing you're facing this week, remember: the whole story starts and ends
with God on his throne, worthy of worship, and totally in control. That doesn't erase
hard things, but it means we don't face them alone or without hope.

**Memory verse:** "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to
come." (Revelation 4:8)

## 8. Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you are always on your throne, no matter what's happening around
us. Help us to worship you not just at church, but every day. Remind us that you are in
control even when things feel scary. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening activity:** Build a tower of blocks together, starting with a wobbly base versus
a strong one, to illustrate how worship is the strong "base" for everything in Revelation.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Draw a simple rainbow and write "God keeps his promises" on it,
to take home as a reminder.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. Why do you think John sees worship in heaven before anything else happens in the book?
2. What does the rainbow around God's throne remind you of?
3. How does knowing God is always on his throne help you when you're scared or worried?
