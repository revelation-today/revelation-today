# Who Is Really in Charge?
### A story from the time Revelation was written

## 1. A silly game

Let's play a quick game. Every time I raise my hand, you have to jump up, clap, and shout,
"You're the best!" Anyone who doesn't do it has to sit out a round.

*(Play once or twice, then ask:)*

- How did that feel?
- Did the rule seem fair?
- What if you had to do that every single day — even on days you didn't feel like it was true?

Something like that game — except it was completely real, not for fun — is what the first
Christians had to face. That's what we're talking about today.

## 2. Long ago: the emperor wants everyone to say "yes"

When the last book of the Bible, Revelation, was written, almost the whole known world
belonged to the Roman Empire. At the very top was one man: the emperor.

The emperor said, "I take care of everything — safety, enough food, peace, a good life. All
I ask in return is that everyone show that I'm the greatest — almost like a god."

You could see it everywhere: his face was on every coin, there were huge statues of him,
and at parties everyone had to raise a cup and praise him. Anyone who didn't do it got in
trouble — sometimes very serious trouble.

## 3. The problem: Christians already had a King

The Christians thought: "Wait a minute. We already have a King — Jesus! And food, safety,
and a good life come from God, not from the emperor." So they couldn't honestly shout,
"The emperor is the greatest!"

It's a bit like if someone in your class suddenly announced, "From now on, everyone laughs
at the new kid, or you're not part of the group anymore." Some kids laugh along anyway —
not because they think it's funny, but because they want to belong. That's exactly the
choice the first Christians faced, every single day: go along with it completely and act
like they didn't believe in Jesus anymore; say "no" completely, even if it meant real
trouble; or quietly go along on the outside while secretly still believing in Jesus on the
inside.

## 4. John — someone who said no

The man who wrote Revelation was named John. He tells us himself: because he wouldn't go
along with it, he was sent away to a lonely island called Patmos — far from home.

But right there, all alone, God showed John an enormous, encouraging vision: Jesus is
alive, and Jesus truly reigns — far more than any emperor ever could. The whole book John
wrote afterward was meant to give Christians courage to stay with Jesus, no matter what.

## 5. And us today? Who is our "emperor"?

Nobody today has to cheer for a statue of an emperor. But the same question is still
around, just wearing new clothes: **Who do you trust the most? Who do you most want to
make happy?**

A few things to think about: Is it more important that other kids think you're cool than
that you do the right thing? Do you feel safer with your phone or your games than talking
to God? Do you go along with something you know is wrong, just so you fit in?

Those are our "emperors" today — things or people we trust more, or care about more, than
Jesus, without always noticing it.

## 6. The frog in the warm water

You may know the old story: throw a frog into boiling water, and it jumps right out. But
warm the water up very slowly, and the frog doesn't notice the danger until it's too
late. *(That's not quite true biologically, but it's a good picture.)*

That's how it can sneak up on us too. Nobody forces us to forget Jesus overnight. It
happens little by little — a small excuse here, a little bit of going-along there — until
we don't even notice anymore who has really become the "boss" of our life.

## 7. What helps us

The good news: we don't have to hide, and we don't have to go along with things just to
belong. Jesus is already the true King — much stronger than any emperor, trend, or crowd.
He invites us to honestly belong to him, to trust him, and to not hide him away.

**Memory verse:** "No one can serve two masters." (Matthew 6:24)

## 8. Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are really the King — much stronger than anything that
scares us or anything we want to seem cool for. Help us to stand honestly for you, even
when it's hard. Show us when we secretly trust something else more than you. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening game:** The "You're the Best" game above — discuss afterward how it felt.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Each child draws a small crown. On one side they write or draw
what sometimes acts like an "emperor" in their daily life (phone, being cool, candy,
sports...). On the other side they write "Jesus." Discuss: whose crown should really be
the biggest?

**Questions for the kids:**
1. When is it hard to say "no" when others are doing something wrong?
2. What would you do if someone laughed at you for believing in Jesus?
3. What is sometimes more important to you than Jesus, even though you don't really want it to be?

**Song suggestions:** A simple song about trusting God with your time; a song calling
Jesus the true King; a familiar movement song about courage and trust.
