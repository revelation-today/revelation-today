# The Copycat Team
### The unholy trinity

## 1. Spot the fake

Show the kids two similar-looking items — for example, a real coin and a play/toy coin,
or a name-brand snack and a lookalike knockoff. Ask: how can you tell which one is real?

Today's story is about a fake "team" that tries to copy God, but gets found out.

## 2. Three bad guys who copy the real Three

In Revelation 13, we meet a dragon (that's Satan) and two beasts — one from the sea, one
from the land. Together, they're trying to copy something: God the Father, Jesus the Son,
and the Holy Spirit — three Persons, one God.

Here's how the copy works: the dragon gives all his power to the first beast — kind of
like how God the Father gives Jesus all authority. The first beast looks like he was
killed and then came back to life — copying Jesus's death and resurrection. And the second
beast makes everyone worship the first beast — copying how the Holy Spirit points
everyone's attention to Jesus.

It's a fake trinity, dressed up to look like the real one.

## 3. How can you tell it's fake?

The Bible gives us a big clue: the second, fake "spirit" character has horns like a lamb
(looks gentle, like Jesus) — but it speaks like a dragon (Revelation 13:11). Looks aren't
enough to tell the difference. You have to listen to the voice.

Jesus said, "My sheep listen to my voice... they will never follow a stranger" (John
10:4–5). That means the way to spot a fake isn't just by how something looks — it's by
really knowing Jesus well enough to recognize when something doesn't sound like him.

## 4. What does the fake team do wrong?

The fake team tries to get what it wants through scaring people and forcing them, not
through love. That's the giveaway. Jesus never forces anyone to love him or follow him. He
invites. He gave his life out of love, not to make people afraid.

So whenever anyone — even people at church! — tries to scare people or force people
instead of loving them, that's a warning sign they might be following the copycat pattern
instead of Jesus's real pattern.

## 5. Not everything powerful is fake

Does this mean anything powerful or amazing must be a trick? No! God does real, amazing
things too — miracles, answered prayers, real victories. The difference isn't "powerful
versus weak." It's love versus fear. God's power always comes wrapped in love. The fake
team's power always comes wrapped in fear.

## 6. What this means for us

We can ask ourselves: when I want people to do the right thing, do I try to scare them or
love them into it? Following Jesus's pattern means choosing love, even when scaring people
might seem like it would work faster.

**Memory verse:** "My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me." (John
10:27)

## 7. Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to really know your voice, so we're not fooled by things that look
like you but aren't. Help us to love people the way you do, instead of trying to scare or
force them. Amen.

## Appendix: Ideas for leaders

**Opening activity:** The "spot the fake" game above with two similar items.

**Craft/discussion idea:** Make two simple puppets or masks — one "kind" looking and one
"scary" looking — and have kids act out a short skit where the "kind-looking" one turns
out to speak unkindly, to illustrate that looks aren't enough.

**Questions for the kids:**
1. How do you tell the difference between something real and a good copy?
2. Why do you think Jesus said his sheep "know his voice"? How do we get to know it better?
3. Can you think of a time someone tried to get you to do something through fear, instead
   of through love or kindness?
