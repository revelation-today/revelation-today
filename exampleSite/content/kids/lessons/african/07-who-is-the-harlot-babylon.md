## A beautiful woman with a terrible secret

In Revelation 17, John sees a woman dressed in gold, jewels, and fine clothing — she looks rich, glamorous, and powerful. Her name is Babylon. But there's a terrible secret hidden underneath all that glitter: her beauty was built on hurting people.

## What she promises

Babylon promises: "Follow me, and you'll have everything — money, comfort, an easy life, and everyone will respect you." That sounds wonderful, doesn't it? But look at her cargo list in chapter 18 — gold, jewels, spices... and then, right in the same list, "human beings" — real people, treated like things to be bought and sold. That's the terrible secret: her riches come at other people's expense.

She even says, "I will never be sad. Nothing bad can happen to me" (Revelation 18:7). She feels completely safe and untouchable, because of all her wealth.

## What happens to her

But guess what happens? In just one hour, everything falls apart — everyone who depended on her, everyone who helped make her rich, suddenly turns against her (Revelation 18:9-10). Anything built only on money and power, instead of on God, can fall apart in a single moment, without any warning at all.

## A gentle word about money and blessing

Sometimes people teach that if you truly have faith, God will always make you rich and comfortable right away. It's true that God is generous and loves to bless his children! But Babylon's story reminds us: real blessing from God isn't about having lots of stuff or an easy life. It's about God himself being with us — which is worth far more than money, and never falls apart.

## The real treasure

Revelation doesn't end with Babylon's sad story. A few chapters later, we see something far better: the New Jerusalem, where God lives with his people forever, where nobody cries anymore, and where God gives good things freely, "without cost," to anyone who's thirsty (Revelation 21:6). That's the real treasure — better than any amount of gold.

## Talk about it

What's the difference between being rich and being truly blessed? Why do you think Babylon's riches disappeared so suddenly? What does it mean that God gives us good things "without cost"?

**Memory verse:** "To the thirsty I will give water without cost from the spring of the water of life." (Revelation 21:6)

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for being so generous with us. Help us to know that the best gift you give us is yourself, not just things. Help us to never trust money more than we trust you. Amen.
