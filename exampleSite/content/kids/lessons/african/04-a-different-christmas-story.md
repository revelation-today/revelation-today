## A strange Christmas picture

You know the Christmas story: baby Jesus, a manger, shepherds, a star. But did you know the Bible tells the Christmas story a second time — in a very different, very dramatic way? It's in Revelation chapter 12, and it goes like this.

## The big scene

John sees a woman in the sky, wearing the sun like clothing, standing on the moon, with twelve stars like a crown on her head. She is about to have a baby, and she's in pain. Suddenly, a huge, terrifying red dragon appears — with seven heads and ten horns! The dragon stands right in front of the woman, waiting for her baby to be born so it can eat him.

Doesn't that sound frightening? A giant dragon versus a mother about to give birth — who do you think would win?

The woman has her baby. And right when the dragon reaches for him — the baby is snatched up to heaven, safe with God! The dragon completely fails.

## Who are they?

The dragon is Satan — the devil himself. The baby is Jesus. And the woman represents God's people, who were promised, long before Jesus was born, that they would bring the Rescuer into the world.

This happened for real: Jesus was born into a hard time, when God's people were being pushed around by a powerful empire. A king even tried to kill baby Jesus! But God protected him. Later, the devil tried again to trick Jesus into doing wrong — and failed again. Even when Jesus died on the cross, that wasn't the end — God raised him back to life!

## The dragon always loses

Here's the pattern you'll notice again and again in Revelation: things that look weak and helpless — like a baby, or a mother giving birth — turn out to win. And things that look huge and scary and unstoppable — like the dragon — turn out to lose.

## A note about spiritual battles

Maybe your family or church talks about spirits, curses, or spiritual attacks — that's something the Bible takes seriously too! But look at what this story actually tells us: there is one dragon, already defeated, because of what Jesus did. We don't need to be afraid of a complicated list of things to fight against. Jesus already won the real battle.

## Talk about it

Have you ever felt like something scary or big was about to win, but it didn't? Why do you think God let the dragon almost win before Jesus was safely taken to heaven? What does it mean that the "biggest, scariest" thing in the story completely fails?

**Memory verse:** "Her child was snatched up to God and to his throne." (Revelation 12:5)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you already beat the dragon. Thank you that nothing is too big or scary for you. When we feel afraid, help us remember that you have already won. Amen.
