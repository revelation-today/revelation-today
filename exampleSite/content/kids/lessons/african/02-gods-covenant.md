## Belonging to a family

Who is in your family? Your mother, father, brothers, sisters, grandparents, aunties, uncles — maybe a big extended family. Family is important, isn't it? Family helps you, protects you, feeds you.

But what if you weren't born into a family like that? Long ago, there was another way to become part of a family: making a very serious promise called a covenant. Today's story is about the biggest covenant promise ever made — between God and his people.

## A scary kind of promise

A long time ago, when two groups of people made a serious deal, they did something dramatic to show they meant it. They would cut animals in half and lay the pieces in two lines. Then the person making the promise would walk between the pieces. It was like saying: "If I break this promise, let what happened to these animals happen to me."

## God does something surprising

When God made his special promise to Abraham, the animals were cut in half, just like always. But here's the surprising part: it wasn't Abraham who walked between the pieces. It was God himself — shown as fire — who walked through!

God was saying: "If this promise gets broken, I will take the punishment. Not you."

And later, when people did break their promises to God, guess who took the punishment? Not us. God's own Son, Jesus, did — on the cross.

## God's promise to his people

At Mount Sinai, God made a big promise to his people, Israel. He said, "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of slavery in Egypt." First he reminded them of everything good he had already done. Then he gave them his rules — starting with the most important one: love only me, don't chase after other gods.

Here's something important: God's rules were never impossible to keep. His people could have obeyed. They just chose not to, again and again. And even then, God stayed loyal to his promise.

## A Father who keeps his word

The Bible has a beautiful word for the way God loves his people even when they mess up: hesed. It means God's love that never gives up, his loyalty that never runs out.

## Why this matters

Some people think God is like a strict teacher waiting to catch you doing something wrong. But that's not true! God made a promise, and he already paid the price himself, through Jesus, before we ever did anything to deserve it. Because of that promise, everyone who trusts Jesus becomes part of God's own family — a family even stronger and more certain than any family here on earth.

## Talk about it

What does it feel like to know God already kept his promise, even before you were born? Is there someone in your family who always keeps their word, no matter what? How is God like that, and even better?

**Memory verse:** "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of Egypt." (Exodus 20:2)

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for your amazing promise. Thank you that you already loved us and kept your word before we ever did anything right. Help us to trust you like family — the best family there is. Amen.
