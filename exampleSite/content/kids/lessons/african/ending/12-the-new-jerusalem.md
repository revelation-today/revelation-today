## A brand new everything

At the very end of Revelation, John sees something amazing: a brand new heaven and a brand new earth! Everything old and broken is gone, and everything is made completely new.

## God lives with us

John hears a loud voice say: "Look! God's home is now with his people! He will live with them" (Revelation 21:3). Can you imagine that — God, living right there with us, not far away at all?

And then this beautiful promise: "God will wipe away every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death, no more sadness, no more crying, no more pain" (Revelation 21:4). Every sad thing that ever happened gets healed, personally, by God himself.

## A very special shape

The New Jerusalem is shaped like a perfect cube — like a big box with all equal sides. Long ago, only one place in the whole Bible had that shape: the most holy room in the temple, where only one priest could go, only once a year, very carefully. But now, in the New Jerusalem, that special shape is the whole city — meaning everyone gets to be that close to God, all the time, forever!

## Everyone welcome

People from every country, every language, every nation come into this city, bringing honor to God together (Revelation 21:24). Nobody is left out who wants to be there.

## Free water for everyone

At the end, there's a beautiful river flowing right from God's own throne, and a tree whose fruit anyone can eat. And God says: "To anyone who is thirsty I will give water from the spring of life — for free" (Revelation 21:6). No cost, no charge — just a free gift from God.

## Talk about it

What do you think it will feel like when God wipes away every tear? Why do you think it's special that everyone gets to be close to God now, not just one priest? What are you most looking forward to about our forever home with God?

**Memory verse:** "God will wipe away every tear from their eyes." (Revelation 21:4)

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you for preparing an amazing forever home for us, where you will live with us and wipe away every tear. Thank you that everyone who loves you is welcome there. We can't wait! Amen.
