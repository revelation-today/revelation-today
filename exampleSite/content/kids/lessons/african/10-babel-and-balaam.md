## A big tower

Long, long ago, people decided to build an enormous tower, tall enough to reach the sky. Why? They wanted to make a great name for themselves, without God. Do you think that worked?

It didn't! God simply came to look at their tower, and their big plan fell apart completely. Later, God did something far better than what they were trying to do for themselves — he made an amazing promise to a man named Abraham, and gave him a name and a family bigger and more lasting than any tower could ever be.

## A trick with a feast

Here's another story. Long ago, a man named Balaam couldn't say bad things about God's people, no matter how hard he tried — every time he opened his mouth to curse them, blessing came out instead! So he thought of another way to cause trouble: he suggested inviting God's people to a big, friendly feast. At that feast, some of God's people started worshiping other things instead of the one true God.

Notice something interesting: the trouble didn't come from someone attacking or fighting God's people. It came from something that felt warm and friendly — an invitation, a feast, a party. That's exactly what made it tricky to notice.

## The good news: Jesus is enough

Here's the best part, and the one thing we really want you to remember today: Jesus already gives us everything we need. We don't have to add anything else to make ourselves safe, or important, or loved. Jesus' love is already complete, all by itself.

Sometimes it can be easy to think we need extra things alongside Jesus to feel truly safe or truly blessed. But Jesus alone is enough. He doesn't need any help from anything else to take care of us, to love us, or to make us belong to his family.

## Talk about it

Can you think of a time someone invited you to do something that seemed fun and friendly, but wasn't actually a good idea? What does it mean that Jesus is "enough" all by himself? Why do you think it's important to talk to a parent or trusted grown-up if you're ever not sure whether something is a good idea?

**Memory verse:** "You shall have no other gods before me." (Exodus 20:3)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that you are enough — we don't need to add anything else to feel safe or loved by you. Help us to always come to a trusted grown-up when we're not sure about something. Thank you for loving us completely, just as we are. Amen.
