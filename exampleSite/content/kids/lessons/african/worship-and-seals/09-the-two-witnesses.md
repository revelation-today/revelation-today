## A gentle start

Today's story is about two brave witnesses for God who face something very hard. For some of us, that might feel far away. For others, it might feel close to things we've heard about happening to Christians in some places. Wherever you are, remember: this story ends with God's people winning, even when it doesn't look that way at first.

## Two brave witnesses

In Revelation 11, John tells us about two witnesses for God who have amazing power — they can call down fire from the sky and stop the rain, just like the prophets Elijah and Moses did long ago. They wear rough, plain clothes and tell people to turn back to God.

## They lose... but they don't

After a long time telling people about God, something sad happens: their enemies attack them and kill them. Everyone celebrates, thinking the witnesses have completely lost.

But that's not the end of the story! After three and a half days, God brings them back to life, right in front of everyone, and lifts them up to heaven — just like what happened to Jesus. Their enemies thought they'd won. They hadn't.

## Why this matters

Sometimes doing the right thing — following Jesus, telling the truth, standing for what's good — costs us something. It might mean people laugh at us, or leave us out, or in some places, something much harder than that. This story tells us something important: when we stay faithful to Jesus even when it's hard, it might look like we're losing. But with God, that's actually how his people win.

## Talk about it

Have you ever done the right thing even though it was hard or cost you something? What do you think it means that the witnesses' "losing" was actually the moment they won? Who do you know who has been brave for Jesus, even when it was difficult?

**Memory verse:** "They came to life and stood on their feet." (Revelation 11:11)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us to be brave like the two witnesses, even when following you is hard. Thank you that even when it looks like we're losing, you can turn it into winning. We pray for anyone anywhere who is being brave for you right now. Amen.
