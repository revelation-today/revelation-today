## A gentle start

Today's story talks about hard times some Christians go through because they love Jesus. For some children hearing this, that might feel far away. For others, it might feel very close to home. Either way, remember: this story ends with good news for every single one of God's people.

## An army... that's really a worshiping crowd

In Revelation 7, an angel counts 144,000 people — sounds like counting soldiers for an army, doesn't it? But then John actually looks — and instead of a counted army, he sees something even better: a huge crowd, too big to count, from every nation and language, all standing together worshiping God (Revelation 7:9).

What John hears is "soldiers." What he sees is "worshipers who can't even be counted." That's the church — God's people from everywhere, sealed and kept safe by God.

## Going through hard times

The Bible says this crowd came "out of great trouble" (Revelation 7:14) — meaning they went through hard things because they loved Jesus. Sadly, that's true for some Christians even today, in some places.

But here's the good news: God says he knows every single one of them by name. Nobody is forgotten. And one day, all their sadness ends completely: "Never again will they be hungry or thirsty... God will wipe away every tear from their eyes" (Revelation 7:16-17).

## God sees you

If you or your family have ever faced hard times because you love Jesus, this story is for you especially: God sees you, God counts you, and God promises a day when every tear will be wiped away — by his own hand.

If things are peaceful for you right now, this story is still important: it teaches us to remember and pray for Christians in harder places, because we're all part of the same family.

## Talk about it

Why do you think it matters that God "counts" his people one by one? What do you think it will feel like when God wipes away every tear? Who could you pray for today who might be going through a hard time for their faith?

**Memory verse:** "God will wipe away every tear from their eyes." (Revelation 7:17)

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you know each of us by name and that you never forget your people, even in hard times. We pray for Christians anywhere who are facing danger because they love you. Please keep them safe, and help us remember them. Amen.
