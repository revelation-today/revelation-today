## Four riders

In Revelation 6, John sees four riders on four horses — white, red, black, and pale. Each one brings something difficult: fighting, hunger, and death. But the first rider, on the white horse, is tricky. He looks good at first — riding out with a crown, like a winner. Do you think he's one of the good guys?

## A trick in disguise

Actually, no! This rider is a trickster, pretending to be like Jesus. He looks victorious and important — a shiny crown, a triumphant ride — but he's actually part of the same bad news as the other three riders. He's dressed up nice, but underneath, he's not good at all.

Jesus warned his friends about exactly this: before wars and hard times come, watch out for people who "come in my name" trying to trick you (Mark 13:5-6). This rider is the very first trick — someone who looks like he's bringing good news and victory, but isn't really from God at all.

## Why this matters for us

Sometimes people promise, "Believe this, do this, and you'll immediately get everything good — money, success, an easy life, no problems at all." That can sound wonderful! But real faith in Jesus isn't a magic trick for instant blessing. Jesus himself didn't get an easy, comfortable life — he loved people through hard things, even through the cross, and that's what real love and real victory look like.

The white horse rider looks like a winner but is actually a trickster. Real love from Jesus doesn't look like a shiny shortcut — it looks like the cross, and then, after that, real victory.

## Talk about it

Has anyone ever promised you something that sounded too good to be true? How did you find out it wasn't true? How can you tell the difference between someone who really loves you and someone who's just trying to trick you?

**Memory verse:** "Watch out that no one deceives you." (Mark 13:5)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, help us not to be tricked by things that look shiny and good on the outside but aren't really from you. Help us to trust your kind of love — the kind that isn't a shortcut, but is always real. Amen.
