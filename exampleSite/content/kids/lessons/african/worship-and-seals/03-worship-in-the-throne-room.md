## The biggest room you've ever imagined

Imagine a door opens in the sky, and you can suddenly see into heaven. What would you expect to see first? Maybe angels flying? A big battle? Something scary?

That's what happens to John in Revelation chapter 4. A door opens in heaven, and John looks in. But what he sees isn't a battle at all. It's a throne — God's throne — and all around it, everyone is worshiping.

## What's in the room

Around God's throne, John sees a rainbow, shining like a beautiful green stone. Do you remember another time God used a rainbow? After the flood, God gave Noah a rainbow as a promise: "I will never flood the whole earth again." That same rainbow-promise is right there around God's throne, reminding everyone that God keeps his word.

John also sees four amazing creatures — like a lion, an ox, a man, and an eagle — representing all of creation. And twenty-four elders wearing crowns, sitting on thrones around God. Everyone in the room is doing one thing together: worshiping God, saying how great, how holy, how worthy he is.

## Why worship first?

Here's something important: before anything else happens in the whole book of Revelation — before any of the exciting or scary parts — God shows John this room of worship first. Why?

Because worship is the answer to everything. When we truly know how big and good and holy God is, we're not so easily tricked by wrong things. When we remember God is on the throne, we're not so scared of what's happening around us. Worship helps us see everything else the right way.

## A special note about "spiritual battles"

Maybe you've heard grown-ups talk about spiritual battles — about fighting against bad spirits or bad influences. That's a real thing, and it's good to take seriously! But look again at Revelation 4: God doesn't show John a battle plan or a fighting strategy. He shows him worship. That's the real secret weapon in this whole book — not clever tricks or strategies, but simply worshiping God with our whole hearts.

## Talk about it

What do you think it would feel like to be in that room with John, watching everyone worship God? What helps you worship God with your whole heart — is it songs, is it quiet, is it dancing? Why do you think worship comes before everything else in Revelation?

**Memory verse:** "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty." (Revelation 4:8)

## Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you are on your throne, and that you are good and holy and strong. Help us to worship you with our whole hearts, just like the creatures and the elders around your throne. When we're scared or confused, remind us that you are still in charge. Amen.
