## A puzzling number

Revelation 20 talks about a "thousand years" when God's people reign with Jesus. People have all kinds of ideas about what this means! Let's look at what the Bible actually says.

## Not a faraway future

Some people think the thousand years is a special time far in the future, after some huge battle. But when we look closely, it seems like the "thousand years" is actually a picture of something already happening: the whole time between when Jesus rose from the dead and when he comes back again. That means — we might already be living in it, right now!

## Reigning by staying faithful

The Bible says God's people "reigned with Christ" because they stayed faithful to Jesus, even when it was hard, even when it cost them something. That might sound surprising — how can staying faithful in hard times be like being a king or queen?

Here's the secret: when you choose to love and follow Jesus, even when it's difficult, even when nobody else notices, you are already part of something bigger — you're already reigning with Jesus, right now, even if it doesn't look like it on the outside.

## You don't have to wait

Here's the good news: you don't have to wait for some faraway future to start reigning with Jesus. Every time you choose to be kind instead of mean, honest instead of sneaky, brave instead of giving in to peer pressure — that's you reigning with Jesus, today.

## Talk about it

Can you think of a time you did something hard because you wanted to follow Jesus? How might that be like "reigning" with him? What's one way you could choose to be faithful to Jesus this week, even if it's small?

**Memory verse:** "They came to life and reigned with Christ." (Revelation 20:4)

## Prayer

Dear Jesus, thank you that we don't have to wait to reign with you — we can start today, by staying faithful even in small things. Help us to choose you, even when it's hard. Amen.
