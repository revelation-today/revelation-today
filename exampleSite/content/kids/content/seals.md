## Overview

Now that Jesus is the one worthy to open the seals, the story moves straight on.

What happens when he does? Well, there's someone who would have loved to be in Jesus' place and to hold the scroll himself. That's Satan (we'll meet him a few more times). Nobody's told you that yet, because everyone already knows he's evil, right?

Does everyone really know that? How many people do you know who don't believe in Jesus, but believe in all sorts of other things instead? With the seals, Jesus makes it completely clear to everyone that Satan is not the good guy.

At the first seal, Satan still gets to show off as the great hero. But already at the second seal, he's revealed as someone who's always starting fights. At the third seal, he's the one causing so much suffering that people go hungry, and by the fourth seal it's completely clear that all Satan ever brings, in the end, is death.

That's actually a good thing - now everyone understands that Satan is evil. But we who already knew that still suffer because of it. So everyone who's suffering cries out, and Jesus says: you're right.

And then it happens: Jesus clears the table and simply sweeps the whole world away. Everyone is completely overwhelmed. They didn't expect Jesus to be that strong, with everything under his control.

But Jesus isn't the only strong one. He also has an army - we'll look at that next time.
