## The Story

Now we see the third and final mystery. It's the harlot, who sits on a beast.

But what actually is a "harlot"? In the Bible, it's someone who looks beautiful on the outside but is evil on the inside. And she sits on the beast we know from the story with the dragon (the violent first beast from the sea).

This harlot tells the kings of the world what to do, and she delights in people's suffering. Everything about her is fake and cruel.

The harlot sits on the beast, which also controls all the kings. God laughs at this beast, because it has no power at all once God gets involved. God has a plan: the beast hates the bride (a picture for the church, and also for heaven) and wants to destroy her, with the kings' help. And God uses exactly that to judge the harlot.

The harlot is trying to look as beautiful as the bride. So God leads all the kings to attack the fake bride - and that's the harlot! Satan's kingdom destroys itself: the harlot gets wiped out by the very beast and kings who always controlled her.

Now we learn more about the harlot. All the kings weep over her now. But really, they never cared about her at all - they only cared about what they could get from her. And once she's destroyed, all they do is complain about their loss.

But all the good people rejoice that the harlot is finally finished. Now that she's gone, Jesus can finally marry the bride. And that's going to be a truly great celebration.

And right after that, Jesus goes out and defeats the beast with his great army (the one that gathered at Armageddon). Jesus gathers an army too (of birds!) - but he defeats the other army entirely on his own. Afterward the birds have to clean up the whole battlefield, because Jesus won so completely.

## Overview

The harlot is the opposite of the bride, and shows exactly how the bride should never be.

As powerful and unbeatable as the harlot looks, sitting on her beast, God simply laughs at her - and her own strength becomes her downfall, because she is evil and wants evil, even toward her own friends.
