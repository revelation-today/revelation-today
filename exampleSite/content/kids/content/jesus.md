## Overview

Did you know there's actually a Christmas story hidden in the book of Revelation?

Yes - but it's a little different from the one you hear at Christmas, and maybe even your parents don't know this version. But here it is.

A huge picture is used. First you see a woman, clothed with the sun, moon, and stars (that's Israel), who's about to have a baby. But then there's also a giant, fiery red dragon who wants to destroy the child. That sounds like a hopeless fight for the new mother, because the dragon has a third of all the angels with him, and wants to destroy the child as soon as it's born.

On top of that, a woman with a big belly, about to give birth, isn't exactly a great fighter either.

But then the miracle happens. The dragon fails to destroy Jesus - instead, Jesus is caught up to God. Whoa, that was fast! Are we already at the ascension? Yes - because you already know the rest of the story from Sunday school: Satan keeps trying to kill Jesus, and when he finally manages it, Jesus simply rises from the dead.

As you've probably already guessed, the dragon was, of course, Satan, and the woman wasn't Mary, but the people of Israel.

But the story continues. As you can see, this is about Satan - and about how, even though he looks so terrifying, he's the one who always loses in the end.

Now the story gets told a second time, this time from heaven's point of view. Here too, he loses, and gets thrown out of heaven by Michael. Now he's so furious that all he wants to do is destroy everything, because he doesn't care about anything anymore - his end could come at any moment.

We get to look forward to Jesus coming back - but Satan trembles just thinking about it.

First, he attacks the woman again, but he fails, because even nature is against him: the earth swallows up the water he tries to use to drown her.

Like we said: no matter how terrifying Satan looks, he always loses in the end. Just like in the next chapter.
