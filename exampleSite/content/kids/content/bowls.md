## The Story

Now it gets serious. God's wrath is coming. God isn't holding back anymore - he goes fully on the offensive.

First, God's wrath is aimed at everyone who went along with Satan. Afterward, they look pretty ugly, because of all the ugly things they did together with him.

They have to go through disgusting things too, because of all the mean things they did to God's children.

And because of their stubbornness - because they refused to change, or apologize, or anything like that - it gets so unbearably hot and cold for them that they bite their own tongues, and still they refuse to change.

And then what has to happen, happens. They all believe the great lie and gather at a place called Armageddon (which isn't a real place, but a code name). They gather there to fight against what looks evil to them. But Satan has tricked all of them - because they're all going to lose, and be completely humiliated, since they've come to fight the one truly good side.

And then it's simply over, because God puts an end to it. But we'll look at that in the next stories.

## Overview

What does it mean when God is angry? Does he just get moody and start yelling?

No - anger, here, is almost the opposite. God's anger is his passionate determination to keep working until he's fixed the problem. He doesn't quit and doesn't let up until he's done it.

So what is God's anger aimed at? It's named here:
- First of all, against the mark of the beast. We know this from chapter 13 - it describes everyone who obeys Satan out of fear and even does Satan-like things themselves.
- Next, and the one after that: because his witnesses - that's you - have to suffer for living like Jesus.
- Then twice more against people's stubbornness (refusing to admit when they've done something wrong, refusing to change).
- Then, because they gather with Satan at Armageddon - meaning they're in Satan's army and enemies of Jesus.
- And finally, of course, against Satan's kingdom itself.

God invites us to be "angry" together with him. What does that mean?
- Encourage others not to be afraid of Satan, and inspire them through your example to act like Jesus.
- Don't take revenge - that's God's job. But we can, and should, set boundaries.
- Admit it ourselves when we've done something wrong, ask for forgiveness, and commit to living like Jesus.
- Always be on Jesus' side, even when it feels lonely.
- Always remember that God wins in the end.

Extra: Armageddon. God is actually making fun of Satan here, because the name has three meanings:
- A great battle
- Mountain of assembly, a phrase for a place where Satan acts like he's the greatest
- Mountain of Megiddo, a place where the weakest defeated the strongest

## For Bible Explorers

Do you know some of the Armageddon stories?
- Mountain of assembly (Isaiah 14:12-15)
- A housewife defeats an army commander at Megiddo (Judges 4:12-22)
- Elijah, a single prophet of God, defeats 850 (imagine trying to fit them all in one place!) on Mount Carmel, near Megiddo (1 Kings 17-18)
- Josiah, a king, goes into battle at Megiddo and dies - but by doing so saves Israel and changes world history (2 Chronicles 35:20-25)
