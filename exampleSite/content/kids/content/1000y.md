## The Story

So, that was a lot. Before we get to the end, let's sum it all up.

When Jesus came the first time, Satan really lost. He can't do anything now, and he's chained up. That's why the church can act and rule. But our kind of ruling is different from strong people who just crush everyone else. It sometimes looks like losing - but even in our greatest suffering, we're still treated like kings.

And even when Satan gathers every last bit of strength he has, God will make him fizzle out like a firework on New Year's Eve. And everyone who was on his side - whether they didn't dare to leave, or actually enjoyed being mean to others - will spend eternity without God.

## Overview

This is a story that a lot of grown-ups disagree about, but it's actually a fairly simple one:
- It starts with the amazing story of Satan being thrown out of heaven (Ch. 12) - and here, not just out of heaven, but into the abyss. He's held there for a long time and can't do anything.
- Then we're told why: because the church - that's us - rules the world and lives out the gospel for everyone to see, and nobody, especially not Satan, can stop us. Because we're not afraid, even when we're threatened. No, we don't literally get "beheaded" - that's a picture, showing that even in death, even in our greatest weakness, we're still treated like kings.
- Then Satan gathers all his strength, attacks, and is completely destroyed. We already saw that last time - that's the moment Jesus returns.
- And then everyone faces the decision: spend eternity with God, or without him. And what matters there is whether someone has received Jesus into their heart, and let that change their life - or at least tried not to do anything terrible.
