## Overview

The letters describe seven churches that are all very different, and how Jesus sees them. Will they live up to the vision? Which ones do you think are closest to Jesus' heart?

Which two churches does Jesus think are truly good, and which is truly bad?

First there's Ephesus, a big city with a church that's very active, does a lot of good work, and is good at spotting and stopping false teaching. But they're a little loveless. Other than that, there's nothing wrong with them at all.

Then there's Smyrna, a very small church that's faithful to Jesus, but has trouble with some of the Jews there, and many of them end up in prison.

Next comes Pergamon. They have a really difficult job, since they're almost overwhelmed by evil people - one of them even died because of it. But they allow people to teach false things about Jesus inside the church.

Then there's Thyatira, a very small town Jesus has a lot to say about. They keep getting better in their faith and have endured a great deal - but here the church's own leaders are the ones spreading false teaching.

Then we have Sardis, a very grand city. They seem to have their faith all under control - which is the problem, because it turns out they don't actually need Jesus in their lives, except for a few of them.

Next we see Philadelphia. The people there try hard, face a lot of opposition, and are genuinely poor. But they stay faithful to Jesus, even when it's hard.

And finally there's Laodicea. Lots of people would love to belong to this church, because the people there are rich and healthy. And they probably thought they were better than everyone else.

So who got it right? Smyrna and Philadelphia are truly great, because even though they suffer, they stay faithful to Jesus.

But being loveless (Ephesus) or arrogant (Laodicea) is never okay.

Living life without really needing God (Sardis) is just as bad as spreading wrong ideas about Jesus and leading others into wrongdoing (Pergamon and Thyatira).
