## Overview

That was pretty intense. Good thing God protects us and marks us with a seal as belonging to him, before all of that happens.

And Jesus has an army too. You might think that has to be a huge fighting force, meant to battle all the evil people and destroy them. But God shows us once again that it's completely different.

God's army is everyone who loves God and wants to be like him. Sometimes they're very sad, because others hurt them. But instead of losing heart, they keep seeking God's presence, where they find comfort.

- What does God's army look like?
- What weapons do we have?
- What kind of people are in it, and how can we defend ourselves?

Those are important questions, and here's where they get answered.

Most important first: the sealing.

Before anything starts, God makes one thing clear: he's watching out for us. God seals his army. What does that actually mean? It isn't described in detail, but it's a mark showing that we belong to God's army. That becomes important later, at the fifth trumpet. When everyone else is sad and discouraged, we have courage and hope. Nobody can take that away from us.

Then God's army gets counted. That matters, to make sure nobody gets lost. 144,000 is a huge number, almost impossible to picture. And that's just what John hears.

In reality, it's a crowd too large to count. And it looks nothing like what you'd picture as an army:
- They are worshipers of God. They don't want to hurt anyone - they want to do good to people and invite them to Jesus.
- They come out of the great tribulation. That's the whole time since Jesus first came, during which God's people have to suffer, even when they've done nothing wrong. People sometimes hate us simply because we believe in Jesus.
- Good thing we don't hold grudges - instead, we show that love always wins.
