## The Story

Satan is tired of losing all the time. So he calls for backup: first, a beast comes up out of the sea.

The beast from the sea looks incredibly strong and terrifying. And then something happens: one of its heads is struck down - but then the head grows back. And everyone is thrilled and thinks it's amazing. That sounds a little like Jesus, since he also died and rose again.

But this is completely different. The beast was never really dead, just wounded. And when Jesus rose again, his disciples were overjoyed - but here, everyone is actually afraid, because the beast is so powerful. Then the beast goes around insulting God and attacking everyone. Only God's children see through it, and their names are written in the book of life. Sadly, that means they have to endure a lot.

But that's not enough for Satan. He brings in a second beast, one that looks like Jesus but sounds like Satan. It performs great miracles and can even bring dead things to life - but really, it's trying to manipulate and control everyone. Anyone who won't go along with it gets killed or loses everything. And everyone has to wear a mark showing they're loyal to the beast, so everyone can see it.

That's genuinely hard to bear. But if you're paying attention, you might already see through the whole thing. Satan, as the dragon and his two beasts, has failed again - three sixes, three fails.

## Overview

Last time we saw that Satan is a constant loser. Now we see his last great attack: with the help of two more beasts, he builds his own fake "trinity":
- the dragon, pretending to be all-powerful like God the Father
- the first beast, dying and rising again like Jesus, and defeating all its enemies
- the second beast, working miracles and bringing the dead to life, like the Holy Spirit

Is it even possible to see through Satan's deception? Verse 11 gives us a hint: the beast looks like God - but it sounds like Satan. So anyone who recognizes God's voice sees through the trick. And who can do that? Everyone who believes in Jesus (John 10:4 - "my sheep recognize my voice").

So where's the real difference? A lot of people think that with Jesus, everything is always big and strong and good. But that's not actually the difference. Satan uses fear, while Jesus uses love:
- Jesus died and rose again because he loves us - but after its "resurrection," the first beast makes everyone afraid of it instead.
- The Holy Spirit works miracles to heal people, but the second beast works miracles to hurt people, kill them, or make them poor.

Satan can never be weak or admit a mistake:
- The first beast never really dies - it's only wounded - but Jesus really died, and was completely at the mercy of others.
- The Holy Spirit can be turned away, but the second beast always tries to control everything.

Also: God rules forever, but Satan is only given a limited time (42 months).

So who are these beasts, really? The second beast is all the people around us that we deal with every day - they want to please the first beast and do whatever it wants. And the first beast? That's all the powerful people we don't have much to do with directly, but who seem to control everything (a ringleader, maybe?). But even that's not quite true, because the first beast is only doing what the dragon tells it to do.

One more thing: this chapter mentions the number 666, and that number actually shows up three times in the Bible:
- Here: three times Satan's deception scores a 6 (a fail). Nobody who knows better falls for it.
- With Daniel's friends in the fiery furnace (Daniel 3): look at the statue's measurements sometime. The friends know God can save them - but they'd stay faithful even if he didn't (verse 18). And even though God doesn't stop them from being thrown into the fire, Jesus is right there with them in the flames (verse 25) and keeps them safe.
- With Solomon (1 Kings 10:14): the wise king let himself be fooled by Satan, because he loved God's gifts - wisdom, wealth, power - more than he loved God.

That's both an encouragement and a warning for us:
- Don't be fooled - anything that isn't from Jesus will bring you down, no matter how tempting it looks.
- Stay faithful to Jesus, even when things don't look good right now.
- Love God, the giver, more than you love his gifts.

## Reflection

- What behavior looks like Jesus, and what looks like Satan?
- What does it do to people when someone tries to scare them?
- Would you rather love others, even if some people think that makes you weak?
- Who do you think is really strong?
- Can you admit when you've made a mistake?
- How does it feel when someone apologizes to you?
