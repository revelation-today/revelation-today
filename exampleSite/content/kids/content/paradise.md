## Overview

What will heaven actually be like? Today we're going to find out more. A lot of people think heaven is mostly about seeing good friends again, and everything always going well, with no more hard times.

But is that really what it's about? Let's look at this together.

Heaven is described here in a lot of detail - but it's a little confusing. Heaven, also called the New Jerusalem, is quite strange.

First of all, it's a city (Jerusalem was a city too) with very unusual measurements. But it's also a garden, and finally, also a bride. It's described a lot like a temple, but it isn't actually a temple. And all around the city is hell.

What does all of that mean? It really is more than a little confusing.

Let's start with the temple. The temple is the place where God lives and spends time with us. That's a good thing - but the problem was that in the temple, you could be very close to God (in the Most Holy Place), but also very far away. God, though, wants to have close fellowship with all of us.

That's why the city has a special shape: its height, width, and depth are all exactly equal - just like the Most Holy Place in the temple, the place where you were closest to God.

That sounds great at first - but it also means heaven is for people who really want to be close to God. It's not like the harlot, who only cared about what she could get from God - the people in heaven simply want to be with him.

Heaven is also a garden, because God wants a direct, honest relationship, like in the Garden of Eden. And heaven is a city, because that's what people built for themselves as security once they had to leave the Garden of Eden - and now God wants to live right there with people.

And of course heaven is also like a bride, because for God, it's all about the relationship. But everyone who doesn't want a relationship with God at all won't live with him - and that's called hell.

We're also told about Israel, the Jewish people, who will have their own part in this too. We already know that Christians and Jews had a very hard time with each other back then. In heaven, we'll be together with other people we may not have found it easy to get along with here on earth. But we'll be together with them, because we all love God.

One more thing: heaven isn't up in the clouds somewhere - the New Jerusalem comes *down* to earth.
