## Overview

Now Jesus and his army are here, and the question is: what do they do now? Force all the bad people to become good? Or maybe kill them, or lock them away forever so they can't do anything bad anymore?

The story goes differently than you might think. God's army prays. That's their great weapon. And because their prayer reaches God, the trumpets are blown. So what happens then?

A whole lot of disasters happen. And they all have one thing in common: they take away people's sense of security.
- There's little to eat on the earth
- There's little to drink on the earth
- There's nothing to eat in the seas either
- There's no way to find direction by looking at the sky

But that's not all. The next two plagues even threaten people's lives, and this is where Satan's powers get involved. They torment people so badly that they'd rather not live anymore.

But we're protected from this, because we're sealed. God wants everyone, though, to trust him and receive his seal.

But even as Satan's powers kill people by the thousands, people still don't want to trust God - they keep trusting Satan instead. Because they don't want to change their lives; they want to stay exactly as they are.

And with that, the first mystery ends - the mystery of the church. It looks as if the church hasn't succeeded. But that's not quite right. Something essential isn't missing - we'll find out what, in the next mystery: the mystery of God.

## For Bible Explorers

If you know your Bible well, you may have already noticed a lot of similarities with the plagues in Egypt. But what were those actually about?

God showed everyone that he's the only one you can really trust, because he has everything under control. And he doesn't want to hurt people through the plagues - he even warns them ahead of time.

He wants people to trust him. But he also gives people like Pharaoh a strong heart, so that they're able to decide against him too, if that's what they want.
