# About This Module

This is not a standalone series. It is a short, four-session module built to be inserted into
whichever regional or confessional pack a congregation is already using — most naturally into
the harlot-Babylon and beast sessions of the Latin American and African packs, both of which
already point here explicitly, but it fits just as well anywhere health-and-wealth teaching has
real traction in the room. Health-and-wealth (Word of Faith / prosperity gospel) teaching isn't
confined to one region or tradition; it cuts across all of them. So rather than build a
thirteenth regional pack, this module gives any pack a deeper engagement exactly where
Revelation's own content already collides with this teaching most directly: the harlot Babylon
and the counterfeit-Christ imagery of the beasts and the first horseman.

Use it where the underlying pack already has a harlot-Babylon or beast session scheduled — go
deeper there instead of teaching both, then return to the pack's normal flow. Presented on its
own, disconnected from the natural movement through Revelation, this risks feeling like an
isolated attack on a named movement. Woven into the larger reading of the book, it reads instead
as what it actually is: Revelation confronting a temptation the text has named since chapter 6.

**The four sessions:**

1. **The First Horseman's Fake Gospel** — the counterfeit-Christ figure who offers conquest,
   security, and provision while asking nothing of the cross.
2. **666: Whose Security Are You Actually Buying?** — the devil's imitation trinity, graded and
   found wanting, and Solomon's own warning about mistaking the gift for the Giver.
3. **The Harlot as Glamorized Empire** — the load-bearing session of the module: wealth and
   glamor built on violence and idolatry, dressed up to look like blessing.
4. **What Real Security Looks Like** — the New Jerusalem's permanent, undeserved security,
   offered as the real "prosperity" this book has been pointing to all along.

**Core thesis, stated plainly and respectfully:** many who hold to health-and-wealth teaching
do so for understandable reasons — real economic hardship, a genuine desire to trust God for
provision, a justified refusal to accept poverty as some kind of spiritual virtue. This module
does not question that sincerity, and it is not aimed at any named ministry or teacher. It
simply asks Revelation's own question, the same one it asked the first churches facing Rome's
version of a golden age: who do you actually credit for your security, and what is it asking of
you in return? The book's answer is not that wealth is evil or that God is indifferent to
suffering. Its answer is that real, permanent security was never a formula to be purchased — it
is a Person to be trusted, and it is coming, in full, whether or not the ledger ever balances in
this life. Teach all four sessions in that spirit: an invitation to something more durable than
either poverty or prosperity, not a takedown of people trying, however imperfectly, to trust God
with their lives.
