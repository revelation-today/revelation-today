{{% bible val="Philadelphia" link="rev:3,7-13" lang="en" %}} is the city holding the key of David. What doors does that key open — evangelism, opportunity, relationships, or something else entirely? Read on, and you'll see why this is one church Jesus never chastises.

## The historical background

<a name="cca2"></a>
Philadelphia was the youngest of the seven cities, located 50 km south of Sardis and probably founded in the second century BC. Its name comes from Attalus II, who succeeded his brother Eumenes as king. [He was so loyal to his brother that he earned the nickname Philadelphus, "brother-loving"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attalus_II_Philadelphus).

Sitting at the crossroads of several trade routes, at the mouth of a river basin and the foot of a large volcanic plain, the city's soil was ideally suited to vineyards. It also sat on a geological fault line and was constantly plagued by tremors. After a massive earthquake destroyed it in AD 60, Emperor Tiberius funded its rebuilding and granted a five-year tax break while reconstruction was underway.

Even so, in AD 92 another emperor issued a decree cutting back wine cultivation, ostensibly to free up land for growing grain for the army. The people of Philadelphia, who had always been fiercely loyal to Rome, felt betrayed.

## Jesus' view

<a name="294f"></a>
Jesus offers this church no rebuke at all. To him, Philadelphia holds David's key, able to open what no one can close and close what no one can open — a {{% bible val="reference to Shebna and Eliakim" link="isa:22,15-25" lang="en" %}}, in which {{% bible val="Shebna abused his position as steward of the royal treasury, and the position was given instead to Eliakim, so that he could provide for those in need" link="isa:22,21-22" lang="en" %}}.

The congregation in Philadelphia is in real financial trouble, yet they remain faithful to the God who supplies their needs. Their other challenge comes from Jewish opponents who oppress the church, just as they oppress the church in {{% bible val="Smyrna" link="rev:2,9" lang="en" %}}. Jesus promises the Philadelphians that these very opponents will one day bow down before them — meaning they will eventually join the church themselves, and reconciliation will follow.

The members of this church are genuine overcomers. What's their secret? Simply this: {{% bible val="they patiently endure." link="rev:3,10" lang="en" %}}

## The promise

<a name="9039"></a>
God promises to make them like a pillar in his Temple, meaning they will never again have to leave his presence, but will dwell there forever. The promise that "I will write on them the name of my God and the name of the city of my God" echoes {{% bible val="Ezekiel, where the city's name is finally 'the LORD is here'" link="ezk:48,35" lang="en" %}}.

This promise answers their patient endurance over a long stretch of hardship — because what they want most, in the end, is simply to be close to Jesus.

## The hour of trial

<a name="202d"></a>
The letter also contains a striking statement: "{{% bible val="Since you have kept my command to endure patiently, I will also keep you from the hour of trial that is going to come on the whole world to test the inhabitants of the earth" link="rev:3,10" lang="en" %}}."

What exactly is this "hour of trial," and how does Jesus keep believers from it? The Greek word translated "[keep from](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/revelation/3-10.htm)" appears in only one other place in the New Testament, [in the Gospel of John](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/john/17-15.htm), where Jesus prays for his disciples: "{{% bible val="My prayer is not that you take them out of the world but that you protect them from the evil one" link="jhn:17,15" lang="en" %}}." That parallel is telling — Jesus is not promising to remove believers from the world, but to protect them within it, in the same sense found in {{% bible val="Proverbs 7:5" link="pro:7,5" lang="en" %}} and the {{% bible val="Letter of James 1:27" link="jas:1,27" lang="en" %}}. In other words, this "hour of trial" describes the same reality as the {{% int_link val="Great Tribulation" link="/expl/content/army/the-end-time-and-the-great-tribulation" %}}.
