What is this mysterious number in Revelation all about? Who are the 144,000, and what is the larger context around them?

## The sealing

<a name="9366"></a>
After the sixth seal, there's an interlude that already connects forward to the trumpets — even before the seventh seal itself has happened.

An angel is called to seal the servants of God before the trumpets begin. {{% bible val="This reminds of the sealing of the righteous in the prophet Ezekiel, where the angel was to mark all the people who mourned over the unrighteousness that had taken place. After they were all sealed, another angel came and struck down those without a seal, starting at the temple of God." link="ezk:9" lang="en" %}}

This scene isn't only about protection — it's also a reminder not to compromise with what God sees as evil. That protection will only become active during the {{% int_link val="fifth trumpet" link="/expl/content/trumpets/the-trumpets-in-revelation" %}}, when people become so desperate that they want to die.

## The army of God

<a name="2cd4"></a>
Next, the 144,000 are introduced, described as 12,000 from each tribe of Israel. But there are actually 14 tribes to draw from, since the list counts not only the 12 sons of Jacob but also the two sons of Joseph, whom Jacob adopted — which is why Old Testament tribal lists vary from one another.

What's especially interesting is which tribe leads them: Judah, the tribe Jesus comes from. And once the church becomes part of Israel, this description of Israel doubles as a description of the church, since it is now a multitude that no one can count.

The way the counting is presented is also worth noticing — it echoes the counting of the men ready for military service during the Exodus. Here, the same people who are sealed are presented as an army.

But there's a twist here, just as in {{% int_link val="chapter 5" link="/expl/content/worship/the-book-with-the-seven-seals" %}}, where the {{% bible val="lion of Judah is identified as a lamb being slaughtered" link="rev:5,6" lang="en" %}}. The same pattern plays out again: {{% bible val="the 144,000 counted as soldiers is what John hears" link="rev:7,4" lang="en" %}}, but {{% bible val="what John sees is an uncountable number from all nations who worship God" link="rev:7,9" lang="en" %}}.

There's also a connection to the {{% bible val="fifth seal" link="rev:6,9-11" lang="en" %}}, where the souls under the altar cry out and receive white robes, just like these people here. They appear to be the same group: they come out of the {{% int_link val="Great Tribulation" link="/expl/content/army/the-end-time-and-the-great-tribulation" %}} and {{% bible val="are now comforted by God" link="rev:7,15-17" lang="en" %}}, living "already in the not yet." We'll look at this more closely in chapter 14, when the 144,000 appear again.
