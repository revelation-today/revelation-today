{{% bible val="The magic number of the beast" link="rev:13,18" lang="en" %}} has been used, wrongly in my opinion, to decode current politics through one numbering scheme or another. The real answer isn't found by hunting outside the text — it's already there in the Bible itself.

## Some basics

<a name="0630"></a>
First, we need some background before looking at this in more detail:

- **Gematria**: This is the most common way people try to decode the number 666. [Gematria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gematria) assigns each letter of the alphabet a numerical value, and if a name's letters add up to 666, that person is declared "the evil one." If a name doesn't fit, proponents switch to variants of the name, try a different language (Hebrew, Latin, Greek), or fall back on reading it as a triple six — which is how Ronald Wilson Reagan ended up accused of being the beast, since each of his three names has six letters. With enough patience, you [can map many names across history and the present to 666](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Number_of_the_beast), and that flexibility is exactly the problem: this method has been badly abused, because a lock that fits every key isn't worth much.
- **Numbers**: In the Bible, numbers are often tied to specific events. {{% bible val="Moses fasted for 40 days when he received the Law at Mount Sinai" link="exo:34,28" lang="en" %}}, {{% bible val="the people spent 40 days scouting out the new land" link="num:13,25" lang="en" %}}, and {{% bible val="because of their unbelief they had to wander in the desert for 40 years" link="num:14,34" lang="en" %}}. Is it a coincidence that {{% bible val="Jesus fasted for 40 days in the desert, facing the same challenges" link="mat:4,1-11" lang="en" %}} Israel faced across their 40 years of wandering? You could say the number 40 is tied to {{% int_link val="acts of faith" link="/expl/background/structure/the-use-of-numbers-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}}. In the same way, the number 7 points to completion and to God, {{% bible val="who completed the world in 7 days" link="gen:2,1-3" lang="en" %}}, while the number 6 points to man and to humanity's imperfect actions, since man was created on the sixth day. {{% int_link val="See more about numbers." link="/expl/background/structure/the-use-of-numbers-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}}
- **A letter**: We've already seen that {{% int_link val="we should always look out for clues that are relevant to the original readers" link="/expl/background/literature/the-book-of-revelation-how-to-read-it" %}}. So what would 666 have meant at the time of the early church?

## 6–6–6: The failure of the evil trinity

<a name="69b0"></a>
The first way of understanding it comes straight from the story in {{% bible val="Rev. 13" link="rev:13" lang="en" %}} itself, explained in more depth in {{% int_link val="another article" link="/expl/content/beasts/the-nature-of-the-beast-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}}: the beast's whole project is to imitate the Holy Trinity of God, Jesus, and the Holy Spirit.

For believers who know what to look for, though, the deception falls apart, because each element of the imitation earns itself a score that grades how well the counterfeit actually holds up:

- The dragon — does he really hold all the power the first beast has? No: that power is only ever *given*, and only for 42 months. Score: 6.
- The first beast — did he really lay down his life out of love, and did he really die? No: even his death was a fake, since only one head was struck down. Score: 6.
- The second beast — does he really invite and counsel people the way the Holy Spirit does? No: he operates purely through fear and intimidation. Score: 6.

Add it up, and you get 6-6-6 — the number of the beast: a grade of failure, three times over, on the very points where the imitation matters most. If you know the real God, the counterfeit can't fool you. Stay in the love, and you're safe.

## The 666 in context

<a name="2def"></a>
The number 666 also has some very interesting {{% int_link val="arithmetic properties" link="/background/structure/expl/the-use-of-numbers-in-the-book-of-revelation#6395" %}} and links elsewhere inside the book of Revelation.

- 666 connects to the number 8, which the Bible associates with resurrection: Jesus rose on the eighth day — the day after the Sabbath, so the first day of the new week, or better, the first day of the new creation. Resurrection is also prominent in the {{% bible val="description of the first beast" link="rev:13,3" lang="en" %}}, in {{% bible val="the sixth bowl " link="rev:16,13" lang="en" %}} (whose frogs represent the Egyptian goddess of birth and resurrection), and in the {{% bible val="description of the beast on which the harlot sits" link="rev:18,11" lang="en" %}}. There's a strong similarity, too, between [this passage's call for wisdom](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/revelation/13-18.htm) and the [call for wisdom about the beast the harlot sits on](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/revelation/17-9.htm), which is later {{% bible val="described as one of the seven heads (the kings) but also as an eighth king" link="rev:17,11" lang="en" %}}.
- The text calls this a {{% bible val="number of a man" link="rev:13,18" lang="en" %}} and places it directly before the {{% bible val="mention of the 144,000" link="rev:14,1" lang="en" %}}, which itself points forward to the New Jerusalem, [whose wall is measured at 12×12=144 cubits, by human measurement, which is also an angel's measurement](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/revelation/21-17.htm) — a detail the {{% bible val="NIV" link="rev:21,17" lang="en" %}} doesn't translate as clearly as the ESV does. Even though the exact meaning stays elusive, it underlines the same contrast again: the beast represents humanity debased, while the witnesses are exalted to the rank of angels.

Together, these point to two things:

- This mirrors the pattern at {{% int_link val="Armageddon" link="/expl/content/bowls/the-key-to-armageddon" %}}, where what looks impressive is exposed as utterly weak, while what looks weak turns out to be superior.
- The passage sets up the resolution of the mystery of 666 as the eighth king, taken up next.

## The eighth king

<a name="22e8"></a>
So who would this eighth king be? The story of Nero fits remarkably well. He was notorious for killing his own mother, yet he was also very popular with the common people of Rome. He alienated the Roman aristocracy but was genuinely popular, even adored, in the East — especially in Greece and among the Parthians. Even though the first Jewish war took place in Nero's time, the Jews didn't think badly of him, since the war itself was carried out by Vespasian and Titus. For Christians, though, Nero was the one who launched the first persecutions, and many came to see him as the Antichrist.

When Nero realized he was about to die, rumors spread that he was fleeing east to his allies there. That never happened — he was surrounded, the Senate declared him a public enemy, and he killed himself with a dagger instead. Because he was never given a proper funeral, a myth spread that he hadn't died at all. In the years that followed, a string of impostors claimed to be Nero, and several of them found major support from the Parthians.

This kept the myth of a returning Nero alive. Since he was now associated with the Parthians — a genuine threat to Rome — he increasingly came to be seen as judgment bearing down on Rome itself.

His unclear end, whether he was really dead or not, makes the {{% bible val="first beast's appearance a perfect resemblance of him" link="rev:13,3" lang="en" %}}. The {{% bible val="sixth bowl" link="rev:16,12" lang="en" %}} may point the same direction, since it could well be describing an attack by the Parthians.

## Emperor Trajan

<a name="576c"></a>
Since gematria came up at the beginning of this section, it's worth pointing to one figure who fits into that category for two reasons:

- he is relevant to the first readers, because he lived in their own time, and
- he {{% int_link val="reflects the dual attack of the beast" link="/expl/content/beasts/the-nature-of-the-beast-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}}.

The person who fits the description is the Emperor [Trajan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan), who:

- lived at the time (emperor from 98–117), making him a fitting illustration,
- supported the persecution of Christians when they refused to comply, and yet
- was considered one of the [five good emperors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nerva%E2%80%93Antonine_dynasty#Five_Good_Emperors): under his reign the Roman Empire reached its greatest extent, he established a substantial [welfare program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alimenta), carried out major [building projects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan#Building_projects), and invested heavily in [public games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan#Games) — all of it embodying the [Pax Romana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pax_Romana).

## Daniel's friends in the furnace

<a name="9bcc"></a>
The second reference is the story of {{% bible val="Daniel’s friends in the fiery furnace" link="dan:3" lang="en" %}}, which has many parallels to the second beast. Here too there's a statue that people are required to worship, and whoever is caught disobeying is killed by fire. What's particularly interesting are the {{% bible val="dimensions of the statue" link="dan:3,1" lang="en" %}}: 60 feet high by 6 feet by 6 feet at the base.

Even more interesting is how the friends respond when confronted for {{% bible val="not bowing to the statue" link="dan:3,16-18" lang="en" %}}: "We trust that God can save us — but even if not, we will be faithful." This is where 666 touches our own response to oppression: those who don't comply are killed, or at least cannot buy or sell, facing real financial struggle. The call here is to stay faithful even if God doesn't visibly intervene.

But what does this have to do with the beast? Look at how the story in Daniel ends. Because the friends stayed faithful, they were preserved — "someone like the Son of God" stood with them in the fire — and the story closes with a happy ending. In trouble, we can be just as sure that Jesus is with us, and the next chapter of Revelation turns the tables the same way: the devil's followers end up in serious trouble, while the believers come through in good shape.

## Solomon's riches

<a name="719b"></a>
The third reference to 666 appears written out exactly like that in the Bible, in {{% bible val="the summary of the life of King Solomon" link="1ki:10,14-29" lang="en" %}}, right at its opening — the last high point before {{% bible val="the description of his catastrophic failure" link="1ki:11,1-13" lang="en" %}}.

What is the story of Solomon? It begins with the {{% bible val="story of Bathsheba" link="2sa:11" lang="en" %}}, which led to {{% bible val="Nathan rebuking him, and to the loss of the child born from that sin" link="2sa:12,1-23" lang="en" %}} — yet {{% bible val="God gave him another child with Bathsheba, a sign of God's own love" link="2sa:12,24-25" lang="en" %}}. After he was enthroned, {{% bible val="he asked God for an understanding heart instead of wealth and power — and because he asked for that, God gave him all the power and wealth" link="1ki:3" lang="en" %}} on top.

As a result, he came to see God as the source of his blessing, but the blessing itself gradually became more important to him than his relationship with God, and in the end, this {{% bible val="was his downfall" link="1ki:11,1-13" lang="en" %}}. And doesn't it seem strange that {{% bible val="he received exactly 666 shekels of silver every year" link="1ki:10,14" lang="en" %}}, when every other number in that chapter is rounded?

## Conclusion

<a name="89a3"></a>
So what is the takeaway, both for the first listeners and for us?

- 666 shows that the great, overpowering, controlling beast is ultimately a failure, so believers need not be afraid.
- We need to stay strong and not be overcome by fear when things get hard — persecution, the fear of death, economic exclusion — even when God doesn't visibly step in to intervene; that's the lesson of Daniel's friends.
- We shouldn't be tempted by an attractive compromise, telling ourselves we're still following God while really trying to have the best of both God and the world, the way Solomon's compromise eventually failed him.
- Trajan illustrates the same danger in his own time: he seemed to bring wealth and security, yet had the blood of Christians on his hands.

So today, it's worth examining where we see the same {{% int_link val="principles of the 666" link="/expl/content/beasts/the-nature-of-the-beast-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}} at work around us, and judging whether they:

- follow God's principles and will therefore remain, or
- follow the principles of the devil, which may look tempting now but will end in disaster and pull us away from God.

If you think 666 refers to some computer chip waiting to be implanted, ask yourself this instead: what information could such a chip provide that isn't already available through

- Your Google search
- Browsing history
- Smartphone usage (including location tracking)
- Credit or debit card usage
- Tons of social media records?
