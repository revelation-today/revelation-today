The book of Revelation is filled with descriptions of judgment, and it can look at first glance like the whole point is to scare unbelievers into repenting. That impression, however, turns out to be wrong on almost every count.

## Some common misunderstandings about judgment

<a name="1ae8"></a>
The table below sets four widespread assumptions about judgment in Revelation against what the text actually supports.

| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| The book is addressed to unbelievers, to bring them to repentance. | No — {{% int_link val="it is addressed to the church(es), so that believers become more committed" link="/expl/background/literature/the-book-of-revelation-how-to-read-it" %}}. |
| It is meant to create fear and scare people. | No. Fear and manipulation are not attributes of the church; they are shown throughout the book to be {{% int_link val="attributes of the beast" link="/expl/content/beasts/the-nature-of-the-beast-in-the-book-of-revelation" %}} instead. The church and this book operate instead through {{% int_link val="worship" link="/appl/topics/power/worship" %}}, {{% int_link val="testimony" link="/appl/topics/power/the-power-of-testimony" %}}, perseverance, and {{% int_link val="prayer" link="/appl/topics/power/the-key-of-prayer" %}}. Once again, the book is directed at the church, not at outsiders. |
| The only purpose of judgment is to punish. | No. Judgment scenes are {{% int_link val="used to expose the devil" link="/expl/content/seals/the-mystery-of-the-four-horse-men" %}}, to {{% int_link val="shake false securities" link="/expl/content/trumpets/the-trumpets-in-revelation" %}}, and to {{% int_link val="challenge people to make a clear decision" link="/expl/content/bowls/the-bowls-of-wrath" %}}. Punishment is certainly part of the picture, but not in the way most people assume — see the {{% int_link val="topic of heaven and hell" link="/expl/content/paradise/heaven-and-hell" %}}. In many cases, too, the cause of suffering is not God at all, but demons or the devil. Even in the Old Testament, blessings and curses are laid out as an educational device: “{{% bible val="If you still do not listen" link="lev:26" lang="en" %}}.” |
| Judgment is the main theme of Revelation. | It isn't. The main theme is worship, coupled with testimony and witness. |
