The church isn't God's backup plan, running alongside Israel or replacing it outright — it's grafted into the same family, the same covenant, the same story that runs all the way back to Abraham. That changes how the Old Testament is meant to be read: not as someone else's history with a few pre-Christian lessons in it, but as your own family's story.

It also changes how easily "us and them" thinking can slip into how you think about Israel, the church, or any other group of God's people that looks different from your own corner of it.

- Do you actually read the Old Testament as your own family's history, or as background material for someone else's story?
- Where has "us and them" thinking crept into how you view believers who don't look, worship, or belong the way you do?
